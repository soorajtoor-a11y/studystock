HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — MEDICAL TERMINOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. The test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references below are OFFICIAL (verbatim from the HOSA guideline). Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and to the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (20 tiebreaker questions; correct spelling required).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Medical language — roots, prefixes, suffixes, and combining forms, plus terminology by body system.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Ehrlich. Medical Terminology for Health Professions. Cengage, latest ed.
- Taber's Cyclopedic Medical Dictionary. F.A. Davis, latest ed.
- Stanhope & Turnbull. Introduction to Medical Terminology. Goodheart-Willcox, latest ed.
- Dean Vaughn's Medical Terminology by Body Systems (online).

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Roots, Prefixes, Suffixes & Combining Forms (23 items — 45%)
1. Identify common prefixes, suffixes, and word roots and their meanings.
2. Build and deconstruct medical terms; form plurals.
3. Interpret common abbreviations and symbols.

B. Overview of the Body (3 items — 5%)
1. Use directional terms, planes, cavities, and quadrants.

C. Skeletal (3 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for bones, joints, and skeletal conditions/procedures.

D. Muscular (3 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for muscles, movement, and related conditions.

E. Respiratory (3 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for respiratory structures, conditions, and procedures.

F. Digestive (3 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for digestive structures, conditions, and procedures.

G. Cardiovascular & Lymphatic (2 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for heart, vessels, blood, and lymph.

H. Nervous & Special Senses (2 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for the nervous system, eye, and ear.

I. Endocrine (2 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for endocrine glands and hormones.

J. Reproductive (2 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for reproductive and obstetric terms.

K. Integumentary (2 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for skin, hair, and nails.

L. Urinary (2 items — 5%)
1. Identify roots/terms for the urinary system, conditions, and procedures.
