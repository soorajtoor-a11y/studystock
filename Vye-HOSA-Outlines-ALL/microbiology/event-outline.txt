HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — MICROBIOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA NGL/Cengage Academic Testing Center Guidelines 2025-2026. Test plan (content-area %) and reference are OFFICIAL. Item counts = official % applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official area.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (1 tiebreaker). Type: ATC (Academic Testing Center) textbook test.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Microorganisms, infection, immunity, and infection control.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Microbiology: Practical Applications and Infection Prevention, 1st ed. (ISBN 9781133693642). Cengage/NGL.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Sanitization, Sterilization & Disinfection (4 items — 8%)
1. Differentiate sanitization, disinfection, and sterilization.

B. Infectious Diseases & Disorders (13 items — 26%)
1. Identify common infectious diseases and their causative agents.
2. Describe signs, transmission, and treatment of infections.

C. Infection Control, Chain of Infection & Transmission (7 items — 14%)
1. Explain the chain of infection and standard precautions.

D. Healthcare-Associated Infections (3 items — 6%)
1. Identify common healthcare-associated infections and prevention.

E. History of Microbiology (2 items — 4%)
1. Describe key figures and milestones in microbiology.

F. Immunity, Immunizations & Antimicrobials (5 items — 10%)
1. Differentiate types of immunity; explain vaccines and antimicrobials.

G. Tests to Determine Infection (2 items — 4%)
1. Identify tests used to detect infection.

H. Asexual Reproduction (1 item — 2%)
1. Explain microbial asexual reproduction (binary fission).

I. Characteristics of Mold (2 items — 4%)
1. Identify characteristics of molds and fungi.

J. Classifications of Organisms (3 items — 6%)
1. Describe classification of microorganisms.

K. Prokaryotic Cells (incl. Bacteria) (4 items — 8%)
1. Identify characteristics of prokaryotic cells and bacterial structure.

L. Stains Used in Microbiology (3 items — 6%)
1. Describe Gram and other staining techniques.

M. pH Scale & Effect on Microorganisms (1 item — 2%)
1. Explain how pH affects microbial growth.
