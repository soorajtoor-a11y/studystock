DECA STUDY OUTLINE — PERSONAL FINANCIAL LITERACY EXAM (2025–2026)
Source: DECA Exam Blueprints 2025 + MBA Research DECA Exam Specifications (ICDC/national blueprint) for instructional areas and item counts; performance indicators from the official DECA Career Cluster Performance Indicators (MBA Research).
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions.
Used for: Personal Financial Literacy.
Item counts per instructional area (ICDC level) shown in (parentheses). Objectives are authentic MBA Research performance indicators for each area (a representative subset — expand from the official PI list as needed).

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Covers personal money management across six domains from the National Standards for Personal Financial Education: earning income, spending, saving, investing, managing credit, and managing risk.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Earning Income (16 items)
1. Research potential income and employee benefit packages likely to be offered
2. Explain why people should evaluate employee benefits in addition to wages and salaries
3. Differentiate between contributory and non-contributory employee benefits
4. Examine the benefits of participating in employer-sponsored retirement savings plans
5. Give examples of intangible job benefits
6. Evaluate the tradeoffs between income and non-income factors when making career choices
7. Evaluate the costs and benefits of investing in additional education or training
8. Compare earnings and unemployment rates by level of education and training
9. Explain why wages or salaries vary among employees in different types of jobs
10. Describe how intangible benefits can affect a worker's career choices and income

B. Spending (14 items)
1. Identify short-term and long-term financial goals
2. Develop a budget to allocate current income to necessary and desired spending
3. Explain methods for adjusting a budget for unexpected expenses or emergencies
4. Evaluate the advantages of using budgeting tools, such as spreadsheets or apps
5. Describe a process for making an informed consumer decision
6. Explain the factors to evaluate when buying a durable good
7. Analyze the cost and features of three competing products or services
8. Explain how pre-purchase research encourages consumers to avoid impulse buying
9. Assess how spending priorities reflect goals and values
10. Discuss the components of a personal spending plan, including income, planned saving, and expenses

C. Saving (13 items)
1. Compare the features of regular savings accounts, money market accounts, and CDs
2. Explain why CDs typically pay higher interest rates than regular savings accounts
3. Select a preferred location for a savings account based on comparison of interest rates
4. Illustrate how inflation can reduce the purchasing power of savings over time
5. Investigate how federal I bonds provide inflation protection for savers
6. Compare the tax advantages of traditional and Roth IRAs
7. Explain how an employer match of employee contributions provides an incentive to save
8. Identify strategies to manage psychological and emotional obstacles to saving
9. Compare the advantages and disadvantages of saving for financial goals

D. Investing (21 items)
1. Explain why some people prefer to buy investments that grow in value over time
2. Compare rates of return on different types of investments and order them by risk
3. Estimate the future value of a lump sum invested today for a specified period
4. Demonstrate the difference in wealth accumulation for a person who begins to invest early versus later
5. Explain why bonds with longer maturities generally earn a higher return
6. Discuss how economic downturns that result in high unemployment can affect asset prices
7. Compare the expense ratios for several mutual funds
8. Describe the advantages of investing through a tax-deferred account such as an IRA
9. Identify several behavioral biases that can result in poor investment decisions

E. Managing Credit (21 items)
1. Describe how credit card grace periods, methods of interest calculation, and fees affect borrowing costs
2. Give examples of unsecured and secured loans
3. Explain why lenders charge lower interest rates on secured loans than on unsecured loans
4. Differentiate between adjustable-rate and fixed-rate mortgages
5. Compare federal and private student loans based on interest rates, repayment rules, and other characteristics
6. Identify the primary organizations that maintain and provide consumer credit reports
7. Explain how a borrower's credit score can impact their cost of credit and ability to get credit
8. Describe how failing to repay a loan can negatively impact a person's finances and life
9. Describe the different sources of funding for post-secondary education

F. Managing Risk (15 items)
1. Analyze the conditions under which it is appropriate for young adults to have life, health, and disability insurance
2. Identify individual characteristics that influence insurance purchase decisions
3. Recommend types of insurance needed by people with different characteristics
4. Explain why homeowners' insurance is required by a lender when a homeowner takes out a mortgage
5. Discuss why most states mandate auto liability coverage
6. Explain why taking a safe-driving course can lower a driver's auto insurance premium
7. Discuss the advantages of obtaining health insurance through an employer plan versus buying private insurance
8. Describe conditions under which individuals should and should not disclose sensitive information
9. Recommend strategies to reduce the risk of identity theft and financial fraud
