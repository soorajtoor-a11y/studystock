HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — ANATOMY & PHYSIOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. The test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references below are OFFICIAL (verbatim from the HOSA guideline). Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and to the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (ATC online test; tiebreaker per guideline).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Structure and function of the human body and common diseases/disorders. NOTE: HOSA offers Anatomy & Physiology only as an Academic Testing Center (ATC) test with a broad 3-category plan.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Body Structures and Functions, 14th ed. (ISBN 9780357457542). National Geographic Learning / Cengage.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Physiology of the human body (20 items — 40%)
1. Explain the function of each body system.
2. Describe homeostasis and system interdependence.
3. Explain physiological processes (e.g., gas exchange, filtration, muscle contraction).

B. Anatomy of the human body (20 items — 40%)
1. Identify major structures/organs of each body system.
2. Use directional terms, planes, cavities, and regions.
3. Describe levels of organization (cell, tissue, organ, system).

C. Common diseases and disorders (10 items — 20%)
1. Identify common diseases/disorders by body system.
2. Relate a disorder to the affected structure or function.
