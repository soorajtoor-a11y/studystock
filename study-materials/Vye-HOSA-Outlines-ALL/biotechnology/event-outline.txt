HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — BIOTECHNOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. Test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references are OFFICIAL. Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (Round 1 written test; highest test score breaks ties). NOTE: Round 2 is a skill component scored by rating sheet.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Biotechnology laboratory skills, molecular biology, and applications.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Brown. Biotechnology: A Laboratory Skills Course. Bio-Rad, latest ed.
- Starr & Taggart. Biology: The Unity and Diversity of Life (AP). NGL/Cengage, latest ed.
- Bio-Rad Biotechnology resources (supplemental).

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Biotechnology Industry Practices & Careers (2 items — 4%)
1. Describe biotechnology careers and industry practices.

B. Biotechnology in Health (2 items — 4%)
1. Explain applications of biotechnology in healthcare.

C. Governmental Regulation of Biotechnology (2 items — 4%)
1. Describe regulation of biotechnology (FDA, EPA) and bioethics.

D. Basic Laboratory Skills (7 items — 14%)
1. Demonstrate lab safety, measurement, and aseptic technique.
2. Use micropipettes and prepare solutions.

E. Microbiology & Cell Culture (6 items — 12%)
1. Explain microbial growth and sterile cell-culture technique.

F. DNA Structure & Analysis (7 items — 14%)
1. Explain DNA structure, replication, and gel electrophoresis.
2. Describe DNA extraction and restriction analysis.

G. Bacterial Transformation (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain plasmids and the bacterial transformation process.

H. Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) (7 items — 14%)
1. Explain the steps and applications of PCR.

I. Protein Structure, Function & Analysis (7 items — 14%)
1. Explain protein structure and analysis techniques (SDS-PAGE).

J. Immunological Applications (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain antibodies and immunoassays (ELISA).
