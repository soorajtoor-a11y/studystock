HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — FORENSIC SCIENCE (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. Test plan (content-area %) and reference are OFFICIAL. Item counts = official % applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official area.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (1 tiebreaker). Type: HOSA Teamwork event — Round 1 written test.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Forensic investigation methods across disciplines. HOSA Teamwork event (2 competitors): Round 1 written test (below) + Round 2 hands-on case study.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Bertino. Forensic Science: Fundamentals & Investigations. Cengage, latest ed.
- Bell. Forensic Science. Latest ed.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Forensic History & Careers (5 items — 10%)
1. Describe the history of forensic science and its careers.

B. Crime Scene & Death Investigation (10 items — 20%)
1. Explain crime-scene processing and death investigation.
2. Describe evidence collection and chain of custody.

C. Forensic Toxicology & Pharmacology (10 items — 20%)
1. Explain toxicology testing and drug effects.

D. Forensic Anthropology (5 items — 10%)
1. Describe skeletal analysis in forensic identification.

E. Forensic Entomology (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain how insects estimate post-mortem interval.

F. Blood/Biological Fluid Identification (5 items — 10%)
1. Describe identification and analysis of biological fluids.

G. DNA Analysis Techniques (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain forensic DNA analysis techniques.

H. Forensic Psychology & Psychiatry (5 items — 10%)
1. Describe the role of psychology/psychiatry in forensics.
