HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — HUMAN HEREDITY (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. Test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references are OFFICIAL. Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (1 tiebreaker). ATC (Academic Testing Center) textbook test. NOTE: percentages are from the most recent readable ATC guideline (2023-24); ATC plans are stable year-to-year but verify against the current ATC PDF.

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Human genetics, inheritance, and genetic technologies.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Cummings. Human Heredity: Principles and Issues. NGL/Cengage (11th ed., ISBN 9781305251052).

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Genetic Diseases & Disorders (incl. cancer) (12 items — 24%)
1. Identify common genetic diseases and disorders, including cancer.

B. Genetics, Genes, Cells & Cell Division (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain genes, cells, mitosis, and meiosis.

C. Chromosomes, Inheritance & Transmission of Traits (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain chromosomes and Mendelian inheritance.

D. Pedigrees, Karyotypes & Complex Traits (7 items — 14%)
1. Interpret pedigrees and karyotypes; explain complex traits.

E. Immune System & Viruses Affecting Genes (1 item — 2%)
1. Explain how the immune system and viruses affect genes.

F. DNA & RNA in Genetics (4 items — 8%)
1. Describe DNA/RNA structure and their role in heredity.

G. Phenotypes, Gene Expression & Regulation (4 items — 8%)
1. Explain gene expression and regulation.

H. Mutations & Epigenetics (4 items — 8%)
1. Describe mutations and epigenetic effects on genes.

I. Genetic Technologies & Testing (6 items — 12%)
1. Describe genetic testing, gene therapy, and biotechnology.

J. Genetics of Behavior (1 item — 2%)
1. Describe the genetics of behavior.

K. Population Genetics (1 item — 2%)
1. Describe population genetics.
