HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PATHOPHYSIOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. The test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references below are OFFICIAL (verbatim from the HOSA guideline). Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and to the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (essay tiebreaker).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Disease mechanisms and the body's response to injury and illness, by body system.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- iCEV Pathophysiology and Anatomy & Physiology courses
- Frazier. Essentials of Human Diseases and Conditions. Elsevier, latest ed.
- Zelman et al. Human Diseases: A Systemic Approach. Pearson, latest ed.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Disease Process & Terminology (incl. cancer & neoplasms) (6 items — 12%)
1. Define etiology, pathogenesis, signs/symptoms, and prognosis.
2. Differentiate benign vs. malignant neoplasms and describe metastasis.
3. Use disease-related terminology correctly.

B. Integumentary (4 items — 8%)
1. Describe pathophysiology of common skin disorders (dermatitis, infections, burns).
2. Explain skin cancer types and risk factors.

C. Musculoskeletal (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain pathophysiology of arthritis, osteoporosis, and fractures.
2. Describe common muscular and connective-tissue disorders.

D. Circulatory & Lymphatic (6 items — 12%)
1. Explain atherosclerosis, hypertension, and heart failure.
2. Describe MI, stroke, and thromboembolic disease.
3. Describe common blood and lymphatic disorders (anemia, leukemia).

E. Respiratory (4 items — 8%)
1. Differentiate obstructive vs. restrictive lung disease.
2. Explain pathophysiology of pneumonia, COPD, and asthma.

F. Digestive (6 items — 12%)
1. Explain pathophysiology of GERD, ulcers, and IBD.
2. Describe liver, gallbladder, and pancreatic disorders.

G. Endocrine (4 items — 8%)
1. Differentiate type 1 vs. type 2 diabetes and their complications.
2. Explain thyroid and adrenal disorders.

H. Nervous & Senses (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain pathophysiology of seizure, stroke, and neurodegenerative disease.
2. Describe common disorders of the eye and ear.

I. Reproductive (3 items — 6%)
1. Describe common disorders of the male and female reproductive systems.
2. Explain common sexually transmitted infections.

J. Urinary (3 items — 6%)
1. Differentiate acute kidney injury from chronic kidney disease.
2. Explain UTIs, nephrolithiasis, and glomerular disorders.

K. Mental Disorders (4 items — 8%)
1. Describe major categories of mental illness (mood, anxiety, psychotic).
2. Explain basic pathophysiology and treatment approaches.
