HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PHARMACOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. The test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references below are OFFICIAL (verbatim from the HOSA guideline). Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and to the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (essay tiebreaker).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Drug names, classifications, actions, interactions, administration, and dosage.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Moini. The Pharmacy Technician: A Comprehensive Approach. Cengage, latest ed.
- Ford & Roach. Introductory Clinical Pharmacology. Wolters Kluwer, latest ed.
- Dean Vaughn's Pharmacology by Body System (online).

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Basic Concepts in Pharmacology (8 items — 15%)
1. Define pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics.
2. Explain drug sources, forms, and terminology.

B. Ethical & Legal Responsibilities (5 items — 10%)
1. Describe drug schedules and regulatory agencies (FDA, DEA).
2. Explain legal/ethical duties in medication handling.

C. Pediatric & Geriatric Adaptations (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain dosing and safety considerations for pediatric and geriatric patients.

D. Drug Names, Classifications, Actions & Interactions (15 items — 30%)
1. Classify major drug categories by system and use.
2. Explain mechanisms of action, indications, and adverse effects.
3. Identify common drug interactions and contraindications.

E. Drug Administration (10 items — 20%)
1. Describe routes of administration and the rights of medication administration.
2. Explain safe medication-administration procedures.

F. Drug Measurement, Dosage & Conversions (5 items — 10%)
1. Perform dosage calculations and unit conversions.
2. Interpret medication labels and orders.

G. Technologies in Pharmacology (2 items — 5%)
1. Describe technologies used in medication dispensing and safety.
