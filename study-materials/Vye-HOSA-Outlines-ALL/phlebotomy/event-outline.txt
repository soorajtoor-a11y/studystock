HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PHLEBOTOMY (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. Test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references are OFFICIAL. Item counts = official % applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official area.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes. Type: Skill event — Round 1 written test + Round 2 skill.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Blood collection: technique, specimen handling, safety, and professionalism.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Booth & Mundt. Phlebotomy: A Competency Based Approach. McGraw Hill, latest ed.
- McCall. Phlebotomy Essentials. Jones & Bartlett, latest ed.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Obtaining Blood Samples (incl. tube selection) (21 items — 42%)
1. Perform venipuncture and dermal puncture correctly.
2. Select the correct tubes and order of draw.

B. Specimen Collection & Processing (8 items — 16%)
1. Label, handle, and process specimens properly.

C. Safety Standards & Procedures (5 items — 10%)
1. Apply safety and infection-control standards.

D. Terminology, Anatomy & Physiology (4 items — 8%)
1. Use phlebotomy terminology and vascular anatomy.

E. Time Management & Assignment Organization (4 items — 8%)
1. Organize and prioritize collection assignments.

F. Legal, Ethical & Professional Considerations (4 items — 8%)
1. Explain consent, confidentiality, and professional conduct.

G. Professional Communications (2 items — 4%)
1. Communicate professionally with patients and staff.

H. Clerical Skills & Duties (2 items — 4%)
1. Perform clerical and requisition duties accurately.
