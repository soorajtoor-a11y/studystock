HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — VETERINARY SCIENCE (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. Test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references are OFFICIAL. Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (Round 1 written test; highest test score breaks ties). NOTE: Round 2 is a skill component scored by rating sheet.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Veterinary anatomy, care, diseases, and clinical/surgical principles across common species.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Lawhead & Baker. Introduction to Veterinary Science. Cengage, latest ed.
- Sirois & Wortinger. Principles and Practice of Veterinary Technology. Elsevier, latest ed.
- AAFP & ISFM. Feline-Friendly Handling Guidelines.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Anatomy & Physiology (12 items — 24%)
1. Identify major anatomical structures and systems of common species.
2. Explain normal physiologic function and vital signs.

B. Basic Nutrients (5 items — 10%)
1. Describe essential nutrients and species-appropriate diets.

C. Animal Behavior (3 items — 6%)
1. Explain normal animal behavior and safe handling/restraint.

D. Diseases / Disease Prevention (7 items — 14%)
1. Identify common animal diseases and prevention (vaccination, parasite control).
2. Recognize signs of illness.

E. Zoonoses (3 items — 6%)
1. Identify common zoonotic diseases and their prevention.

F. Clinical Practice (5 items — 10%)
1. Describe routine clinical procedures and patient care.
2. Interpret common lab/diagnostic tests.

G. Emergency Care / First Aid (6 items — 12%)
1. Provide basic animal first aid and recognize emergencies.

H. Principles of Surgery (6 items — 12%)
1. Explain aseptic technique, surgical prep, and instruments.
2. Describe pre- and post-operative care.

I. Ethical, Legal & Safety (3 items — 6%)
1. Explain veterinary ethics, laws, and workplace safety.
