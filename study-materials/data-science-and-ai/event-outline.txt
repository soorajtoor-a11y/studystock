FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — DATA SCIENCE & AI (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Aug 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Data analysis, machine learning, and AI principles: the role of data
and AI in solving real-world problems across industries.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Probability and Statistics Foundations (15 test items)
1. Calculate mean, median, mode, and range of a dataset.
2. Discuss measures of statistical variance (standard deviation, variance, covariance).
3. Discuss characteristics and importance of Gaussian (normal) distribution.
4. Calculate the expected value of a random variable.
5. Differentiate variable types (continuous, discrete).

B. Data Analysis and Statistics for AI (15 test items)
1. Select the most appropriate visual medium for a dataset.
2. Describe diagram types (boxplots, histograms, scatterplots).
3. Discuss multivariate data techniques (dependence/interdependence methods, multiple linear and logistic regression).
4. Discuss the importance of cleaning data.
5. Identify factors affecting data quality (duplicates, low-quality sources, incomplete datasets).
6. Describe how data science algorithms apply to real-world problems (linear regression, decision trees, k-means).

C. Tools for Data and AI (10 test items)
1. Write queries in SQL.
2. Describe common data/AI packages and libraries (Pandas, NumPy, PyTorch).
3. Discuss Python for cleaning and wrangling datasets.
4. Discuss R for data science.
5. Describe characteristics of relational databases.

D. AI Basics (10 test items)
1. Discuss the nature of generative AI.
2. Discuss capabilities and limitations of generative AI.
3. List uses of generative AI (healthcare, research, digital art).
4. Describe AI subfields (computer vision, NLP, human interaction, robotics).
5. Define large language models (LLMs).
6. Discuss the capabilities of LLMs.

E. Machine Learning (10 test items)
1. Discuss the nature of machine learning.
2. Describe training, test, and validation datasets.
3. Describe how ML algorithms behave (neural networks, decision trees, learning functions).
4. Characterize unsupervised, supervised, and reinforcement learning.
5. Select an appropriate ML algorithm for a reasoning problem.
6. Explain the concept of deep learning.

F. Perception, Representation, and Reasoning (10 test items)
1. Explain how predicate logic is used in AI models.
2. Give examples of predicate logic.
3. Discuss logic-based vs. probability-based reasoning.
4. Describe Bayesian networks and components (nodes, edges, Directed Acyclic Graphs).
5. Discuss knowledge representation and reasoning for AI.

G. Privacy and Ethics (10 test items)
1. Discuss dilemmas from AI systems (self-driving vehicles, generative AI, surveillance).
2. Describe how AI inherits bias (algorithmic bias).
3. Discuss security and privacy risks of LLMs.
4. Discuss credibility concerns of LLMs (hallucinations, misinformation).

H. Data Literacy and Foundations (20 test items)
1. Discuss the nature of data science.
2. Describe structured vs. unstructured data.
3. Identify numeric and categorical data points.
4. Convert among data representations (binary, hexadecimal, decimal).
5. Describe data types gathered from various sources.
6. Describe the importance of data wrangling and transformation.
7. Describe the stages of the data science process.
