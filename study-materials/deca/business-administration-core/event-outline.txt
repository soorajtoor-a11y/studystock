DECA STUDY OUTLINE — BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION CORE EXAM (2024–2025)
Source: MBA Research DECA Exam Specifications 2024-2025 (national/ICDC blueprint).
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions.
Used for the "Principles" events (first-year members):
Principles of Business Management & Administration, Principles of Finance,
Principles of Hospitality & Tourism, Principles of Marketing.
Item counts per instructional area (ICDC level) shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The foundational DECA exam covering the Business Administration Core — the
knowledge and skills common to every career cluster: communication, emotional
intelligence, economics, financial literacy, operations, and professional
development.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Business Law (4 items)
1. Explain the nature of legally binding contracts.
2. Explain the role of ethics and social responsibility in business.
3. Describe the nature of legal procedure and regulatory compliance.
4. Explain how businesses can maintain ethical standards.

B. Communications (11 items)
1. Explain the nature of effective business communications.
2. Apply active listening skills.
3. Ask relevant questions and interpret others' nonverbal cues.
4. Use proper grammar and vocabulary in written and oral communication.
5. Write business letters, memos, emails, and simple reports.
6. Make oral presentations and give clear directions.
7. Explain communication techniques that support and encourage a speaker.
8. Facilitate and participate effectively in meetings.
9. Handle telephone calls and inquiries professionally.
10. Explain the nature of staff communication and organizational messaging.

C. Customer Relations (4 items)
1. Explain the nature of positive customer relations.
2. Demonstrate a customer-service mindset.
3. Handle customer/client complaints and difficult situations.
4. Build and maintain long-term customer relationships.

D. Economics (12 items)
1. Distinguish between economic goods and services.
2. Explain the concept of scarcity, choice, and opportunity cost.
3. Explain the principles of supply and demand.
4. Describe the functions of prices in markets.
5. Explain the types of economic systems.
6. Determine the impact of business cycles on business activities.
7. Describe the concept of economic resources (factors of production).
8. Explain measures used to analyze economic conditions (GDP, inflation, unemployment).
9. Describe the concept of competition and market structures.
10. Determine the relationship between government and business.

E. Emotional Intelligence (19 items)
1. Describe the nature of emotional intelligence.
2. Explain the concept of self-understanding and self-esteem.
3. Exhibit a positive attitude and demonstrate self-control.
4. Demonstrate responsible and ethical work behaviors.
5. Recognize and respect individual differences among people.
6. Foster positive working relationships.
7. Use appropriate assertiveness and handle stress constructively.
8. Demonstrate empathy and show interest in others.
9. Manage and resolve conflict effectively.
10. Participate as an effective team member and lead others.
11. Motivate team members and encourage collaboration.
12. Explain the nature of effective leadership and influence.
13. Make responsible decisions and solve problems.

F. Entrepreneurship (1 item)
1. Explain the role and importance of entrepreneurship in the economy.

G. Financial Analysis (13 items)
1. Explain the role of finance in business.
2. Describe the need for and uses of financial information.
3. Read and interpret basic financial statements (income statement, balance sheet).
4. Explain the concept of cash flow and its importance.
5. Explain forms of financial exchange (cash, credit, debit, electronic).
6. Describe the nature of budgets and the budgeting process.
7. Explain the time value of money and return on investment (ROI).
8. Manage personal and business finances responsibly.
9. Explain the purposes and importance of credit.

H. Human Resources Management (1 item)
1. Explain the role and functions of human resources management.

I. Information Management (11 items)
1. Explain the role of information in business decision-making.
2. Use technology and information systems in business.
3. Explain the nature of business records and record-keeping.
4. Maintain and query databases and spreadsheets.
5. Explain the importance of data privacy and security.
6. Assess the quality and reliability of information sources.
7. Use word-processing, spreadsheet, and presentation software effectively.

J. Marketing (1 item)
1. Explain marketing and its role, including the marketing concept and marketing mix.

K. Operations (13 items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of business operations.
2. Comply with health, safety, and environmental regulations (e.g., OSHA).
3. Maintain a safe and secure work environment.
4. Explain the nature of inventory management.
5. Describe the concept of the supply chain.
6. Explain the role of quality control in operations.
7. Explain the purpose and components of standard operating procedures.
8. Manage business risks related to daily operations.

L. Professional Development (9 items)
1. Explain career opportunities and pathways in business.
2. Assess personal interests, aptitudes, and skills.
3. Utilize job-search and networking techniques.
4. Prepare a resume and cover letter.
5. Interview effectively for a job.
6. Explain the need for ongoing education and professional growth.
7. Participate in career planning and goal setting.

M. Strategic Management (1 item)
1. Explain the nature of strategic management, including vision, mission, and SWOT analysis.
