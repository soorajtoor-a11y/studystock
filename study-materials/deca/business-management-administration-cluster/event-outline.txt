DECA STUDY OUTLINE — BUSINESS MANAGEMENT & ADMINISTRATION CLUSTER EXAM (2024–2025)
Source: MBA Research DECA Exam Specifications 2024-2025 (national/ICDC blueprint).
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions (includes BA Core).
Used for: Business Law and Ethics, Human Resources Management.
Item counts per instructional area (ICDC level) shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The Business Management & Administration Cluster exam covers the Business
Administration Core plus management functions: operations, strategic management,
project management, knowledge management, quality management, and risk management.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Business Law (5 items)
1. Explain the nature of legally binding contracts.
2. Describe the nature of business ethics and social responsibility.
3. Explain labor and employment law.
4. Describe intellectual-property protection (patents, trademarks, copyrights).
5. Explain regulatory compliance and its importance.

B. Communications (6 items)
1. Explain the nature of effective business communications.
2. Apply active listening and questioning skills.
3. Write clear business documents and reports.
4. Facilitate and participate in meetings effectively.

C. Customer Relations (1 item)
1. Explain the nature of positive stakeholder and customer relations.

D. Economics (4 items)
1. Explain supply, demand, and market structures.
2. Describe measures of economic activity.
3. Explain the relationship between government and business.

E. Emotional Intelligence (6 items)
1. Foster positive working relationships and teamwork.
2. Manage conflict and demonstrate self-control.
3. Lead, influence, and motivate others.
4. Demonstrate ethical work behaviors.

F. Financial Analysis (5 items)
1. Read and interpret basic financial statements.
2. Explain the nature of budgets and cost control.
3. Explain return on investment and financial decision making.

G. Information Management (6 items)
1. Use technology and information systems in management.
2. Explain the nature of management information systems.
3. Protect data privacy and security.

H. Knowledge Management (8 items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of knowledge management.
2. Describe the use of information to make business decisions.
3. Explain organizational learning and best-practice sharing.
4. Explain the role of knowledge management in innovation.
5. Manage organizational data as a strategic asset.

I. Marketing (1 item)
1. Explain the role of marketing within business management.

J. Operations (26 items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of business operations.
2. Comply with health, safety, and environmental regulations.
3. Maintain a safe and secure work environment.
4. Explain the nature of inventory and materials management.
5. Describe the concept of the supply chain and logistics.
6. Explain the purpose of standard operating procedures.
7. Explain the nature of production and process management.
8. Manage facilities and physical resources.
9. Explain purchasing and vendor management.
10. Describe continuous-improvement and lean-operations concepts.
11. Explain capacity planning and scheduling.
12. Explain the role of technology in operations.
13. Manage operational risk and business continuity.
14. Explain the nature of organizational structure and workflow.
15. Coordinate cross-functional operations to meet goals.

K. Professional Development (4 items)
1. Explain career opportunities in business management.
2. Utilize job-search and networking techniques.
3. Participate in career planning and ongoing education.

L. Project Management (8 items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of project management.
2. Develop a project plan (scope, schedule, budget).
3. Define project roles, responsibilities, and stakeholders.
4. Monitor project progress and manage change.
5. Explain project risk identification and mitigation.
6. Use project-management tools (Gantt charts, milestones).
7. Evaluate project outcomes and lessons learned.

M. Quality Management (5 items)
1. Explain the nature of quality management.
2. Describe continuous-improvement methods (e.g., PDCA, Six Sigma, TQM).
3. Explain the use of quality standards and metrics.
4. Explain the role of the customer in defining quality.

N. Risk Management (5 items)
1. Explain the concept of risk management.
2. Describe the types of business risk.
3. Explain risk-mitigation and transfer strategies (including insurance).
4. Explain business-continuity and crisis planning.

O. Strategic Management (10 items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of strategic management.
2. Describe the strategic-planning process.
3. Explain company vision, mission, and values.
4. Conduct a SWOT analysis.
5. Explain competitive advantage and business strategy.
6. Set organizational goals and measure performance.
7. Explain the role of leadership in executing strategy.
8. Describe change management and organizational culture.
