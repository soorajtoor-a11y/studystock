DECA STUDY OUTLINE — ENTREPRENEURSHIP EXAM (2024–2025)
Source: MBA Research DECA Exam Specifications 2024-2025 (national/ICDC blueprint).
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions.
Draws from all six curriculum planning levels (prerequisite through owner).
Used for: Entrepreneurship Team Decision Making, Entrepreneurship Series (and
related entrepreneurship events).
Item counts per instructional area (ICDC level) shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The Entrepreneurship exam covers starting and running a business: recognizing
opportunities, financing and operating a venture, marketing, and strategic
growth — spanning entry-level through owner-level performance indicators.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Business Law (3 items)
1. Explain legal forms of business ownership (sole proprietorship, partnership, LLC, corporation).
2. Explain contracts, licenses, permits, and regulatory compliance for a new venture.
3. Describe intellectual-property protection for a startup.

B. Channel Management (3 items)
1. Explain distribution options for a new business.
2. Describe the use of e-commerce and direct-to-consumer channels.

C. Communications (1 item)
1. Communicate a business idea clearly to stakeholders and investors.

D. Customer Relations (1 item)
1. Explain the role of customer relationships in a growing venture.

E. Economics (2 items)
1. Explain supply, demand, and competition for a new venture.
2. Describe economic factors affecting small businesses.

F. Emotional Intelligence (4 items)
1. Demonstrate leadership and self-motivation as an entrepreneur.
2. Manage stress, risk, and uncertainty.
3. Build and lead a founding team.

G. Entrepreneurship (14 items)
1. Explain the role and importance of entrepreneurship in the economy.
2. Assess entrepreneurial opportunities and generate business ideas.
3. Describe the characteristics of successful entrepreneurs.
4. Explain the risks and rewards of entrepreneurship.
5. Develop a business concept and value proposition.
6. Explain the components of a business plan.
7. Determine the feasibility of a business idea.
8. Explain methods of entering entrepreneurship (startup, franchise, buyout).
9. Describe the stages of the entrepreneurial process.
10. Explain the role of innovation in entrepreneurship.
11. Explain strategies for growing and scaling a venture.
12. Describe exit strategies for a business owner.

H. Financial Analysis (11 items)
1. Explain startup costs and sources of financing (debt, equity, bootstrapping).
2. Prepare and interpret financial projections and statements.
3. Explain cash-flow management for a new venture.
4. Calculate break-even and profitability.
5. Explain the concept of return on investment for founders/investors.
6. Develop and manage a business budget.
7. Explain the role of investors, venture capital, and crowdfunding.

I. Human Resources Management (4 items)
1. Explain hiring and staffing for a small business.
2. Describe how to build company culture and manage a team.
3. Explain compensation and employee-development basics.

J. Information Management (2 items)
1. Use technology and information systems in a startup.
2. Protect business data and customer information.

K. Market Planning (6 items)
1. Identify and analyze a target market for a new venture.
2. Conduct market segmentation and a SWOT analysis.
3. Develop a marketing plan and objectives.
4. Explain market-entry and positioning strategies.

L. Marketing (1 item)
1. Explain the marketing mix as applied to a new business.

M. Marketing-Information Management (2 items)
1. Conduct market research to validate a business idea.
2. Interpret market data to guide decisions.

N. Operations (14 items)
1. Explain the nature of operations for a new venture.
2. Set up business processes and standard operating procedures.
3. Manage inventory, suppliers, and the supply chain.
4. Comply with health, safety, and business regulations.
5. Explain facility, equipment, and location decisions.
6. Explain the use of technology to run operations.
7. Manage day-to-day operational risk.
8. Explain quality control in a small business.

O. Pricing (2 items)
1. Explain pricing strategies for a new product/service.
2. Calculate markups and set profitable prices.

P. Product/Service Management (4 items)
1. Develop and refine a product/service offering.
2. Explain branding and positioning for a startup.
3. Explain the product life cycle for new ventures.

Q. Professional Development (4 items)
1. Explain the entrepreneurial mindset and continuous learning.
2. Build a professional network and mentorship relationships.

R. Promotion (8 items)
1. Explain the promotional mix for a new venture.
2. Describe low-cost and digital marketing strategies.
3. Explain social-media and content marketing for startups.
4. Develop a promotional plan and messaging.
5. Explain publicity and word-of-mouth marketing.

S. Quality Management (1 item)
1. Explain the role of quality in building a business reputation.

T. Risk Management (4 items)
1. Explain the types of risk facing a new business.
2. Describe risk-mitigation and insurance for small businesses.
3. Explain business-continuity planning.

U. Selling (1 item)
1. Explain the selling process for an entrepreneur.

V. Strategic Management (8 items)
1. Explain vision, mission, and goal setting for a venture.
2. Conduct a SWOT analysis and competitive analysis.
3. Explain growth and scaling strategies.
4. Describe strategic decision making under uncertainty.
5. Explain how to build competitive advantage.
