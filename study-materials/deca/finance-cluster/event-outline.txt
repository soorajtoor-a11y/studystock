DECA STUDY OUTLINE — FINANCE CLUSTER EXAM (2024–2025)
Source: MBA Research DECA Exam Specifications 2024-2025 (national/ICDC blueprint).
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions (includes BA Core).
Used for: Accounting Applications, Business Finance, Financial Services,
Financial Consulting.
Item counts per instructional area (ICDC level) shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The Finance Cluster exam covers the Business Administration Core plus finance
functions: financial analysis, financial-information management (accounting),
risk management, and financial services.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Business Law (7 items)
1. Explain the nature of legally binding contracts.
2. Describe the regulation of the finance industry and compliance requirements.
3. Explain the nature of banking and consumer-finance law.
4. Describe the role of ethics in finance.
5. Explain types of business ownership and their legal implications.

B. Communications (3 items)
1. Communicate financial information clearly to clients.
2. Explain communication techniques that build client trust.

C. Customer Relations (4 items)
1. Explain the nature of positive client relations in finance.
2. Handle client complaints and difficult situations.
3. Build and maintain long-term client relationships.

D. Economics (4 items)
1. Explain the principles of supply and demand.
2. Explain the role of the Federal Reserve and monetary policy.
3. Describe measures of economic activity (GDP, inflation, interest rates).

E. Emotional Intelligence (6 items)
1. Foster positive working relationships.
2. Demonstrate ethical work behaviors and integrity.
3. Manage conflict and demonstrate self-control.
4. Lead and motivate others.

F. Financial Analysis (30 items)
1. Explain the role of finance in business.
2. Describe the need for and uses of financial information.
3. Read and interpret the income statement, balance sheet, and cash-flow statement.
4. Calculate and interpret financial ratios (liquidity, profitability, solvency).
5. Explain the concept of cash flow and working-capital management.
6. Explain the time value of money (present value, future value).
7. Calculate and interpret return on investment (ROI).
8. Explain the nature of budgets and the budgeting process.
9. Describe sources of financing (debt vs. equity).
10. Explain the concept of risk and return in financial decisions.
11. Explain forms of financial exchange (cash, credit, debit, electronic).
12. Describe the purposes and importance of credit.
13. Explain the nature of financial planning for individuals and businesses.
14. Explain the concept of interest (simple and compound).
15. Explain the role of financial markets and investing.
16. Analyze capital-budgeting decisions.

G. Financial-Information Management (12 items)
1. Explain the nature of financial-information management (accounting).
2. Describe the accounting cycle and double-entry bookkeeping.
3. Explain the accounting equation (assets = liabilities + owner's equity).
4. Journalize and post business transactions.
5. Prepare and interpret financial statements from accounting records.
6. Explain the nature of payroll and payroll records.
7. Explain accounting principles (GAAP) and internal controls.
8. Use accounting software and information systems.
9. Explain the role of auditing in financial reporting.

H. Information Management (5 items)
1. Use technology and information systems in finance.
2. Protect the privacy and security of financial data.
3. Assess the reliability of financial information sources.

I. Marketing (1 item)
1. Explain the role of marketing in promoting financial products/services.

J. Operations (4 items)
1. Explain the nature of operations in financial institutions.
2. Comply with regulations and standard operating procedures.
3. Explain fraud prevention and internal-control operations.

K. Professional Development (15 items)
1. Explain career opportunities in finance and accounting.
2. Assess personal interests, aptitudes, and skills for finance careers.
3. Utilize job-search and networking techniques.
4. Prepare a resume and interview effectively.
5. Explain the need for professional certifications (e.g., CPA, CFP).
6. Participate in career planning and ongoing education.
7. Demonstrate professional ethics and standards in finance.

L. Risk Management (9 items)
1. Explain the concept of risk management.
2. Describe the types of business and financial risk.
3. Explain the nature of insurance and its role in managing risk.
4. Describe methods of managing and transferring risk.
5. Explain the concept of diversification in reducing investment risk.
6. Explain the role of credit risk and default.
