DECA STUDY OUTLINE — MARKETING CLUSTER EXAM (2024–2025)
Source: MBA Research DECA Exam Specifications 2024-2025 (national/ICDC blueprint).
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions (includes BA Core).
Used for: Apparel & Accessories Marketing, Automotive Services Marketing,
Business Services Marketing, Buying & Merchandising, Food Marketing, Integrated
Marketing Campaign (Event/Product/Service), Marketing Communications, Marketing
Management, Professional Selling, Retail Merchandising, Sports & Entertainment
Marketing.
Item counts per instructional area (ICDC level) shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The Marketing Cluster exam covers the Business Administration Core plus the
marketing functions: product/service management, promotion, selling, pricing,
channel management, market planning, and marketing-information management.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Business Law (1 item)
1. Explain the regulation of marketing activities and consumer protection.

B. Channel Management (7 items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of channel management.
2. Describe the use of distribution/marketing channels.
3. Explain the relationship between customer service and channel management.
4. Explain the nature of channels of distribution (direct, indirect, intensity).
5. Coordinate channel management with other marketing activities.
6. Explain the nature of physical distribution and logistics.
7. Describe the impact of e-commerce on channel management.

C. Communications (3 items)
1. Apply effective written and verbal communication in marketing.
2. Explain communication techniques that support customers.
3. Use proper grammar and messaging in marketing materials.

D. Customer Relations (1 item)
1. Demonstrate a customer-service mindset in marketing.

E. Economics (4 items)
1. Explain the principles of supply and demand.
2. Explain the concept of competition and market structures.
3. Describe the functions of prices in markets.
4. Determine economic utilities created by business activities.

F. Emotional Intelligence (6 items)
1. Foster positive working relationships and teamwork.
2. Manage conflict and demonstrate self-control.
3. Demonstrate ethical work behaviors.
4. Lead and motivate others in a marketing setting.

G. Financial Analysis (4 items)
1. Interpret basic financial information used in marketing decisions.
2. Explain the nature of marketing budgets and ROI.
3. Explain forms of financial exchange in sales transactions.

H. Information Management (3 items)
1. Use technology and information systems in marketing.
2. Explain the role of data in marketing decisions.

I. Market Planning (5 items)
1. Explain the concept of market and market identification.
2. Explain the nature of a target market and market segmentation.
3. Develop a marketing plan and set marketing objectives.
4. Conduct a SWOT analysis for a marketing situation.
5. Explain the role of situation analysis in market planning.

J. Marketing (1 item)
1. Explain the marketing concept and the marketing mix (product, price, place, promotion).

K. Marketing-Information Management (16 items)
1. Explain the need for marketing information.
2. Describe the nature of marketing research.
3. Explain the marketing research process.
4. Identify types of marketing research (primary vs. secondary).
5. Describe methods of collecting marketing data (surveys, observation, focus groups).
6. Explain the nature of sampling in marketing research.
7. Interpret marketing data and research findings.
8. Explain the use of a marketing-information system (MkIS).
9. Explain the role of ethics in marketing-information management.
10. Explain the use of descriptive statistics in marketing decision making.
11. Track and analyze marketing performance metrics.
12. Describe the nature of customer relationship management (CRM) data.

L. Operations (4 items)
1. Explain the nature of operations as it relates to marketing.
2. Manage inventory and merchandising operations.
3. Explain loss-prevention and security in a retail setting.

M. Pricing (4 items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of pricing.
2. Describe pricing objectives, policies, and strategies.
3. Explain factors affecting pricing decisions.
4. Calculate and apply markups, markdowns, and break-even.

N. Product/Service Management (15 items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of product/service management.
2. Describe the concept of product mix, product line, and product depth/width.
3. Explain the product life cycle and its marketing implications.
4. Explain the nature of product branding, packaging, and labeling.
5. Explain the concept of product positioning.
6. Describe factors used to position products/businesses.
7. Explain business ethics in product/service management.
8. Identify product opportunities and new-product development.
9. Explain warranties, guarantees, and product support services.
10. Explain the role of quality in product/service management.

O. Professional Development (5 items)
1. Explain career opportunities in marketing.
2. Utilize job-search and networking techniques.
3. Participate in career planning and goal setting.

P. Promotion (13 items)
1. Explain the role of promotion in marketing.
2. Describe the elements of the promotional mix.
3. Explain the types of advertising media (traditional and digital).
4. Explain the nature of sales promotion.
5. Explain the nature of publicity and public relations.
6. Explain the concept of personal selling within promotion.
7. Describe the use of digital and social-media marketing.
8. Explain the components of advertisements and promotional messages.
9. Calculate media costs (reach, frequency, CPM).
10. Explain the role of ethics and regulation in promotion.

Q. Selling (8 items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of selling.
2. Explain the role of customer service in selling.
3. Describe the steps of the selling process.
4. Determine customer/client needs and buying motives.
5. Explain the selling process for feature-benefit selling.
6. Handle customer objections and close the sale.
7. Perform suggestion and up-selling techniques.
8. Process sales transactions and follow up after the sale.
