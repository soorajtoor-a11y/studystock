DECA STUDY OUTLINE — PERSONAL FINANCIAL LITERACY EXAM (2024–2025)
Source: MBA Research DECA Exam Specifications 2024-2025 (national/ICDC blueprint).
Based on the National Standards for Personal Financial Education (Council for
Economic Education & Jump$tart Coalition).
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions.
Used for: Personal Financial Literacy (PFL) event.
Item counts per topic (ICDC level) shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The Personal Financial Literacy exam covers managing one's own money: earning
income, spending, saving, investing, managing credit, and managing risk.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Earned Income (16 items)
1. Explain sources of income (wages, salary, tips, commissions, self-employment).
2. Explain how education, skills, and career choices affect income.
3. Read and interpret a paycheck and pay stub.
4. Explain payroll taxes, income taxes, and withholdings (W-4, W-2).
5. Explain the difference between gross pay and net pay.
6. Describe employee benefits and their value.
7. Explain how to file a basic income tax return.
8. Describe entrepreneurship and self-employment as income sources.

B. Spending (14 items)
1. Explain the nature and importance of a personal budget.
2. Create and manage a spending plan.
3. Distinguish between needs and wants.
4. Explain comparison shopping and consumer decision making.
5. Describe payment methods (cash, debit, credit, mobile, checks).
6. Explain the cost of using credit vs. cash for purchases.
7. Explain how to track expenses and avoid overspending.
8. Describe consumer rights and protections.

C. Saving (13 items)
1. Explain the reasons and benefits of saving.
2. Describe types of savings accounts and financial institutions.
3. Explain the concept of compound interest and how it grows savings.
4. Explain the importance of an emergency fund.
5. Set short-term and long-term savings goals.
6. Compare interest rates, APY, and account fees.
7. Explain how inflation affects the value of savings.

D. Investing (21 items)
1. Explain the purpose and benefits of investing.
2. Describe types of investments (stocks, bonds, mutual funds, ETFs, real estate).
3. Explain the relationship between risk and return.
4. Explain the concept of diversification.
5. Describe how the stock market works.
6. Explain compound growth and time value of money in investing.
7. Explain retirement-savings vehicles (401(k), IRA, Roth IRA).
8. Describe the role of employer matching and tax advantages.
9. Explain investment fees, dividends, and capital gains.
10. Explain the importance of starting to invest early.
11. Describe how to research and evaluate investments.

E. Managing Credit (21 items)
1. Explain the nature and uses of credit.
2. Describe types of credit (credit cards, loans, mortgages, student loans).
3. Explain interest, APR, and the cost of borrowing.
4. Explain how credit scores and credit reports work.
5. Describe factors that affect a credit score.
6. Explain how to build and maintain good credit.
7. Describe the consequences of missed payments and default.
8. Explain strategies for managing and paying off debt.
9. Explain predatory lending and how to avoid it.
10. Describe consumer credit protections and rights.
11. Explain how to read a credit-card statement.

F. Managing Risk (15 items)
1. Explain the concept of risk and risk management for individuals.
2. Describe types of insurance (health, auto, life, homeowners/renters, disability).
3. Explain how insurance works (premiums, deductibles, coverage, claims).
4. Explain how to protect against identity theft and fraud.
5. Describe strategies to reduce personal financial risk.
6. Explain the importance of financial and estate planning.
7. Explain how to evaluate insurance needs at different life stages.
