FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — ACCOUNTING (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Eligibility note: for students with no more than 2 semesters of HS accounting.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Demonstrates understanding of fundamental accounting principles:
financial statements, journal entries, and the accounting cycle.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Journalizing
1. Prepare a multi-column journal for recording data.
2. Record transactions (accounts receivable/payable) in appropriate journals.
3. Journalize business transactions (cash receipts, cash payments, purchases, sales, general).
4. Journalize adjusting and closing entries for a business.
5. Prove and rule journals.

B. Account Classification
1. Evaluate assets and liabilities to determine their function in an accounting system.
2. Identify asset accounts.
3. Identify liability accounts.
4. Identify capital or owner's equity accounts.
5. Create a chart of accounts to reflect business needs and update as needed.
6. Differentiate among fixed assets, intangible assets, and current assets.
7. Differentiate between current and long-term liabilities.

C. Terminology, Concepts, and Practices
1. Identify the steps in the accounting cycle.
2. Identify and apply the accounting equation.
3. Maintain knowledge of current financial laws, regulations, and policies.
4. Identify resources for information on financial laws, regulations, and policies.
5. Describe the types of financial statements, their purposes, and differences.
6. Define general accounting terms (asset, liability, accounts payable, capital, income, expense).
7. Define double entry accounting, debits, and credits.
8. Describe the purpose of accounting and its role in the economy.
9. Describe the purpose of GAAP.

D. Income Statement
1. Apply the income statement equation to determine revenue and expenses.
2. Prepare an income statement reporting results of operations.
3. Interpret the information on an income statement.

E. Posting
1. Post from journals to a general ledger.
2. Post from journals to a subsidiary ledger.
3. Record postings in journal and ledger reference columns.
4. Balance and verify the ledger accounts.
5. Prepare a trial balance.
6. Post adjusting and closing entries.
7. Prepare schedules for subsidiary ledgers.

F. Balance Sheet
1. Classify account titles into assets, liabilities, and owner's equity.
2. Prepare a balance sheet reporting the entity's financial condition.
3. Interpret the information found on a balance sheet.

G. Worksheet
1. Identify information needed to prepare a worksheet.
2. Record adjustments on the worksheet.
3. Prepare trial balance, income statement, and balance sheet sections.

H. Bank Reconciliation
1. Maintain a check register.
2. Compare checkbook to bank statement.
3. Identify inequalities and make adjusting entries.
4. Prepare a bank reconciliation statement.
5. Journalize banking transactions.

I. Payroll
1. Identify methods for determining gross earnings (piece-rate, hourly, commission).
2. Explain the purposes of withholdings and other deductions.
3. Verify time and wage documents (timecards, etc.).
4. Compute employee gross earnings, deductions, and net pay.
5. Maintain employee earnings records.
6. Journalize payroll transactions.
7. Record payroll earnings and deductions in a payroll register.
8. Prepare payroll checks and vouchers.
9. Complete federal/state employment forms (W-4, W-2, quarterly reports, unemployment tax).

J. Types of Ownership
1. Identify types of business organizations and functions.
2. Identify reporting and information flow required per ownership type.
3. Explain characteristics of corporations.
4. Define advantages and disadvantages of corporations.
5. Explain characteristics of partnerships.
6. Define advantages and disadvantages of partnerships.
7. Explain characteristics of sole proprietorships.
8. Define advantages and disadvantages of sole proprietorships.
9. Compare accounting implications for different ownership types.

K. Depreciation
1. Identify the appropriate method for calculating depreciation.
2. Determine depreciation using straight-line, units of production,
   double-declining balance, and sum of the years' digits.
3. Maintain records for depreciation of plant assets.
4. Journalize and post depreciation on plant assets.

L. Manual and Computerized Systems
1. Operate financial software to generate usable data.
2. Operate electronic spreadsheet software to create formulas and reports.
3. Adapt accounting/record-keeping functions to computerized systems.
4. Use a spreadsheet to prepare payroll, worksheets, and reports.
5. Enter data and print checks to process payroll.
6. Identify and review financial management software packages.

M. Ethics
1. Identify confidentiality concepts and policies.
2. Identify characteristics of professional conduct.
3. Identify and practice good ethical behavior.
4. Differentiate between good and poor business ethics.
5. Adhere to established company rules, regulations, and policies.
6. Identify issues and trends affecting computers and information privacy.
7. Implement security measures to minimize loss and create trust.
