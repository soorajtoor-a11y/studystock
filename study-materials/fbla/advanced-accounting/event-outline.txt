FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — ADVANCED ACCOUNTING (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Higher-level accounting concepts: corporate accounting, managerial
accounting, and financial analysis.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Financial Statements
1. Describe types of financial statements; explain purposes and compare differences.
2. Prepare a trial balance, worksheet (8 and 10 columns), and adjusted trial balance.
3. Prepare and interpret an income statement.
4. Prepare and interpret a balance sheet.
5. Prepare a statement of Owner's Equity / Partner's Equity / Retained Earnings.
6. Explain the purpose of each statement and how they articulate with each other.
7. Analyze the effects of revenue on financial statements.
8. Use manual and computerized systems for preparing statements and evaluating changes.
9. Determine acceptable levels of financial performance for management decisions.
10. Define terminology and classifications for financial statements.

B. Corporate Accounting
1. Identify methods for forming a corporation; complete the accounting cycle for a corporation or not-for-profit.
2. Identify and describe classes of stock and the rights of each class.
3. Journalize capital stock issuance, organization costs, stock subscriptions, dividend declaration/payment.
4. Differentiate stock splits vs. stock dividends and their effects on equity.
5. Determine stockholders' equity and EPS; prepare a statement of stockholders' equity.
6. Perform the closing process end-to-end for a corporation (closing entries, post-closing trial balance, statements/reports).
7. Apply accounting techniques for formation, allocation/distribution of earnings, and dissolution of corporations.
8. Describe purposes of revenue, expense, and drawing accounts and effects on equity.
9. Describe liability and taxation levels for Subchapter S Corporations and LLCs.
10. Analyze information derived from corporate financial statements.

C. Partnerships
1. Identify partnership characteristics; steps for forming and admitting partners.
2. Create partnership financial statements (worksheet, income statement, distribution of net income, owner's equity statement, balance sheet, adjusting/closing entries, post-closing trial balance).
3. Apply techniques for formation, allocation of earnings, and dissolution of a partnership.

D. Ratios and Data Analysis
1. Compute financial ratios; analyze statements via horizontal analysis, vertical analysis, and common ratios.
2. Analyze how a change in an independent variable produces change in a dependent variable.
3. Calculate financial strength: current ratio, acid-test ratio, debt ratio, working capital, equity ratio, EPS.
4. Apply differential analysis to decisions (make vs. buy, lease vs. buy, discontinue a department/product, repair vs. replace, discounted special orders).

E. Accounts Receivable and Payable
1. Journalize merchandise sales (cash, on account, credit card); post; prepare schedule of accounts receivable.
2. Journalize purchases on account; net purchases; post; prepare schedule of accounts payable.
3. Write off uncollectible accounts: direct write-off and allowance methods.
4. Prepare adjustments: aging of receivables, percentage of sales, percentage of receivables.
5. Explain notes payable/receivable; calculate, record, and journalize interest and payments.
6. Prepare adjusting and reversing entries for prepaid/accrued expenses and unearned/accrued revenue.

F. Budgeting and Cash Flow
1. Review revenues, expenditures, trends, priorities, projections to prepare a budget.
2. Prepare master and flexible budgets; describe uses of each.
3. Compare budgeted vs. actual amounts via a performance report.
4. Use cost-volume-profit and contribution margin analysis to plan operations.
5. Prepare budget analysis using spreadsheet software.
6. Calculate and prepare cash flow statements.
7. Determine cash flows from operating, investing, and financing activities.

G. Cost Accounting / Manufacturing
1. Define managerial accounting; cost accounting terminology and techniques.
2. Interpret costs and accounts unique to manufacturing.
3. Calculate manufacturing cost of finished goods (direct materials, labor, factory overhead).
4. Prepare a schedule of cost of goods manufactured; relate it to the income statement.
5. Journalize entries summarizing cost records at fiscal year end.
6. Use allocation methods for overhead and indirect costs.
7. Explain variable, mixed, and fixed costs.
8. Compute break-even and cost-revenue analysis; interpret results.
9. Prepare materials ledger and finished goods ledger.
10. Apply software to cost accounting records.

H. Departmentalized Accounting
1. Define departmentalized accounting terminology.
2. Journalize departmental purchases/cash payments and sales/cash receipts; post to ledgers.
3. Create departmental statement of gross profit, financial statements, end-of-period work.
4. Prepare and maintain payroll records for a departmentalized business.
5. Journalize and post adjusting/closing entries.
6. Apply software to departmentalized records.

I. Ethics
1. Describe importance of integrity, confidentiality, and ethical standards in financial statements and data use.
2. Explain the importance of ethical business decisions.
3. Adhere to financial laws, regulations, and company policies.
4. Utilize security measures to minimize loss and create trust.

J. Income Tax
1. Complete basic business tax forms (941, 940, payroll, etc.).
2. Identify differences between pretax financial income and taxable income.
3. Identify deferred income tax issues; effects of tax rates; presentation in statements.
4. Differentiate personal vs. business taxation, including tax-planning strategies.
5. Calculate federal income tax expense; record adjustment; complete corporate worksheet.
6. Identify basic differences between GAAP and tax law.
7. Distinguish tax avoidance vs. tax evasion.
8. Evaluate accrual methods, inventory methods, depreciation options for tax effects.
9. Analyze business decisions for tax impact.

K. Inventory
1. Explain the importance of an inventory system and its valuation.
2. Determine inventory value using LIFO, FIFO, Weighted Average.
3. Estimate inventory cost using retail and gross profit methods.
4. Calculate and analyze merchandise turnover ratio.
5. Determine costs of obsolete inventory/materials.

L. Journalizing and Posting
1. Journalize cash receipts and payments; post to ledgers.
2. Journalize purchases and sales on account.
3. Journalize merchandise returns; post appropriately.
4. Journalize and post adjusting and closing entries.
5. Record transactions in general and special journals with correct correction methods.
6. Post to general and subsidiary ledgers; prove accuracy.
7. Journalize using multicolumn journals.
8. Journalize adjustments of deferred expenses/revenue and accrued expenses/revenue.
9. Prove and rule journals.
10. Prepare a post-closing trial balance.
11. Journalize entries in a voucher system.

M. Payroll
1. Calculate earnings: hourly, piece rate, salary, commission, salary+commission.
2. Calculate and journalize deductions (federal income tax, social security, Medicare, state tax, other) to determine net pay.
3. Calculate and journalize employer payroll taxes and employer-paid benefits.
4. Prepare payroll checks and vouchers.
5. Record earnings and deductions in a payroll register.
6. Maintain employee earnings record.
7. Complete federal/state employment tax forms (W-4, W-2, quarterly, unemployment).

N. Plant Assets and Depreciation
1. Explain the need for recording depreciation.
2. Calculate/journalize annual and partial-year depreciation: straight line, sum-of-years' digits, declining balance, production unit, MACRS, depletion.
3. Record disposition of plant assets (buy, sell, trade).
4. Compare depreciation methods and their effects on asset value.
5. Identify property, plant, and equipment assets; record initial costs.

O. Purchases and Sales
1. Evaluate sales and purchasing activities to minimize expenses.
2. Differentiate cost of sales vs. general operating expenses.
3. Compare transactions recorded as assets vs. expenses (e.g., supplies).
4. Calculate sales dollars/units required for a target net income.
5. Calculate contribution margin rate, breakeven point, required sales.
6. Describe effects of changes in volume, unit costs, and prices on net income.
7. Calculate cost of goods sold for a period.
8. Calculate amount due with terms of sale, sales tax, discounts.
9. Calculate cash/trade discounts with terms of sale, shipping.
10. Identify difference between debit and credit memorandums.
