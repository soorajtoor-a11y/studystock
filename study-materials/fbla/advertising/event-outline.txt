FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — ADVERTISING (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Question counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Advertising principles and strategies: media planning, branding,
consumer behavior, and promotional techniques to reach target audiences.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Basic Marketing Functions (4 items)
1. Explain marketing concepts and benefits of marketing.
2. Explain the functions in marketing goods and services.
3. Identify the marketing mix (product, price, place, promotion).
4. Describe the process for new product/service development.
5. Explain the rationale for extending a product line.
6. Explain the functions of packaging.
7. Describe the importance of branding, packaging, and labeling.
8. Describe positioning factors (features, benefits, price, quality, competition, brand loyalty).
9. Identify factors that influence a product's price.
10. Demonstrate knowledge of basic advertising terms.

B. Personal Selling & Sales Promotion (4 items)
1. Explain the steps in the sales process (leads, approach, determine needs, present, overcome objections, close, follow up).
2. Describe reasons sales promotion is needed (influence decisions, encourage trial, strengthen loyalty, influence behavior).

C. Traditional Advertising Media (4 items)
1. Describe traditional media (TV, radio, print, direct mail, flyers/brochures, outdoor).
2. Identify media selection criteria (reach, frequency, lead time, cost).

D. Alternative Forms of Advertising Media (4 items)
1. Recognize alternative media outlets (transit, aerial, cinema, product placement, directory).
2. Describe latest media trends (video advertising, mobile advertising).

E. Branding & Positioning (4 items)
1. Explain why branding is important to business success.
2. Describe product positioning strategies.

F. Advertising & the Economy (4 items)
1. Identify economic factors that impact advertising.
2. Describe how advertising stimulates the economy.

G. Promotion (3 items)
1. Define promotional mix and explain its importance.
2. Identify steps in developing the promotional plan.

H. Advertising & Public Relations (3 items)
1. Discuss advantages and disadvantages of advertising.
2. Explain the need for public relations.

I. The Advertising Plan (4 items)
1. Identify steps in creating an advertising plan (SWOT, objectives, budget, creative strategy, execute, evaluate).
2. Recognize the analyses performed when creating an advertising plan.

J. Creation of the Advertisement (3 items)
1. Describe different advertising creative formats.
2. Identify copywriting, art, and production stage processes.
3. Describe methods to evaluate advertising effectiveness.
4. Identify simple approaches to determine advertising effectiveness.

K. Consumer-Oriented Advertising (3 items)
1. Define consumer-oriented advertising and how it is used.
2. Describe how sponsorships connect with consumers.

L. Financial Planning for Advertising (5 items)
1. Explain budget-setting strategies (percentage of sales, competition matching, objective and task, response model).
2. Describe financial reports used for planning and operating.
3. Describe factors that affect the advertising budget.
4. Determine whether ad spending generates brand awareness.
5. Identify costs for different forms of advertising.
6. Explain the purpose of an advertising budget.
7. Explain rate of return on advertising investment.

M. Consumer Behavior (3 items)
1. Recognize the importance of understanding customer wants and needs.
2. Identify the five-step consumer decision-making process.

N. Consumer Purchase Classifications (4 items)
1. Distinguish types of consumer purchases and levels of buying involvement.
2. Describe different types of consumer products.

O. Influences on Consumer Behavior (2 items)
1. Discuss consumer buying motives.
2. Describe individual, social, and marketing influences on behavior.

P. The Target Market (2 items)
1. Define target market and strategies to reach different targets.
2. Explain the process for selecting target markets.

Q. Market Segmentation (4 items)
1. Differentiate segmentation vs. mass marketing.
2. Describe market segmentation categories.
3. Define demographics.
4. Explain how demographics influence campaigns.
5. Differentiate buying habits vs. buying preferences.
6. Explain selecting segments with market potential.

R. Product Development (2 items)
1. Identify stages of new product development.
2. Explain product levels and the product mix.

S. Product Life Cycle (2 items)
1. Explain the stages of the product life cycle.
2. Describe real-world applications of the life cycle.

T. Price Planning (2 items)
1. Discuss pricing objectives used by businesses.
2. Compare pricing strategies (skimming, penetration, competition, supply and demand).

U. Channels of Distribution & Supply Chain Management (4 items)
1. Explain channel functions and members (producers, retailers, wholesalers, transport, warehouses, agents/brokers, consumers).
2. Identify channel options (direct, indirect, multiple, nontraditional).
3. Describe supply chain processes (purchasing, order processing, warehousing/shipping, risk management).
4. Identify factors influencing supply chain decisions (international markets, outsourcing, digital distribution, eco-friendly practices).
5. Define logistics and its role in distribution.
6. Explain importance of storage, handling, and packaging.

V. Types of Promotion (2 items)
1. Describe promotion types (endorsements, word-of-mouth, direct marketing).
2. Explain visual merchandising purpose and components.

W. Marketing Research for Advertising (2 items)
1. Explain the marketing research process (define problem, research design, data collection method, collect, analyze, report).
2. Describe benefits and limitations of marketing research.
3. Define primary and secondary market research.

X. Developing an Effective Sales Promotion Strategy (2 items)
1. Identify consumer promotions (coupons, price deals, rebates, premiums, loyalty programs, sampling, contests, sweepstakes, POP displays).
2. Identify trade promotions (push money, deal loader, trade allowance).

Y. Consumer-Oriented Advertising & Sales Promotion (2 items)
1. Explain the communication process (sender, encoding, channel, receiver, decoding, noise, feedback).
2. Explain purposes of advertising and promotional communication.
3. Explain the purpose of consumer-oriented sales promotions.
4. Describe how direct marketing reaches consumers.

Z. Types of Communication (2 items)
1. Explain why interpersonal communication matters in advertising/promotion.
2. Describe other types of business communication.

AA. Effective Advertising & Promotional Messages (2 items)
1. Explain the four C's (comprehension, connection, credibility, contagiousness).
2. Describe how to create effective advertising messages.

BB. Legal & Ethical Issues Affecting Advertising (2 items)
1. Explain the need for government regulation and the FTC's role.
2. Describe other government regulatory agencies.

CC. Ethics in Advertising (2 items)
1. Identify ethical aspects of advertising.
2. Explain how the FTC assures truth in advertising.

DD. Advertising in a Multicultural Market / Global Vision (5 items)
1. Recognize factors impacting global marketing.
2. Describe global marketing challenges.

EE. Diversity & Advertising (2 items)
1. Describe the impact of diversity on advertising.
2. Identify characteristics of growing ethnic markets.
3. Explain advertising needs for different parts of the world.
4. Explain the impact of multiculturalism and multi-generation audiences.

FF. History of Advertising (1 item)
1. Explain how advertising has changed with the times.
2. Describe inventions that impacted advertising.

GG. Influences on Advertising (1 item)
1. Describe environmental influences on advertising.
2. Describe consumer influences on advertising.

HH. The Advertising Industry & Careers (2 items)
1. Explain how the advertising industry works.
2. Describe careers in advertising.
3. Determine characteristics for a successful advertising career.
4. Identify the skill set needed.

II. The Internet & Advertising (1 item)
1. Describe internet advertising types (email, banner/pop-up, search engine, website, blogs, social media).
2. Explain metrics to consider (hits, page views, visits, unique visitors).

JJ. Self-Regulation (1 item)
1. Explain self-regulation in the advertising industry.
2. Recognize how consumers can regulate business practices.

KK. Cross-Cultural Communication (1 item)
1. Recognize how verbal and nonverbal communication differ across cultures.
2. Explain the importance of translation in global marketing.
