FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — AGRIBUSINESS (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Business principles applied to the agriculture industry: agricultural
marketing, economics, finance, and supply chain management.
(AFNR = Agriculture, Food, and Natural Resources)

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Health, Safety, and Environmental Management
1. Use tools, equipment, machinery, and technology appropriate to AFNR work.
2. Define contamination/injury risk levels to set safety priorities.
3. Identify insurance needs in agricultural business (property, health, life, crop, liability).
4. Develop response plans for emergencies.
5. Identify hazards and acquire first aid skills for environmental safety.
6. Examine regulations for safety, health, environmental management, and sustainability.
7. Enact procedures demonstrating safety/health/environmental responsibility.
8. Demonstrate methods to correct common hazards.
9. Apply personal and group health and safety practices.

B. Management Analysis and Decision Making
1. Identify and compare the four agricultural business organizations: sole proprietorship, partnership, cooperative, corporation.
2. Explain environmental considerations in AFNR management decisions.
3. Predict positive and negative impacts of AFNR activities.
4. Analyze SWOT for an AFNR enterprise or business unit.
5. Develop an operation/production plan for required product or service levels.
6. Develop business goals and strategies capitalizing on AFNR market opportunities.
7. Identify and observe ethical standards in AFNR businesses.

C. Marketing
1. Use industry-accepted marketing principles for AFNR objectives.
2. Communicate the importance of AFNR to the general public.
3. Evaluate alternative strategies (value-adding, branding, niche marketing); propose modifications.
4. Perform marketing analysis: competitors, customers, policy environment, regulations, standards, resources.
5. Develop and implement marketing strategies for agricultural commodities, products, services.

D. Economics
1. Utilize economic principles to establish and manage an AFNR enterprise.
2. Describe the role of global supply and demand on AFNR.
3. State the economic output of AFNR-related industries in the US.
4. Evaluate the impact of AFNR activities in the local community.
5. Apply economic principles to marketing.
6. Differentiate ownership types; outline AFNR business structure in a capitalist economy.
7. Classify characteristics of successful AFNR entrepreneurs.

E. Finance and Accounting
1. Manage cash budgets, credit budgets, and credit using GAAP.
2. Budget resources (capital, human, financial, time).
3. Manage assets for optimum utilization.
4. Manage risk of liabilities.
5. Prepare and interpret financial statements (balance sheet, P&L, cash flow).
6. Prepare tax forms (W-4, I-9, depreciation, 1099, Workers Compensation).
7. Determine cost of doing business.
8. Compare advantages/disadvantages of banking procedures (e.g., reconciliation).
9. Analyze investment options (buy, lease, finance, risk).
10. Calculate costs of carrying inventory.
11. Use record keeping for objectives, budgets, and legal compliance.
12. Describe inventory management and selling price determination.
13. Explain the importance of return on investment.

F. Terminology and Trends
1. Examine economic, social, technological changes impacting AFNR.
2. Analyze interactions among AFNR systems (food, fiber, fuel, natural resources).
3. Explain foundational cycles/systems (plant/animal life cycles, nutrient and water cycles, animal production).
4. Explain how changes in one AFNR system affect other systems.
5. Explain how regulations and major laws impact AFNR management.
6. Describe current issues impacting AFNR.
7. Explain the impact of sustainability on AFNR practices.
8. Envision emerging technology and globalization influences on markets.
