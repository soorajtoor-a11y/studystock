FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — BANKING & FINANCIAL SYSTEMS (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Tests knowledge of how banks and financial institutions operate within
the broader financial system: account and lending products, payment
systems, regulatory compliance, and the customer-facing financial
advisory skills bank employees use daily.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Financial System Structure and Institutions (15 test items)
1. Describe the structure and function of the Federal Reserve System (12 district banks, Board of Governors, FOMC).
2. Explain the Federal Reserve's monetary policy tools (open market operations, discount rate, reserve requirements).
3. Differentiate commercial banks, credit unions, savings and loan associations, and investment banks.
4. Describe the role of central banks in a nation's economy.
5. Explain fractional reserve banking and the money multiplier effect.
6. Identify the functions of the U.S. Treasury and its relationship to the banking system.
7. Describe how banks generate revenue (net interest margin, fees, interchange income).

B. Deposit Accounts and Banking Services (15 test items)
1. Differentiate checking, savings, money market, and certificate of deposit (CD) accounts.
2. Explain how compound interest is calculated on savings accounts (APY vs. nominal rate).
3. Describe common bank fees (overdraft, maintenance, ATM, wire transfer).
4. Identify services offered by retail banking versus private/wealth management divisions.
5. Explain the purpose and mechanics of safe deposit boxes and escrow accounts.
6. Describe the process and purpose of account reconciliation and reading a bank statement.
7. Explain the role of online and mobile banking platforms in service delivery.

C. Lending, Credit, and Interest (20 test items)
1. Calculate simple and compound interest on loans and deposits.
2. Differentiate APR and APY, and explain why they differ.
3. Explain how credit scores are calculated (payment history, utilization, length of history, new credit, credit mix).
4. Describe types of loans (secured vs. unsecured, installment vs. revolving, mortgage, auto, personal).
5. Explain amortization and how monthly loan payments are structured (principal vs. interest).
6. Describe the 5 Cs of credit (character, capacity, capital, collateral, conditions).
7. Explain collateral and its role in securing a loan.
8. Differentiate fixed-rate and adjustable-rate mortgages (ARMs).
9. Describe the consequences of default, delinquency, and foreclosure/repossession.

D. Payment Systems and Technology (15 test items)
1. Differentiate ACH transfers, wire transfers, and paper check payments.
2. Explain how debit and credit card transactions are processed (authorization, clearing, settlement).
3. Describe emerging digital payment methods (mobile wallets, P2P apps, contactless/NFC payments).
4. Explain the role of the Automated Clearing House (ACH) network.
5. Describe cryptocurrency and blockchain basics as they relate to financial systems.
6. Explain fraud-prevention measures in electronic payments (tokenization, encryption, two-factor authentication).
7. Describe the check-clearing process and the purpose of the Check 21 Act.

E. Banking Regulation and Compliance (15 test items)
1. Explain the purpose and coverage limits of FDIC deposit insurance.
2. Describe the Truth in Lending Act (Regulation Z) and its disclosure requirements.
3. Explain the purpose of the Truth in Savings Act.
4. Describe the role of the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB).
5. Explain anti-money laundering (AML) and Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements.
6. Describe the Community Reinvestment Act (CRA) and its purpose.
7. Explain the Bank Secrecy Act and the purpose of Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs).
8. Describe the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and fair lending practices.

F. Financial Statements and Analysis for Banking (10 test items)
1. Interpret a personal or business balance sheet (assets, liabilities, net worth).
2. Calculate and interpret ratios used in lending decisions (debt-to-income, loan-to-value).
3. Explain how banks assess creditworthiness using applicant financial statements.
4. Describe the components of a bank's own balance sheet (deposits as liabilities, loans as assets).
5. Interpret a cash flow statement for loan underwriting purposes.

G. Customer Financial Needs, Planning, and Ethics (10 test items)
1. Identify appropriate banking products for different customer life stages and needs.
2. Explain the basics of budgeting and financial goal-setting in a banking advisory context.
3. Describe ethical obligations of bankers (confidentiality, conflicts of interest, product suitability).
4. Explain identity theft prevention and consumer protection responsibilities in banking.
5. Describe financial literacy concepts bank employees should communicate to customers.
