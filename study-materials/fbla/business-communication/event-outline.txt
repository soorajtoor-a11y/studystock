FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — BUSINESS COMMUNICATION (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Effective workplace communication: written and verbal communication,
digital correspondence, and professional etiquette.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Communication Concepts
1. Describe the forms of communication.
2. Identify the five C's of communication.
3. Discuss the role of letters, memos, and reports in business.
4. Manage telephone communications; gather and record information.
5. Discuss communication techniques for internal and external customers.
6. Analyze the impact of cultural diversity on communication.
7. Employ strategies for dissatisfied customers (face-to-face, electronic, writing).
8. Discuss positive/negative aspects of the office grapevine and politics.
9. Discuss discrimination and sexual harassment implications in the workplace.
10. Use bias-free language.
11. Plan a meeting (facilities, speaker, notify participants).
12. Demonstrate negotiation and consensus-building; resolve conflicts.
13. Evaluate appropriate/inappropriate workplace interaction, including teams.
14. Define workplace relationships: supervisor, employee, client, team member.
15. Define upward, downward, and horizontal communication and org structures.
16. Investigate team-based work groups: members, roles, expectations.

B. Written and Report Applications
1. Produce organized paragraphs: topic sentence, transitions, conclusion.
2. Demonstrate the writing process: plan, write, edit, proofread, revise.
3. Write documents (memo, letter, report, email) with correct style/format for persuasive, positive, or negative correspondence.
4. Plan, compose, and produce instructions/directions and descriptions.
5. Conduct research using five steps: plan search, locate sources, organize, evaluate sources, prepare report.
6. Identify traditional and electronic research sources.
7. Interpret and evaluate information for relevance, purpose, timeliness, authenticity.
8. Document all sources using current standards.
9. Distinguish paraphrasing, documentation, and plagiarism.
10. Comprehend copyright laws for text, art, design, music, photography.
11. Use scanning hardware and layout/design/graphics software.
12. Compose common business reports: informational, news releases, proposals, policy statements.
13. Prepare presentation documents: publicity, agenda, handouts, follow-up report.

C. Reading Comprehension
1. Read and follow directions.
2. Demonstrate comprehension by restating or summarizing.
3. Differentiate between fact and opinion.
4. Summarize the important points of a document.
5. Explain enhancements: graphs, charts, tables, illustrations.
6. Interpret information from articles, manuals, etc.
7. Select the appropriate reading method (skimming, scanning, speed-reading, in-depth).
8. Identify factors affecting readability (sentence length, word choice, type size).
9. Investigate workplace reading skills: information, summarization, conclusions, judgments, directions.
10. Evaluate quality and reliability of source information.
11. Analyze information in tables, lists, and figures.
12. Use note-taking with critical listening and reading techniques.

D. Grammar
1. Describe and identify the eight parts of speech in context.
2. Identify the difference between adverbs and adjectives.
3. Identify pronoun categories and reflexive pronoun uses.
4. Identify prepositions and prepositional phrase uses.
5. Identify types of conjunctions.
6. Use subject-verb agreement correctly.
7. Describe verb types; demonstrate the six tenses.
8. Use irregular verbs properly; distinguish transitive/intransitive.
9. Identify subjects, predicates, verbs, adverbs, pronouns, objects, phrases.
10. Select and use pronouns correctly.
11. Correctly use possessive nouns.
12. Create plural forms of nouns.
13. Identify and correct misplaced and dangling modifiers.
14. Write the four sentence kinds: declarative, interrogative, imperative, exclamatory.
15. Recognize fragments, run-ons, and double negatives.
16. Correct problems in completeness, agreement, reference, form.
17. Write in varied patterns: simple, compound, complex, compound-complex.
18. Write coherent phrases/sentences/paragraphs with correct mechanics.

E. Editing and Proofreading
1. Proofread a paragraph; identify spelling, grammar, punctuation errors.
2. Proofread using proofreader's marks.
3. Edit documents to be clear, correct, concise, complete, consistent, courteous.
4. Review word choice and sentence structure effectiveness.

F. Verbal and Nonverbal Communication
1. Demonstrate active listening; identify barriers to listening.
2. Listen objectively; record major points of a speaker's message.
3. Explain verbal vs. nonverbal communication differences.
4. List how nonverbal messages differ across cultures.
5. Identify and interpret major types of verbal/nonverbal communication.
6. Demonstrate basic speaking skills.
7. Discuss delivery methods: verbal, written, multimedia, teleconferencing, interactive.
8. Design effective presentations with multimedia components.
9. Evaluate presentation types: informative, persuasive, debate.
10. Use proper oral presentation techniques.
11. Ask questions to clarify information.
12. Analyze language/custom barriers in business.
13. Describe a simple system/process; give clear directions.
14. Express opinions positively and tactfully.

G. Word Definition and Usage
1. Recognize how word selection affects communication.
2. Recognize slang, jargon, cliches, and common usage errors.
3. Use proper sentence structure.
4. Illustrate proper word division.
5. Identify homophones, synonyms, and homonyms.
6. Use a dictionary and thesaurus effectively.
7. Use contextual clues for word meaning.

H. Punctuation and Capitalization
1. Use periods, question marks, exclamation points appropriately.
2. Explain use of commas, colons, semicolons.
3. Correct punctuation problems (commas, semicolons, apostrophes).
4. Use apostrophes for contractions and possessives.
5. Use quotation marks and punctuation with them correctly.
6. Define rules for dash, hyphen, parentheses.
7. Use ellipses, italics, underlining appropriately.
8. Capitalize sentences, proper nouns, abbreviations, adjectives, titles.
9. Write numbers per standard practice.

I. Spelling
1. Spell words regularly used in business writing.
2. Use dictionary/thesaurus for spelling and pronunciation.
3. Identify prefixes and suffixes.
4. Apply rules for homonyms and confusing words (effect/affect).
5. Apply spelling rules (i before e, silent e, words ending in y).

J. Digital Communication
1. Identify electronic communication forms, including emerging technologies.
2. Select appropriate messaging technologies (voicemail, conference calls, chat, email).
3. Manage email: compose, send, retrieve, read, print, attachments.
4. Operate an email account with folders and address books.
5. Examine proper email/internet communication and business terminology.
6. Explain copyright laws across media.
7. Recognize legal implications in digital publishing.
8. Apply etiquette for electronic messaging.
9. Discuss keeping data secure from theft and destruction.
10. Identify new and emerging communication technologies.
