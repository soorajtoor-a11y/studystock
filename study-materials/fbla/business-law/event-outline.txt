FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — BUSINESS LAW (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The legal system and its impact on business: contracts, ethics,
employment law, and consumer protection.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Legal System
1. Identify ethical character traits: honesty, integrity, compassion, respect, responsibility, citizenship, justice.
2. Explain the relationship between law and ethics and why they sometimes conflict.
3. Explain differences between local, state, and federal government; powers and limitations of each.
4. Define statutory law; describe how a bill becomes a statute under federal law.
5. Identify how laws affect individuals, sources of law, constitutional rights/responsibilities.
6. Distinguish between different types of courts.
7. Distinguish roles of legal professionals (judges, lawyers, paralegals).
8. Identify elements of criminal, civil, and business law, including trial procedures.
9. Define crimes (embezzlement, larceny, robbery, burglary, assault, battery, forgery, white collar, extortion, bribery, conspiracy) and classify by punishment severity.
10. Explain the difference between crimes and torts.
11. Define "negligence per se" with examples.
12. Define torts: assault, battery, false imprisonment, mental distress, invasion of privacy, defamation, trespass, conversion, negligence, strict liability.
13. Identify crimes in the business environment.
14. Understand common legal documents and their uses.
15. Recognize situations that call for legal advice.
16. Know frequently used legal terms (litigation, arbitration, mediation, conciliation).
17. Explain how disputes can be settled outside the courts.
18. Complete legal documents with notarization as required.
19. Define environmental law and energy regulation/conservation.

B. Business Organization
1. Describe legal forms of business organization; advantages/disadvantages.
2. Describe organizational structures and management levels.
3. Examine how businesses are created: rights, limitations, liabilities.
4. Distinguish limited partnership vs. general partnership.
5. Differentiate corporation types; functions of board of directors and officers.
6. Identify shareholder rights; nature of shareholder liability.
7. Differentiate corporate expansion types (mergers, consolidations, conglomerates).
8. Describe franchises and their economic/legal framework.
9. Define LLC and the steps in forming one.
10. Discuss the SEC's role and consequences of violating SEC regulations.
11. Discuss how government regulation and legal issues affect everyone.
12. Understand business responsibility to abide by laws (anti-trust, organized labor, regulatory agencies).
13. Keep current with laws on financial/accounting records and record retention.
14. Identify legal issues and agencies in global business.
15. Identify trade regulations and unfair trade practices regulated by the FTC.

C. Contracts and Sales
1. Explain the nature and importance of contracts.
2. Identify contract elements: offer, acceptance, genuine agreement, consideration, capacity, legality.
3. Analyze steps to creating a legal, binding contract.
4. Discuss offer/acceptance requirements and how offers terminate/discharge/assign.
5. Identify contract classifications: valid, void, voidable, unenforceable, express, implied, oral, written.
6. Define consideration; list examples of valid consideration.
7. Differentiate contract disruptions: fraud, nondisclosure, misrepresentation, mistake, duress, undue influence.
8. Explain a minor's rights regarding contracts.
9. List contracts requiring writing under the Statute of Frauds; consequences of noncompliance.
10. Define breach of contract; name legal remedies.
11. Define sale; explain how the UCC governs sale of goods.
12. Distinguish payment, delivery, and transfer of title.
13. Explain who may transfer ownership of goods and requirements.
14. Define seller and buyer performance obligations.

D. Agency and Employment Law
1. Explain agency relationships and how they're created.
2. Distinguish agents from independent contractors, brokers, bailees, trustees.
3. Identify agents' duties, scope of authority, responsibilities, liabilities.
4. Identify legislation regulating employee rights (interviews, testing, minors, collective bargaining).
5. Identify legislation on employment conditions and benefits (OSHA, workers' comp, unemployment).
6. Identify legislation (civil rights, privacy, ADA) affecting personnel practices.
7. Describe legal reasons for termination (at-will, embezzlement, policy violations).
8. Analyze contracts for labor negotiations and compliance monitoring.
9. Discuss immigration's impact on business operations.

E. Consumer Protection, Product/Personal Liability
1. Identify legislation regulating consumer credit, debt collection, privacy, electronic transactions.
2. Describe consumer protection laws and agencies.
3. Define unfair/deceptive practices: bait and switch, usury, identity theft, deceptive estimates, fraud.
4. Identify regulatory agencies protecting against unsafe products and deceptive trade.
5. Explain strict, absolute, and vicarious liability.
6. Explain business ethics in product service management (packaging, QA, standards, promotion).
7. Apply truth-in-advertising laws to promotion.
8. Distinguish implied vs. express and full vs. limited warranties.

F. Negotiable Instruments, Insurance, Secured Transactions, Bankruptcy
1. Explain negotiable instruments: elements, types, advantages/disadvantages.
2. Distinguish primary vs. secondary parties.
3. Describe presentment for payment vs. acceptance.
4. Compare types of insurance.
5. Identify laws associated with insurance types.
6. Compare insurance policies and coverage.
7. Describe secured transactions and valid security interests.
8. Discuss mortgages, liens, and their purposes.
9. Explain rights of debtors and creditors.
10. Identify reasons for bankruptcy laws.
11. Compare bankruptcy with alternatives.
12. Describe bankruptcy features: liquidation, reorganization, Chapters 7, 11, 12, 13.

G. Property Laws
1. Discuss benefits and burdens of property ownership.
2. Define real property, personal property, fixtures; why distinctions matter.
3. Compare methods of acquiring property and transferring title (renting, leasing, purchasing, deed, title search, mortgage, liens).
4. Distinguish liens, licenses, and easements.
5. Describe legal aspects of real estate transactions.
6. Describe legal characteristics of a lease.
7. Define intellectual property types: patents, copyrights, trademarks.
8. Describe civil/criminal consequences of IP infringement.
9. Explain zoning regulations' impact on property use.
10. Explain bailments and bailees' standards of care.

H. Computer Law
1. Explain ethical/legal issues: hacking, software piracy, source code, licenses, copyright, internet, email.
2. Discuss e-commerce laws, jurisdiction, disputes.
3. Define computer crimes and federal/state statutes combating them.
4. Discuss statutes on computer records and privacy.

I. Domestic/Personal Law
1. Define marriage; rights and obligations involved.
2. Understand marriage concepts: age requirements, prenuptial agreements, common law, prohibited marriages.
3. Contrast annulment, divorce, dissolution; typical grounds for divorce.
4. Describe property distribution in divorce; child custody and support.
5. Describe legal rights/duties of minors and parents/guardians.
6. Explain estate planning protections (power of attorney, will, trusts, living will).
7. Identify valid will requirements; how wills are revoked/altered.
8. Identify responsibilities of personal representative/executor/administrator.
