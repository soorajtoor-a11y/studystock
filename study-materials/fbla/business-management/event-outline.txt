FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — BUSINESS MANAGEMENT (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Tests understanding of core management theory and practice: the
functions managers perform, how organizations are structured and led,
how people and operations are managed, and the strategic and ethical
judgment expected of business leaders.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Management Functions and Theory (20 test items)
1. Describe the four functions of management (planning, organizing, leading, controlling — POLC).
2. Differentiate strategic, tactical, and operational planning.
3. Explain Fayol's principles of management and Taylor's scientific management.
4. Describe the managerial roles identified by Mintzberg (interpersonal, informational, decisional).
5. Differentiate line, staff, and functional authority.
6. Explain span of control and its effect on organizational layers.
7. Describe the management skills required at different levels (technical, human, conceptual).

B. Organizational Structure and Design (15 test items)
1. Differentiate functional, divisional, matrix, and flat organizational structures.
2. Explain centralization versus decentralization of authority.
3. Describe the chain of command and unity of command principles.
4. Interpret an organizational chart to identify reporting relationships.
5. Explain formal versus informal organizational structures.
6. Describe how organizational structure affects communication flow.

C. Leadership and Motivation (15 test items)
1. Differentiate autocratic, democratic, and laissez-faire leadership styles.
2. Explain Maslow's hierarchy of needs as applied to employee motivation.
3. Describe Herzberg's two-factor theory (hygiene factors vs. motivators).
4. Explain Theory X and Theory Y assumptions about workers.
5. Describe transformational versus transactional leadership.
6. Explain the role of emotional intelligence in effective leadership.
7. Describe techniques for building employee engagement and morale.

D. Human Resource Management (15 test items)
1. Describe the stages of the employee lifecycle (recruiting, selecting, onboarding, developing, separating).
2. Explain methods of performance appraisal (360-degree feedback, management by objectives, rating scales).
3. Describe legal considerations in hiring (EEOC, ADA, anti-discrimination laws).
4. Differentiate types of employee compensation and benefits (salary, incentives, benefits packages).
5. Explain the purpose of job analysis, job descriptions, and job specifications.
6. Describe training and development methods (on-the-job, mentoring, e-learning).

E. Operations and Quality Management (15 test items)
1. Describe Total Quality Management (TQM) and continuous improvement (Kaizen).
2. Explain Six Sigma concepts (DMAIC process, defect reduction).
3. Describe inventory management concepts (just-in-time, economic order quantity).
4. Explain productivity measurement and how to calculate output per input.
5. Describe process mapping and workflow analysis.
6. Explain how supply chain management relates to day-to-day operations management.

F. Decision Making and Strategic Planning (10 test items)
1. Describe the steps in the rational decision-making model.
2. Explain SWOT analysis and its use in strategic planning.
3. Differentiate programmed and non-programmed decisions.
4. Describe brainstorming and other group decision-making techniques.
5. Explain the difference between a mission statement and a vision statement.

G. Business Ethics and Change Management (10 test items)
1. Describe corporate social responsibility (CSR) and its business rationale.
2. Explain common ethical dilemmas managers face (conflicts of interest, whistleblowing).
3. Describe Kotter's 8-step model for leading organizational change.
4. Explain sources of resistance to change and strategies to address them.
5. Describe the role of corporate culture in shaping employee behavior.
