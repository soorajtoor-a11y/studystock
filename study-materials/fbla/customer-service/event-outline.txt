FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — CUSTOMER SERVICE (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Tests the principles and skills behind delivering exceptional customer
service: communication technique, complaint and conflict resolution,
relationship management, service-quality measurement, and the role of
technology and culture in modern service delivery.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Customer Service Principles and Foundations (15 test items)
1. Define customer service and explain its impact on business success and brand loyalty.
2. Differentiate internal customers and external customers.
3. Describe the characteristics of exceptional customer service.
4. Explain the "moment of truth" concept in the customer service cycle.
5. Describe how first impressions shape customer perception of a business.
6. Identify traits and behaviors of effective customer service representatives.

B. Communication Skills (20 test items)
1. Describe active listening techniques (paraphrasing, clarifying questions, summarizing).
2. Explain the role of tone of voice and word choice in phone and email communication.
3. Differentiate verbal, nonverbal, and written communication in service settings.
4. Describe techniques for using open-ended versus closed-ended questions.
5. Explain empathy statements and their use in de-escalating tense interactions.
6. Describe common barriers to effective communication (jargon, distractions, assumptions).
7. Explain the importance of positive language framing (stating what can be done, not what can't).

C. Handling Complaints and Difficult Customers (20 test items)
1. Describe structured complaint-handling models (e.g., Listen, Apologize, Solve, Thank).
2. Explain techniques for de-escalating an angry or upset customer.
3. Identify common difficult-customer behavior patterns and appropriate responses.
4. Describe when and how to escalate an issue to a supervisor.
5. Explain the importance of taking ownership of a problem without over-promising a resolution.
6. Describe proper documentation practices for recording customer complaints.
7. Explain how to set realistic expectations when a resolution will take time.

D. Customer Relationship Management (CRM) (15 test items)
1. Explain the purpose and core functions of CRM software.
2. Describe how businesses use customer data to personalize service.
3. Explain the customer lifetime value (CLV) concept.
4. Describe segmentation strategies used to tailor service to different customer groups.
5. Explain how loyalty programs support customer retention.

E. Service Recovery and Quality Measurement (15 test items)
1. Describe the service recovery paradox — how a well-handled failure can increase loyalty.
2. Explain common customer satisfaction metrics (CSAT, NPS, CES).
3. Describe methods for collecting customer feedback (surveys, comment cards, social listening).
4. Explain how service level agreements (SLAs) define acceptable service standards.
5. Describe root cause analysis for recurring service failures.

F. Technology and Cross-Cultural Considerations in Service (15 test items)
1. Describe the role of chatbots, AI assistants, and self-service portals in modern customer service.
2. Explain omnichannel service and why customers expect consistency across channels.
3. Describe considerations for serving customers across different cultures (language, norms, expectations).
4. Explain accessibility considerations in customer service (ADA compliance, assistive technology).
5. Describe a representative's data privacy responsibilities when handling customer information.
