FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — CYBERSECURITY (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Protecting systems and data from digital threats (viruses, malware,
phishing, spyware): foundational principles, tools, and best practices.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Security Fundamentals (15 test items)
1. Describe Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA triad).
2. Describe measures for digital trust (identity proofing, non-repudiation, attestation).
3. Explain authentication, authorization, and accounting (AAA).
4. Provide examples of Zero Trust.
5. Describe deception/disruption technology (honeypots, honeynets, honeyfiles).
6. Explain how binary, hexadecimal, and decimal are used in cryptography.
7. Explain the principle of least privilege.

B. Cyber Threats and Vulnerabilities (20 test items)
1. Describe web/software vulnerability sources (injections, overflows, jailbreaking, race conditions).
2. Discuss threat actors and their goals (internal/external, financial gain, espionage, data theft).
3. Describe types of viruses.
4. Discuss security vulnerabilities (backdoors, zero-days, unpatched software).
5. Discuss social engineering scams (phishing, phone scams, email scams).
6. Describe the purpose, methods, and mechanics of DDoS attacks.
7. Describe malware types (viruses, Trojans, worms, logic bombs).
8. Describe cryptographic attacks (downgrades, collisions, birthday attacks).
9. Discuss vulnerabilities of wireless networks.

C. Security and Design (20 test items)
1. Explain how cloud infrastructure affects system security.
2. Discuss security implications of microservices (attack surfaces, authentication, complexity).
3. Differentiate logical vs. physical segmentation.
4. Explain how containerization and virtualization increase security.
5. Describe IoT security risks and challenges.
6. Describe backups, RAID, and UPS concepts.
7. Identify CIA triad examples in network design (UPS, encryption, data integrity).
8. Explain the role of testing in secure cyber architecture.

D. Network and Data Security (15 test items)
1. Describe the purpose of cryptography.
2. Differentiate public vs. private key cryptography.
3. Discuss shift, Caesar, and substitution ciphers.
4. Describe the three states of data (at rest, in transit, in use).
5. Describe access control models (MAC, DAC, RBAC).
6. Discuss authentication/authorization of network resources (multifactor, certificates, tokens).
7. Describe blockchains and hashing for authentication and data integrity.

E. Security Operations and Management (10 test items)
1. Describe common security policies (acceptable use, information security, business continuity, disaster recovery).
2. Discuss disaster prevention and recovery plan elements.
3. Describe firewall types (network-based, NGFW, WAF).
4. Explain firewall access lists and rules.
5. Describe best practices for messaging, email, and data security.
6. Describe the impact of change management on security.

F. Security Protocols and Threat Mitigation (20 test items)
1. Provide examples of secure protocols (SSH, HTTPS, TLS, WPA2).
2. Describe intrusion prevention and detection systems (IPS/IDS).
3. Describe policies preventing viruses, phishing, email scams.
4. Explain obfuscation methods (tokenization, data masking, steganography).
5. Describe how strong passwords increase security.
6. Describe digital certificates and Certificate Authorities (CAs).
7. Describe importance of patches, updates, version control.
8. Explain penetration testing for security.
