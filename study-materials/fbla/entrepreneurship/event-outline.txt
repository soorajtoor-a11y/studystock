FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — ENTREPRENEURSHIP (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Tests the knowledge needed to launch and grow a new venture: spotting
opportunities, building a business plan, choosing a legal structure,
raising startup capital, marketing on a limited budget, and managing
risk through growth or exit.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Opportunity Recognition and Idea Generation (10 test items)
1. Describe methods for identifying unmet market needs (customer pain points, trend analysis).
2. Explain the difference between an invention and an innovation.
3. Describe the lean startup methodology (build-measure-learn, minimum viable product).
4. Explain how to validate a business idea before committing resources (customer discovery interviews).
5. Describe characteristics of a successful entrepreneur (risk tolerance, resilience, opportunity recognition).

B. Business Plan Development (15 test items)
1. Identify the standard components of a business plan (executive summary, market analysis, operations plan, financial projections).
2. Explain the purpose of a value proposition and how to write one.
3. Describe the business model canvas and its key building blocks.
4. Explain how to conduct a competitive analysis.
5. Describe the purpose of break-even analysis in a business plan.
6. Explain how to develop realistic financial projections (sales forecast, cash flow projection).

C. Legal Structures and Intellectual Property (15 test items)
1. Differentiate sole proprietorship, partnership, LLC, and corporation.
2. Describe the liability, taxation, and control differences among business structures.
3. Explain the purpose of patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets.
4. Describe the process and value of registering a business name or trademark.
5. Explain licensing agreements and franchising as business structures.
6. Describe common contracts entrepreneurs use (NDAs, operating agreements, vendor contracts).

D. Financing a Startup (20 test items)
1. Differentiate equity financing and debt financing.
2. Describe bootstrapping and its advantages and disadvantages.
3. Explain how angel investors and venture capitalists evaluate startups.
4. Describe crowdfunding models (reward-based, equity-based, donation-based).
5. Explain the purpose of a pitch deck and what investors look for in one.
6. Describe SBA loans and other small-business lending options.
7. Explain equity dilution and startup valuation (pre-money vs. post-money).
8. Describe the use of a capitalization (cap) table to track ownership.

E. Marketing for Startups (15 test items)
1. Explain guerrilla marketing and other low-cost marketing tactics for new businesses.
2. Describe how to identify and define a target market or customer persona.
3. Explain the role of branding in differentiating a new venture.
4. Describe digital marketing channels available to startups (social media, SEO, email).
5. Explain customer acquisition cost (CAC) and its relationship to customer lifetime value (LTV).

F. Risk Management and Operations (15 test items)
1. Identify common risks new ventures face (market, financial, operational, legal).
2. Describe strategies for mitigating startup risk (diversification, insurance, contracts).
3. Explain the importance of cash flow management to early-stage survival.
4. Describe basic supply chain and vendor relationship considerations for a new business.
5. Explain the purpose of common business insurance types (general liability, product liability, property).

G. Growth, Scaling, and Exit Strategies (10 test items)
1. Describe strategies for scaling a business (franchising, licensing, geographic expansion).
2. Explain common exit strategies (acquisition, IPO, merger, succession).
3. Describe the challenges of managing rapid growth (hiring, systems, culture).
4. Explain how to recognize when and how to pivot a business model.
