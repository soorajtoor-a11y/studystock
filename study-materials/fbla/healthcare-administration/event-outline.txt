FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — HEALTHCARE ADMINISTRATION (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Medical terminology, office procedures, and administrative functions
in a healthcare setting.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Managing Office Procedures
1. Determine commonly used documents composed in a medical office.
2. List procedures to maintain a healthcare provider's schedule.
3. Demonstrate procedures to schedule patient appointments.
4. Identify procedures to register patients.
5. Identify the parts of a patient chart.
6. State HIPAA patient confidentiality rules.
7. Identify procedures to sort and process mail.
8. Identify emergency-handling procedures in medical offices.
9. Prepare treatment rooms and exam tables.
10. Determine procedures to clean instruments, equipment, work areas.
11. Determine maintenance requirements for supplies and equipment.
12. Identify procedures to maintain, stock, and inventory supplies/equipment.
13. Identify unsafe conditions.
14. Coordinate meetings, events, and activities.
15. Select effective time management techniques.
16. Identify resources needed to complete a job assignment.

B. Medical Terminology
1. Define medical root words.
2. Define medical suffixes.
3. Define medical prefixes.
4. Define common medical abbreviations.
5. Communicate information using medical terms.
6. Differentiate between medical specialties.
7. Use anatomical terminology for body parts and functions.

C. Legal & Ethical Issues in Healthcare
1. State healthcare facility policies and procedures.
2. Identify national/state/local workplace safety standards.
3. Determine laws governing healthcare professionals.
4. State the purpose of a patient's Bill of Rights.
5. Explain employment and labor laws/standards.
6. Explain the purpose of Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS).
7. Explain harassment laws.
8. Determine licensure/certification for healthcare professionals.
9. Identify HIPAA standards.
10. Maintain patient confidentiality.
11. Identify threats to patient confidentiality.
12. Define expressed, implied, and informed consent.
13. Identify practices resulting in malpractice, liability, negligence.
14. Identify ethical issues in healthcare.
15. Identify appropriate work habits and ethics.

D. Communication Skills
1. Describe communication forms common to healthcare.
2. Discuss letters, memos, and reports in healthcare business.
3. Manage telephone communications; gather and record information.
4. Analyze cultural diversity's impact on communication.
5. Employ strategies for dissatisfied customers.
6. Identify techniques for patients'/families' emotional needs (supportive listening).
7. Assist practitioner in communication management.
8. Identify appropriate responses to difficult patient behavior.
9. List purposeful activities for cognitive skills.
10. Analyze effective communication in interdisciplinary teams.

E. Managing Financial Functions
1. Identify procedures for payment on accounts.
2. Complete a balance sheet.
3. List procedures to bill patients.
4. Define procedures for past-due accounts.
5. Make a bank deposit.
6. Open a new patient account.
7. Reconcile a bank statement.
8. Process an accounts payable invoice.
9. Maintain a petty cash fund.
10. Identify common healthcare financial forms.

F. Health Insurance
1. List types of health insurance coverage (HMO, PPO, Medicare, etc.).
2. Identify procedures for insurance authorization for treatment/testing.
3. Identify commonly used insurance forms and statements.
4. List common ICD-11 codes for billing and claims.
5. Identify procedures for processing referrals.
6. Prepare a billing statement.
7. Interpret an Explanation of Benefits (EOB) form.
8. Identify procedures for submitting insurance claims.

G. Records Management
1. Identify components of the medical record.
2. Determine procedures to code medical records.
3. Identify manual and electronic filing procedures.
4. Identify the phases of the record life cycle.
5. List medical record charge-out procedures.
6. Manage electronic file storage (file and disk management).

H. Infection Control
1. List aseptic hand washing procedures.
2. Identify infection control techniques preventing disease transmission.
3. Describe OSHA and CDC standards for infection control.
4. Identify bio-hazardous material disposal procedures.
5. Detail handling of clean and soiled linens/clothing.
6. Identify standard precautions (gown, mask, gloves, cap, eyewear).
7. Detail cleaning, sterilizing, preparing instruments and supplies.
8. List factors promoting/inhibiting microorganism growth.
9. Identify how the body protects against microorganisms and infection.

I. Medical History
1. Identify common elements of a patient's medical history.
2. List procedures to record and report vital signs.
3. Interpret readings on metering devices.
4. List patient responses during testing/treatment.
5. Record/file patient data and lab results (manual and electronic).
6. Answer patient inquiries from medical staff using correct terminology.
7. Carry out plan of care/orders.
8. Identify charting techniques.
9. Identify and use correct chart forms.

J. Technology
1. Define basic computer terminology.
2. Identify healthcare software applications.
3. Explain hardware purpose, operation, and care.
4. Identify diagnostic/troubleshooting for PCs, laptops, portables.
5. Apply basic commands; navigate operating systems.
6. Input data via peripherals (keyboard, touchscreen, scanner, voice).
7. Identify search keywords and search engine navigation.
8. Identify browser window parts.
9. Analyze emerging healthcare technologies.
10. Troubleshoot printers and scanners.
11. Use help resources (help menu, manuals, websites).
