FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — HOSPITALITY & EVENT MANAGEMENT (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Tests knowledge of the hospitality and event industries: how lodging,
food service, and tourism operations run day to day, how events are
planned and executed from concept to post-event evaluation, and how
guest experience, revenue, safety, and marketing are managed together.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Hospitality Industry Sectors and Trends (15 test items)
1. Differentiate the major hospitality sectors (lodging, food service, tourism, event/meeting industry).
2. Describe types of lodging establishments (full-service hotels, limited-service, resorts, boutique).
3. Explain the role of the tourism industry in local and global economies.
4. Describe current trends affecting hospitality (sustainability, experiential travel, technology adoption).
5. Explain star/diamond rating systems used to classify lodging quality.

B. Event Planning Process (20 test items)
1. Describe the stages of event planning (needs assessment, design, logistics, execution, evaluation).
2. Explain site selection criteria (capacity, location, accessibility, cost).
3. Describe how to develop and manage an event budget.
4. Explain the purpose of a banquet event order (BEO) and a run-of-show document.
5. Describe vendor and contract management for events (caterers, AV, décor).
6. Explain timeline and logistics coordination for multi-day events.
7. Describe contingency planning for event-day issues (weather, no-shows, technical failure).
8. Explain post-event evaluation methods (surveys, ROI analysis, debrief).

C. Guest and Customer Experience in Hospitality (15 test items)
1. Describe the concept of service culture in hospitality organizations.
2. Explain techniques for personalizing the guest experience.
3. Describe the guest cycle (pre-arrival, arrival, occupancy, departure) in lodging operations.
4. Explain how to handle guest complaints and perform service recovery in a hospitality setting.
5. Describe the role of amenities and ancillary services in guest satisfaction.

D. Event Marketing and Promotion (15 test items)
1. Describe strategies for promoting an event (social media, email, partnerships, press).
2. Explain the use of sponsorships in event financing and promotion.
3. Describe registration and ticketing considerations for events.
4. Explain the importance of branding consistency across event marketing materials.
5. Describe how to measure event marketing effectiveness (attendance, engagement, conversion).

E. Revenue Management and Finance (15 test items)
1. Explain revenue management concepts (dynamic pricing, yield management, occupancy rate).
2. Describe how average daily rate (ADR) and revenue per available room (RevPAR) are calculated.
3. Explain cost controls used in food and beverage operations.
4. Describe pricing strategies for event packages (per-person pricing, tiered packages).
5. Explain break-even analysis for an event or hospitality venue.

F. Food Safety and Service (10 test items)
1. Describe foodborne illness prevention practices (temperature control, cross-contamination).
2. Explain HACCP (Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points) principles.
3. Describe proper food storage and handling procedures in banquet and catering settings.
4. Explain responsible alcohol service practices and relevant regulations.

G. Risk Management for Events (10 test items)
1. Identify common event risks (safety, liability, weather, security).
2. Describe the purpose of event insurance and liability waivers.
3. Explain crowd management and emergency evacuation planning.
4. Describe ADA accessibility requirements for events and venues.
