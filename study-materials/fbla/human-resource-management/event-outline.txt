FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — HUMAN RESOURCE MANAGEMENT (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Key HR functions: staffing, training, employee relations, and
performance management.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Staff
1. Explain the importance of employee confidentiality.
2. Define transitional employees and strategies for their needs.
3. Explain hiring planning techniques (forecasting, succession planning).
4. Describe the stages of job analysis.
5. Explain the role of labor market statistics in recruitment.
6. Define effective recruitment strategies.
7. Define team recruiting.
8. Describe the employee selection process.
9. Describe testing, interviewing, and legal issues in selection.
10. Explain validity and importance of reference checks.
11. Describe effective interview questions.
12. Define contingency factors of job offers (background checks, physicals).
13. Define outsourcing and its impact on personnel.
14. Define Affirmative Action's role in selection.
15. Explain Equal Employment Opportunity legislation's impact.
16. Describe strategies to attract and keep top talent.
17. Explain factors in employee job satisfaction.

B. Training and Development
1. Explain training's importance for competitive advantage.
2. Define the importance of orientation and training.
3. Describe planning/implementing an employee training program.
4. Determine organization and employee needs for training.
5. Define needs analysis and its relationship to hiring.
6. Determine trainee problems and develop solutions.
7. Develop strategies to reduce employee/trainee conflict.
8. Recognize different learning styles in training.
9. Set observable, measurable, attainable, specific objectives.
10. Explain internal/external sources' impact on curriculum.
11. Explain technology and visual aids in training plans.
12. Administer trainee-focused training delivery.
13. Define strategies to evaluate and improve training delivery.
14. Explain the latest training and development trends.
15. Explain long-term benefits of professional development.
16. Identify online training resources.
17. Define HR safety and security programs compliant with law.
18. Describe evaluation processes for training programs.

C. Employee Compensation and Benefits
1. Define legal aspects of compensation and payroll deductions.
2. List legally required benefits.
3. Describe discretionary fringe benefits.
4. Define fair compensation perception and its productivity impact.
5. Explain rising healthcare costs and compensation expectations.
6. Calculate dollar, average, and percentage labor costs.
7. Calculate dollar, average, and percentage benefit costs.
8. Explain how labor costs affect break-even point.
9. Explain a salary schedule.
10. Explain variable pay based on performance.
11. Define types of employer-provided benefits.
12. Explain broad banding, strategic compensation, comparable worth.
13. Define piecework plan, merit pay, employee reward programs.
14. Describe how pay rates are calculated.
15. Explain team-based compensation plans.
16. Define social security, unemployment compensation, workers' comp.

D. Performance Management
1. Define coaching, counseling, and discipline.
2. Explain the importance of active listening.
3. Describe how influence increases performance.
4. Explain the importance of employer/employee feedback.
5. List the steps in the counseling process.
6. Describe counseling methods and techniques.
7. Explain deficiency in knowledge vs. deficiency in execution.
8. Explain how performance problems relate to personal problems.
9. Explain knowledge deficiency's relation to execution deficiency.
10. Explain the importance of employee agreement to change.
11. Identify performance management system purposes/components.
12. Explain the steps in the appraisal process.
13. Explain appraisal system advantages/disadvantages.
14. Differentiate performance appraisal types and purposes.
15. Describe strategies improving morale, motivation, productivity.
16. Describe behavior requiring disciplinary action.
17. Explain disciplinary techniques.
18. Describe legal issues in employee discipline.
19. Describe best HR practices for positive financial results.
20. Define effective performance management systems.

E. Government Relations and Issues
1. Describe personnel laws.
2. Differentiate equal employment vs. Equal Employment Opportunity Act.
3. Define harassment, hostile environment, discrimination, reverse discrimination.
4. Explain IRCA employability verification and noncompliance penalties.
5. Explain Affirmative Action.
6. Describe an HR Code of Ethics.
7. Define employee rights and responsibilities.
8. Describe habitual employee handicaps (tobacco, drugs, alcohol).
9. Differentiate exempt vs. non-exempt employees.
10. Define an independent contractor.
11. Define defamation, disclosure, negligent hiring.
12. Explain the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA).
13. Explain OSHA requirements.
14. Explain ADEA and its impact on early retirement.
15. Explain FMLA, Worker Adjustment and Retraining Act, Older Worker Benefit Protection Act, Retirement Security Act.
16. Define the ADA and employer accommodations.
17. Define work-related injuries under Workers' Compensation.
18. Define the National Labor Relations Act's role.
19. Explain workplace violence and security programs.
20. Define the Privacy Act of 1974 and its HR impact.
21. Explain the Drug Free Workplace Act of 1988.

F. Human Resource Planning
1. Define human resource planning.
2. Explain HR's role in comprehensive planning.
3. Describe HR goals and policies.
4. Explain the importance of teams at work.
5. Recognize the role of diversity in an organization.
6. Evaluate and update HR policies.
7. Identify HR challenges and remedies.
8. Identify trends: work from home, flexible scheduling, job sharing.
9. Explain HR's role in maintaining a code of ethics.
10. Discuss how technology, diversity, skills, world issues impact HR.

G. Labor Relations and Collective Bargaining
1. Develop a timeline of management-labor relations history.
2. Compare adversarial vs. cooperative labor relations.
3. Describe labor legislation (Wagner Act, Taft-Hartley Act).
4. Describe case law prohibiting acts by organized labor.
5. Describe case law prohibiting acts by management.
6. List the steps of organizing a labor union.
7. Explain the employee grievance process.
8. Describe negotiation and binding arbitration.
9. Explain the National Labor Relations Board's role.
10. List the steps of collective bargaining.
11. Define impasse and labor strike.
12. Define unions, mediation, arbitration.
13. Explain management's goal in labor negotiations.
14. List goals, structure, and management of labor unions.
