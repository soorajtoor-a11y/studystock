FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — INSURANCE & RISK MANAGEMENT (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Risk management principles and types of insurance: how individuals and
businesses identify, assess, and mitigate risks.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Risk Management
1. Understand the risk management model (identify hazards, assess/prioritize risks, choose controls, implement, monitor/review).
2. Understand Enterprise Risk Management (ERM): state goal, identify/evaluate/treat risks, monitor, communicate results.
3. Adapt risk management models to an organization's needs.
4. Understand how risk creates value.
5. Know the process of creating a risk program.
6. Create valid risk forecasts.
7. Perform risk mapping and risk profiling.
8. Determine the cost of risk.
9. Apply risk control theories: prevention, reduction, enablement, enhancement tactics.
10. Create emergency response and business continuity plans.
11. Understand risk retention plans and risk financing transfers.
12. Understand risk management reports.
13. Understand legal components of the risk management industry.
14. Recommend insurance for risks commonly faced by young adults.

B. Property and Liability Insurance
1. Differentiate main types of auto insurance coverage.
2. List factors that increase or reduce auto insurance premiums.
3. Determine legal minimum and optimal auto coverage in one's state.
4. Calculate claim payments after exclusions and deductibles.
5. Compare auto insurance costs across deductibles and liability limits.
6. Explain renter's insurance benefits.
7. Understand workers' compensation benefits and how they're paid.

C. Health, Disability, and Life Insurance
1. Analyze when people need health, disability, and life insurance.
2. Identify government programs (incl. Social Security) assisting with income loss from illness, disability, premature death.
3. Compare sources of health and disability insurance.
4. Explain the purpose of long-term care insurance.
5. Create a basic financial plan; explain benefits and how it changes over time.

D. Insurance Knowledge
1. Recognize basic policy types and features.
2. Determine how companies underwrite life insurance.
3. Define term insurance and permanent insurance.
4. Understand contract provisions and legal doctrines.
5. Understand claims management.
6. Create portfolios and place insurance coverage.
7. Know global insurance markets.
8. Understand pricing, risk aversion, and regulation.
9. Negotiate, write, and renew insurance contracts.
10. Define compliance regulations; demonstrate adherence.
11. Explain legal concepts in the insurance industry.
12. Describe insurance products and their benefits.
13. Discuss the nature of insurance fraud.
14. Investigate suspected insurance fraud.
15. Process a claim fulfilling legal obligations to clients.
16. Ensure compliance with EPA, OSHA, NFPA, ERISA, COBRA.
17. Discuss the insurance business cycle (agent, underwriter, claims, settlement).

E. Decision Making
1. Analyze problems and make decisions in global environments.
2. Use benchmarking to compare data.
3. Understand optimizing risk-taking decisions (ERM).
4. Analyze client risk for insurance approval/denial decisions.
5. Demonstrate ethical decisions via fiduciary duties of care.

F. Ethics
1. Understand confidentiality agreements.
2. Implement data security for confidential records.
3. Recognize state/federal privacy and disclosure regulations.

G. Careers
1. Identify insurance industry jobs.
2. Recognize industry organizations.
3. Describe knowledge/skills needed for insurance employment.
4. Describe roles: underwriter, sales representative, actuary, claims personnel, loss control specialist.
5. Describe insurance licensing and certification programs.
