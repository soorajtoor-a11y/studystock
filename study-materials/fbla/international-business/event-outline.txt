FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — INTERNATIONAL BUSINESS (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Tests knowledge of how businesses operate across national borders: the
economic theory behind global trade, the barriers and agreements that
shape it, foreign exchange and finance, cultural adaptation, market
entry strategy, global supply chains, and international business law
and ethics.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Global Trade Theory and Economics (15 test items)
1. Explain the theory of comparative advantage and how it drives international trade.
2. Differentiate absolute advantage and comparative advantage.
3. Describe balance of trade and balance of payments concepts.
4. Explain how exchange rates affect a country's exports and imports.
5. Describe globalization and its effects on national economies.

B. Trade Barriers and Agreements (20 test items)
1. Differentiate tariffs, quotas, embargoes, and subsidies as trade barriers.
2. Describe the purpose and function of the World Trade Organization (WTO).
3. Explain the key provisions and impact of the USMCA (successor to NAFTA).
4. Describe regional trade blocs (EU, ASEAN, Mercosur) and their purposes.
5. Explain the difference between free trade and protectionism.
6. Describe non-tariff barriers (product standards, licensing requirements, import quotas).
7. Explain the purpose of free trade zones and export processing zones.

C. Foreign Exchange and International Finance (15 test items)
1. Explain how foreign exchange rates are determined (floating vs. fixed exchange rate systems).
2. Describe how currency appreciation or depreciation affects import/export competitiveness.
3. Explain hedging strategies against currency risk (forward contracts, currency options).
4. Describe the role of the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and World Bank.
5. Explain letters of credit and their use in international transactions.

D. Cultural Considerations in Global Business (15 test items)
1. Describe Hofstede's cultural dimensions (individualism/collectivism, power distance, uncertainty avoidance).
2. Explain high-context versus low-context communication cultures.
3. Describe business etiquette considerations when negotiating internationally (greetings, gift-giving, time orientation).
4. Explain how language and translation issues can affect international business communication.
5. Describe strategies for adapting products and marketing to local cultures (localization vs. standardization).

E. Modes of Entry into Foreign Markets (15 test items)
1. Differentiate exporting, licensing, franchising, joint ventures, and foreign direct investment (FDI).
2. Explain the risk and control trade-offs among different market entry modes.
3. Describe strategic alliances and their advantages in entering new markets.
4. Explain wholly owned subsidiaries as a market entry strategy.
5. Describe factors a company weighs when choosing a market entry mode (cost, control, risk).

F. Global Supply Chain (10 test items)
1. Describe the basics of global sourcing and offshoring.
2. Explain how Incoterms define responsibilities in international shipping.
3. Describe risks in global supply chains (political instability, currency fluctuation, logistics disruption).
4. Explain the role of freight forwarders and customs brokers.

G. International Business Ethics and Law (10 test items)
1. Describe the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and its prohibitions on bribery.
2. Explain intellectual property protection challenges in international markets.
3. Describe corporate social responsibility considerations in global operations (labor standards, environmental impact).
4. Explain the role of international arbitration in resolving cross-border business disputes.
