FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — INTRODUCTION TO BUSINESS COMMUNICATION (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Eligibility: 9th & 10th graders only.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Fundamental business communication skills: written and verbal
communication, digital messaging, and professional etiquette.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Grammar
1. Describe and identify the eight parts of speech in context.
2. Identify the difference between adverbs and adjectives.
3. Identify pronoun categories and reflexive pronoun uses.
4. Identify prepositions and prepositional phrase uses.
5. Identify types of conjunctions.
6. Use subject-verb agreement correctly.
7. Describe verb types; demonstrate the six tenses.
8. Use irregular verbs properly; distinguish transitive/intransitive.
9. Identify subjects, predicates, verbs, adverbs, pronouns, objects, phrases.
10. Select and use pronouns correctly.
11. Correctly use possessive nouns.
12. Create plural forms of nouns.
13. Identify and correct misplaced and dangling modifiers.
14. Write the four sentence kinds: declarative, interrogative, imperative, exclamatory.
15. Recognize fragments, run-ons, double negatives.
16. Correct problems in completeness, agreement, reference, form.
17. Write in varied sentence patterns (simple, compound, complex, compound-complex).
18. Write coherent phrases/sentences/paragraphs with correct mechanics.

B. Punctuation and Capitalization
1. Use periods, question marks, exclamation points appropriately.
2. Explain use of commas, colons, semicolons.
3. Correct punctuation problems.
4. Use apostrophes for contractions and possessives.
5. Use quotation marks correctly, including punctuation with them.
6. Define rules for dash, hyphen, parentheses.
7. Use ellipses, italics, underlining appropriately.
8. Capitalize sentences, proper nouns, abbreviations, adjectives, titles.
9. Write numbers per standard practice.

C. Spelling
1. Correctly spell words regularly used in writing.
2. Use a dictionary and thesaurus effectively.
3. Identify prefixes and suffixes.
4. Explain plural spelling rules.
5. Apply rules for homonyms and confusing words (effect/affect).
6. Find silent letters in a word.
7. Recognize letter patterns in words.
8. Apply spelling rules (i before e, silent e, words ending in y).

D. Proofreading & Editing
1. Proofread a paragraph; identify spelling, grammar, punctuation errors.
2. Proofread using proofreader's marks.
3. Compare drafts to final documents; make editorial changes.
4. Edit documents to be clear, correct, concise, complete, consistent, courteous.
5. Review sentence structure and writing style.
6. Review and edit word choice effectiveness.

E. Word Definition and Usage
1. Deduce meanings of words and idiomatic phrases.
2. Recognize how word selection affects communication.
3. Recognize slang, jargon, and cliches.
4. Recognize common errors in word usage.
5. Use proper sentence structure.
6. Illustrate proper word division.
7. Identify homophones.
8. Create new words using root words.
9. Use dictionary and thesaurus as aids.
10. Use contextual clues for word meaning.

F. Oral Communication Concepts
1. Use bias-free language.
2. Illustrate sensitivity to audience needs.
3. Differentiate pronunciation vs. enunciation.
4. Correctly choose and pronounce words in verbal communication.
5. Describe a simple system/process; give clear directions.
6. Use proper oral presentation techniques.
7. Express opinions positively and tactfully.
8. Identify listening barriers and active listening techniques.
9. Ask questions to clarify information.
10. Interpret nonverbal cues in messages.
11. Follow spoken directions.
12. Listen objectively; record major points of a speaker's message.

G. Reading Comprehension
1. Read and follow directions.
2. Demonstrate comprehension by restating or summarizing.
3. Differentiate between fact and opinion.
4. Determine if text is descriptive, informative, instructional, or persuasive.
5. Summarize the important points of a document.
6. Explain enhancements: graphs, charts, tables, illustrations.
7. Interpret information from articles, manuals, etc.
8. Select the appropriate reading method (skimming, scanning, speed-reading, in-depth).
9. Identify factors affecting readability.
10. Investigate workplace reading skills.
11. Evaluate quality and reliability of sources.
12. Analyze information in tables, lists, figures.
13. Use note-taking with critical listening/reading techniques.
