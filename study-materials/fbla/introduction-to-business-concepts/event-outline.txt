FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — INTRODUCTION TO BUSINESS CONCEPTS (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Eligibility: 9th & 10th graders only.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Foundational business principles: management, marketing, finance,
and operations — a broad understanding of how businesses operate.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Fundamental Economic Concepts (10 test items)
1. Distinguish between economic goods and services.
2. Explain the concept of economic resources.
3. Describe the concepts of economics and economic activities.
4. Explain the principles of supply and demand.

B. Nature of Business (20 test items)
1. Explain the role of business in society.
2. Describe types of business models.
3. Explain the organizational design of businesses.
4. Discuss the global environment in which businesses operate.
5. Describe factors that affect the business environment.
6. Explain the nature of business ethics.
7. Explain how organizations adapt to today's markets.
8. Determine the relationship between government and business.

C. Economic Systems (15 test items)
1. Explain the types of economic systems.
2. Identify the impact of small business/entrepreneurship on market economies.
3. Explain the concept of private enterprise.
4. Identify factors affecting a business's profit.
5. Explain the concept of competition.

D. Cost/Profit Relationships (5 test items)
1. Explain the concept of productivity.
2. Analyze impact of specialization/division of labor on productivity.

E. Owning a Business (5 test items)
1. Explain types of business ownership.
2. Describe the nature of entrepreneurship.

F. Types of Business Activities (25 test items)
1. Describe types of business activities.
2. Explain the nature of positive customer relations.
3. Explain the concept of accounting.
4. Explain the role of finance in business.
5. Discuss the nature of human resources management.
6. Explain marketing and its importance in a global economy.
7. Discuss the nature of information management.
8. Explain the nature of operations.
9. Explain the concept of production.
10. Explain the concept of management.

G. Career Planning (10 test items)
1. Assess personal interests and skills needed for success in business.
2. Analyze employer expectations in the business environment.
3. Explain employment opportunities in business.

H. Job-Search Skills (10 test items)
1. Complete a job application.
2. Interview for a job.
3. Write a cover letter.
4. Prepare a resume.

OFFICIAL REFERENCE RESOURCES (from FBLA guidelines)
