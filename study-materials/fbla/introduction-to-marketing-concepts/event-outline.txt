FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — INTRODUCTION TO MARKETING CONCEPTS (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Eligibility: 9th & 10th graders only.
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Foundational marketing principles: product promotion, pricing
strategies, consumer behavior, and selling goods and services.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Marketing Fundamentals (20 test items)
1. Describe marketing functions and related activities.
2. Explain marketing and its importance in a global economy.
3. Explain the concept of market and market identification.
4. Explain the concept of marketing strategies.
5. Explain employment opportunities in marketing.
6. Explain factors influencing customer/client/business buying behavior.
7. Explain the nature of marketing planning.
8. Explain the nature of marketing plans.

B. Product/Service Management (20 test items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of product/service management.
2. Identify the impact of product life cycles on marketing decisions.
3. Describe the uses of grades and standards in marketing.
4. Explain warranties and guarantees.
5. Explain the concept of the product mix.
6. Describe factors used to position products/services.
7. Explain the nature of product/service branding.

C. Channel Management (10 test items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of channel management.
2. Explain the nature of channels of distribution.
3. Describe the use of technology in channel management.

D. Marketing-Information Management (10 test items)
1. Describe the need for marketing data.
2. Identify data monitored for marketing decision making.
3. Explain the nature and scope of marketing-information management.
4. Explain the nature of marketing research.

E. Pricing (5 test items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of the pricing function.
2. Explain factors affecting pricing decisions.

F. Promotion (15 test items)
1. Explain the role of promotion as a marketing function.
2. Explain the types of promotion (institutional, product).
3. Identify the elements of the promotional mix.
4. Explain types of advertising media.

G. Selling (20 test items)
1. Explain the nature and scope of the selling function.
2. Explain customer service as a component of selling relationships.
3. Acquire product information for use in selling.
4. Explain the selling process.
5. Explain company selling policies.
6. Analyze product information to identify features and benefits.

OFFICIAL REFERENCE RESOURCES (from FBLA guidelines)
