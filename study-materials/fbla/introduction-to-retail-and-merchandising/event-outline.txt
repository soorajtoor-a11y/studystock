FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — INTRODUCTION TO RETAIL & MERCHANDISING (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Aug 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Eligibility: 9th & 10th graders only.
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Basic retail operations and merchandising strategies: product
placement, inventory control, customer experience, sales techniques.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Foundational Retail Knowledge (15 test items)
1. Explain the evolution of retailing.
2. Describe the role of merchandisers in retailing.
3. Distinguish between retailing and marketing.
4. Explain the importance of merchandising to retailers.

B. Customer Service and Sales (25 test items)
1. Establish relationship with customer/client.
2. Determine customer/client needs.
3. Recommend specific products.
4. Prepare cash drawers/banks.
5. Open/close register/terminal.
6. Operate register/terminal.
7. Process sales transactions (cash, credit, check).
8. Process returns/exchanges.

C. Stock-Handling Procedures (20 test items)
1. Explain the receiving process.
2. Explain stock-handling techniques for receiving deliveries.
3. Process incoming merchandise.
4. Price mark merchandise.
5. Route stock to sales floor.
6. Rotate stock.
7. Explain policies/procedures for handling shoplifters.

D. Inventory Storage and Control (20 test items)
1. Explain storing considerations.
2. Explain the nature of warehousing.
3. Store inventory.
4. Maintain inventory levels.
5. Report out-of-stocks.
6. Complete inventory counts.
7. Describe inventory control systems.

E. Career Opportunities (5 test items)
1. Identify career opportunities in retailing.
2. Explain career opportunities in merchandising.

F. Visual Merchandising (5 test items)
1. Explain the use of visual merchandising in retailing.
2. Distinguish between visual merchandising and display.

G. Display Techniques (10 test items)
1. Explain types of display arrangements.
2. Maintain displays.
3. Dismantle/store displays, display fixtures, forms.

OFFICIAL REFERENCE RESOURCES (from FBLA guidelines)
- MBA Research National Business Administration Standards (not required)
- Brightpearl, CGS, and Indeed retail merchandising guides
