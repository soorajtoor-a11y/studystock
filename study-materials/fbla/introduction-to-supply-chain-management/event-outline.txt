FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — INTRODUCTION TO SUPPLY CHAIN MANAGEMENT (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Eligibility: 9th & 10th graders only.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The flow of goods, information, and finances in a supply chain:
logistics, inventory control, procurement, and distribution strategies.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Essential Supply Chain Management Concepts
1. Define supply chain management (SCM).
2. Define the entire supply chain.
3. Define logistics.
4. Explain how logistics impacts SCM.
5. Describe how e-commerce, logistics, import taxes, risk, tariffs, customs, and legal aspects impact global trading.
6. Analyze creation of new value for customers, society, environment.
7. Explain SCM practice across industries.
8. Explain demand forecasting.
9. Define logistics management.
10. Define physical and information flows.
11. Define strategic orientation for saving money.
12. Describe the global impact on SCM.
13. Explain importing, exporting, trade agreements, customs regulations.
14. Explain reasons for SCM growth (information revolution, global competition, relationship management).

B. Supply Chain Topics
1. Define analytics.
2. Explain customer relationship management.
3. Describe the role of forecasting.
4. Explain the growth of global distribution.
5. Explain SCM for the healthcare industry.
6. Explain the logistics of inventory management.
7. Define procurement.
8. Describe sales and operations planning.
9. Define supply market intelligence.
10. Define sustainability for SCM.
11. Explain SCM's relationship with transportation planning.
12. Describe SCM careers.

C. Characteristics for Successful Supply Chain Managers
1. Describe leadership opportunities in SCM.
2. Describe effective communication skills for managers.
3. Explain responsibility ethics.
4. Describe language skills of successful managers.
5. Explain the importance of flexible thinking.
6. Understand freight terminology.
7. Explain people and problem-solving skills.
8. Explain technology skills.
9. Explain the sense of urgency in SCM.
10. Describe the importance of Excel knowledge.
11. Describe the temperament required for success.

D. Supply Chain Planning and Design
1. Explain purchasing's relationship with SCM.
2. Explain how a company provides value to SCM.
3. Explain how suppliers, subcontractors, transport providers, and distribution are determined.
4. Define supply chain integration.
5. Describe managing supply and demand.
6. Describe efficient facility network design.
7. Explain expense management for SCM.

E. Supply Chain Process
1. Describe manufacturing's role in a supply chain.
2. Define inventory management's relationship with SCM.
3. Explain transportation management.
4. Explain the role and cost of warehouses.
5. Explain the process for product returns.
6. Describe the importance of customer service.

F. Product Portfolio Management
1. Determine what products to sell.
2. Explain how a company determines products used and sold.
3. Define a product portfolio.
4. Explain the Pareto principle.
5. Identify slow-moving products heading toward obsolescence.
6. Explain minimizing inventory during all stages.

G. Improving Supply Chain Network Performance
1. Explain synchronizing material flow.
2. Define inventory maximization.
3. Define decoupling points.
4. Explain algorithms for capital cost optimization.
5. Explain proximity to the customer and efficiency.
6. Describe product sourcing decisions.
7. Describe evaluation processes.
8. Define how SCM meets consumer needs.
9. Explain market signals and consumer demand impact.
10. Explain meeting and improving customer needs.
11. Describe meeting logistic needs of customer segments.

H. Production Planning and Control
1. Describe types of SCM planning.
2. Define static vs. dynamic planning.
3. Explain how a master production schedule is determined.
4. Define Bill of Material (BOM).
5. Describe raw materials requirements.
6. Describe work-in-process.
7. Define finished goods and the goal to move them.

I. Supply Chain Coordination and Decision Making
1. Explain cooperation and coordination in a supply chain.
2. Describe the role of outsourcing.
3. Describe measurement of supply chain performance.
4. Explain the role of the team.
5. Describe channels of distribution for SCM.

J. Contemporary Issues
1. Describe IT's impact on SCM.
2. Explain e-business impact.
3. Describe the financial flow in SCM.
4. Explain e-commerce's impact on distribution channels.
5. Describe political and economic events impacting SCM.
6. Explain competition's impact on SCM decisions.

K. Channels of Distribution
1. List the functions of distribution channels.
2. Explain time, place, and ownership utility.
3. Explain marketing channels for assembly, storage, sorting, transport.
4. Define facilitation in SCM.
5. Explain pre-sale and post-purchase services (financing, maintenance, information, coordination).
6. Explain how SCM creates efficiencies.
7. Describe risk sharing between channels and manufacturers.
8. Define indirect channels of distribution.
9. Define the role of intermediaries.
10. Define one-level channel (manufacturer → retailer → customer).
11. Define two-level channel (manufacturer → wholesaler → retailer → customer).
12. Define three-level channel (manufacturer → agent → wholesaler → retailer → customer).
13. Describe dual distribution (showroom + internet).
14. Explain how services use intermediaries.
15. Define the on-demand business model.
16. Describe how market, product, competition, company impact channel choice.
17. Explain how product characteristics (perishability, cost, technicality) impact SCM.
