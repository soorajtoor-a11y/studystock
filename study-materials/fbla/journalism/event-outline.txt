FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — JOURNALISM (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Journalistic principles, media ethics, and the business of news:
news writing, reporting, editing, and journalism in a digital economy.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Economics and Business of Journalism
1. Identify major funding sources impacting journalism, journalists, publications.
2. Examine the economic impacts of social media on journalism.
3. Identify funding sources enabling publications and news reporting.
4. Identify education needed for journalism careers.
5. Understand ranking structure and hierarchy in a journalistic environment.
6. Identify potential salary opportunities in journalism.
7. Identify major funding sources for journalism.
8. Identify major expenses for journalism.
9. Examine economic impacts of digital and social media.

B. Grammar & Format
1. Identify perspectives and how they're used in journalism.
2. Understand abbreviation types and correct usage.
3. Understand formats used in different media sources.
4. Understand media terminology: alliteration, onomatopoeia, simile, metaphor.

C. Law and Ethics
1. Identify major laws impacting journalism.
2. Understand ethical boundaries, especially regarding sources and information sharing.
3. Understand impacts of journalists' ethical decisions on society and individuals.
4. Identify major historical events in journalism ethics.

D. Basic Journalism Principles
1. Define journalism.
2. Understand principles determining newsworthiness.
3. Explain how news values remain constant despite societal change.
4. Explain the basic questions used in news gathering (who/what/when/where/why/how).
5. Define the role of sources in journalism.
6. Understand how strong leads define a story.

E. History of Journalism
1. Explain constitutional amendments relating to journalism (esp. First Amendment).
2. Identify major figures in journalism history and their roles.
3. Explain the role of journalists in war and battle.
4. Understand the history of major industry players and their impacts.
5. Understand timelines/transitions in style, medium, circulation of publications.
6. Identify journalism awards, major recipients, and milestones.
