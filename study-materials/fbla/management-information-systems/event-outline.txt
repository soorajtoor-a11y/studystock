FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — MANAGEMENT INFORMATION SYSTEMS (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Tests knowledge of how organizations use information systems to run
and improve the business: systems fundamentals, databases, the systems
development lifecycle, networking, cybersecurity and data privacy,
e-commerce and analytics, and enterprise-wide systems and governance.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Information Systems Fundamentals (15 test items)
1. Identify the five components of an information system (hardware, software, data, people, process).
2. Differentiate transaction processing systems (TPS), management information systems (MIS), and decision support systems (DSS).
3. Explain the role of information systems in supporting core business functions.
4. Describe the difference between data, information, and knowledge.
5. Explain how information systems create competitive advantage for an organization.

B. Database Management (15 test items)
1. Explain the purpose and structure of a relational database (tables, rows, columns, keys).
2. Differentiate primary keys and foreign keys and their role in table relationships.
3. Describe the purpose of SQL in querying and managing databases.
4. Explain data normalization and why it reduces redundancy.
5. Describe the components and purpose of a data warehouse versus an operational database.

C. Systems Development Lifecycle (15 test items)
1. Identify the phases of the systems development life cycle (SDLC): planning, analysis, design, implementation, maintenance.
2. Differentiate the waterfall and agile development methodologies.
3. Explain the purpose of requirements gathering and system documentation.
4. Describe testing types used in systems development (unit, integration, user acceptance testing).
5. Explain the importance of change management and user training during system implementation.

D. Networking and Infrastructure (10 test items)
1. Differentiate LAN, WAN, and cloud-based network infrastructures.
2. Describe the client-server model and its role in business computing.
3. Explain the purpose of IP addresses, routers, and switches in a network.
4. Describe cloud computing service models (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS).

E. Cybersecurity and Data Privacy in MIS (15 test items)
1. Describe common threats to information systems (malware, phishing, insider threats).
2. Explain data privacy regulations relevant to businesses (GDPR, CCPA, and industry-specific rules such as HIPAA).
3. Describe access control and authentication methods (passwords, multifactor authentication, role-based access).
4. Explain the purpose of data backup and disaster recovery planning.
5. Describe encryption's role in protecting data at rest and in transit.

F. E-Commerce and Business Intelligence (15 test items)
1. Differentiate B2B, B2C, C2C, and C2B e-commerce models.
2. Describe the components of an e-commerce transaction (shopping cart, payment gateway, order fulfillment).
3. Explain the purpose of business intelligence (BI) tools in organizational decision-making.
4. Describe data analytics concepts (descriptive, predictive, prescriptive analytics).
5. Explain how dashboards and data visualization support managerial decisions.

G. Enterprise Systems and IT Governance (15 test items)
1. Describe the purpose and modules of an enterprise resource planning (ERP) system.
2. Explain the purpose of customer relationship management (CRM) systems.
3. Describe supply chain management (SCM) systems and their integration with ERP.
4. Explain IT governance frameworks and their role in aligning IT with business strategy.
5. Describe the importance of IT policies (acceptable use, BYOD, software licensing) in organizations.
