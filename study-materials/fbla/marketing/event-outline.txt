FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — MARKETING (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Demonstrates knowledge of core marketing principles used to plan, price,
promote, and distribute goods and services. Covers the marketing mix,
market research and segmentation, consumer behavior, branding, pricing
strategy, distribution channels, and digital/social media promotion as
applied to real business decision-making.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Foundations of Marketing (12 test items)
1. Define marketing and differentiate it from selling.
2. Identify the four Ps of the marketing mix (product, price, place, promotion).
3. Explain the role of the marketing concept in guiding business decisions.
4. Differentiate between a marketing plan and a business plan.
5. Identify the functions of marketing (selling, buying, financing, risk management, distribution, information management).
6. Explain the concept of exchange and utility (form, place, time, possession utility).
7. Describe the evolution of marketing eras (production, sales, marketing, relationship).

B. Market Research and Segmentation (14 test items)
1. Identify the steps of the marketing research process (define problem, collect data, analyze, report).
2. Differentiate between primary and secondary market research.
3. Differentiate between qualitative and quantitative research methods (surveys, focus groups, observation).
4. Explain the purpose of a target market and how it is selected.
5. Identify bases for market segmentation (demographic, geographic, psychographic, behavioral).
6. Explain the concept of market positioning and perceptual maps.
7. Differentiate between mass marketing, niche marketing, and micromarketing.
8. Interpret sample marketing research data to draw conclusions about a target market.

C. Consumer Behavior (12 test items)
1. Identify the stages of the consumer decision-making process (need recognition, information search, evaluation, purchase, post-purchase).
2. Differentiate between rational and emotional buying motives.
3. Explain factors influencing consumer behavior (cultural, social, personal, psychological).
4. Differentiate between business-to-business (B2B) and business-to-consumer (B2C) buying behavior.
5. Explain the concept of brand loyalty and its stages.
6. Describe the diffusion of innovation and adopter categories (innovators, early adopters, laggards).

D. Product and Branding Strategy (14 test items)
1. Identify the stages of the product life cycle (introduction, growth, maturity, decline).
2. Differentiate between a product mix and a product line.
3. Explain the purpose of branding and brand equity.
4. Differentiate between brand name, trademark, and trade character.
5. Identify types of packaging functions (protection, promotion, convenience).
6. Explain the stages of new product development (idea generation, screening, testing, commercialization).
7. Differentiate between generic, private-label, and national brands.
8. Describe warranty and guarantee as elements of product strategy.

E. Pricing Strategy (12 test items)
1. Identify factors that influence pricing decisions (cost, demand, competition, image).
2. Differentiate between markup and markdown pricing.
3. Explain psychological pricing strategies (odd-even pricing, prestige pricing).
4. Differentiate between penetration pricing and price skimming.
5. Calculate a selling price given cost and desired markup percentage.
6. Explain the concept of price elasticity of demand.
7. Differentiate between competitive, cost-plus, and value-based pricing strategies.

F. Place/Distribution and Channel Management (10 test items)
1. Identify types of distribution channels (direct, indirect, dual distribution).
2. Differentiate between intensive, selective, and exclusive distribution.
3. Explain the role of wholesalers and retailers in the channel of distribution.
4. Describe the function of physical distribution (warehousing, transportation, inventory control).
5. Explain how e-commerce has changed traditional distribution channels.

G. Promotion and Personal Selling (14 test items)
1. Identify the elements of the promotional mix (advertising, sales promotion, public relations, personal selling).
2. Differentiate between institutional and product advertising.
3. Identify types of sales promotion (coupons, rebates, sampling, point-of-purchase displays).
4. Explain the steps of the personal selling process (preapproach, approach, presentation, handling objections, close, follow-up).
5. Differentiate between publicity and public relations.
6. Explain the AIDA model of advertising effectiveness (attention, interest, desire, action).
7. Identify factors used to select an appropriate promotional mix for a product.

H. Digital and Social Media Marketing (12 test items)
1. Identify types of digital marketing channels (search engine marketing, email marketing, content marketing).
2. Explain the role of search engine optimization (SEO) in digital marketing.
3. Differentiate between organic and paid social media strategies.
4. Explain how businesses use social media metrics (engagement rate, click-through rate, impressions) to evaluate campaigns.
5. Describe influencer marketing and its role in reaching target audiences.
6. Explain the importance of mobile marketing and responsive design in reaching consumers.
