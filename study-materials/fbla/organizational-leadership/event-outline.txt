FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — ORGANIZATIONAL LEADERSHIP (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Leadership principles in a business context: leadership styles, team
management, motivation, and ethical decision-making.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Leadership Concepts
1. Explain why leadership is important.
2. Define leadership.
3. Explain self-assessment for leadership potential.
4. Describe leader characteristics (initiative, independence, follow-through, ethics, adaptability, resiliency, positive attitude, confidence, excellence).

B. Leadership Managerial Roles
1. Describe interpersonal roles.
2. Explain informational roles.
3. Describe decisional roles.
4. Understand effective research for leadership decisions.
5. Appreciate others' perspectives within an organization.
6. Explain how leaders use reflection and application.
7. Explain the importance of analyzing situations comprehensively.
8. Explain connecting individual thinking with systems thinking.
9. Explain evaluating alternatives for best decisions.
10. Describe generating new ideas beyond convention.
11. Explain problem solving and decision making as key leader duties.
12. Explain how self-understanding determines leadership capabilities.

C. Leadership Behavior and Motivation
1. Compare leadership styles.
2. Explain job-centered vs. employee-centered behavior.
3. Explain the motivation process.
4. Compare and contrast motivation theories.
5. Define Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs Theory.
6. Explain balancing professional and personal needs.
7. Define Equity, Expectancy, Reinforcement, and Goal-Setting Theories.

D. Networking
1. Define professional networking.
2. Explain networking's relationship with leadership.
3. Explain leadership advantages of professional networks.
4. Describe productive interpersonal interaction.
5. Describe characteristics of productive leaders (empathy, mentoring, motivation, empowerment, feedback, collaboration).

E. Communication Skills
1. Explain leadership's foundation in solid communication.
2. Define communication process elements (sender, receiver, message, feedback).
3. Explain approaches to getting feedback on messages.
4. Explain the power of nonverbal communication.
5. Explain conflict negotiation for groups.
6. Explain the importance of listening skills.

F. Leader/Follower Relations
1. Define Leader-Member Exchange (LMX) Theory.
2. Describe group dynamics' impact on team building.
3. Explain strategies for positive leader-member relations.
4. Explain effective leader feedback.
5. List characteristics of an effective follower.
6. Explain the dual role of leader and follower.

G. Team Leadership and Self-Managed Teams
1. Explain the use of teams in organizations.
2. Explain the difference between a group and a team.
3. Explain advantages/disadvantages of teamwork.
4. Describe effective team characteristics and leadership's role.
5. Differentiate team types: functional, cross-functional, self-managed.

H. Strategic Leadership for Managing Crises and Change
1. Explain analyzing the environment for strategic vision.
2. Explain mission statements, objectives, strategic planning/implementation.
3. Explain evaluating leadership strategies.
4. Explain the need for leadership in crises.
5. Define crisis management.
6. List the five-step crisis process (risk identification, assessment/ranking, reduction strategies, prevention simulations, crisis management).
7. Recognize the need for change.
8. Understand why people resist change.
9. Define the change process.
10. Explain mission, vision, goals, plan, organization for decisions.

I. Levels of Leadership
1. Explain individual leadership.
2. Describe leadership within a group.
3. Define organizational leadership.
4. Describe interrelationships among the three levels.

J. Leadership Theory
1. Describe leadership theory.
2. Explain application of leadership theory.
3. Explain leadership skill development and flexibility.

K. Traits of Effective Leaders
1. Define traits of effective leaders.
2. Explain how leadership traits can be acquired.

L. Personality Profile of Effective Leaders
1. Explain the Achievement Motivation Theory.
2. Define the Leader Motive Profile.
3. Explain self-assessment for leadership qualities.

M. Leadership Attitudes
1. Differentiate Theory X and Theory Y leadership.
2. Explain how attitudes influence leadership styles.
3. Explain the Pygmalion Effect.

N. Ethical Leadership
1. Explain ethical leadership behavior.
2. Explain how personality, moral development, and situations affect ethics.
3. Describe how people justify unethical behavior.
4. Describe simple guides to ethical behavior.
5. List characteristics of ethical leaders.

O. Power, Politics, Networking, and Negotiation
1. Describe sources of power for leaders.
2. Explain power types, influencing tactics, increasing personal power.

P. Coaching
1. Explain the coaching-leadership relationship.
2. Describe why criticism does not work.
3. Explain mentoring for preparing future leaders.

Q. Managing Conflict
1. Describe conflict management styles.
2. Define conflict resolution and mediation.

R. Leadership and Team Decision Making
1. Describe leader-centered vs. group-centered decision making.
2. Explain individual vs. team decision making.

S. Organizational Politics
1. Explain the nature of organizational politics.
2. Explain political behavior's relationship with leadership.

T. Charismatic and Transformational Leadership
1. Describe factors determining personal meaning.
2. Describe characteristics of charismatic leaders.
3. Explain effects of charismatic leadership.
4. Define transformational leadership attributes and behaviors.

U. Stewardship and Servant Leadership
1. Explain stewardship and servant leadership.
2. Describe their frameworks.

V. Leadership in a Diverse Setting
1. Understand the power of culture.
2. Differentiate low-performance vs. high-performance culture.
3. Describe leaders as culture creators.
4. Explain culture, values, diversity, and learning organizations.
5. Define diversity; explain inclusion's importance for decisions.
6. Explain the importance of social responsibility.
