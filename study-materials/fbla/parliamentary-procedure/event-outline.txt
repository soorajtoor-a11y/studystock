FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — PARLIAMENTARY PROCEDURE (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Demonstrates knowledge of parliamentary law as codified in Robert's Rules
of Order Newly Revised: the principles that allow deliberative assemblies
to conduct business fairly and efficiently. Covers meeting roles, the
order of business, classes and rank of motions, voting and elections, and
the recording of minutes.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Foundations and Principles of Parliamentary Law (10 test items)
1. Explain the purpose of parliamentary procedure in a deliberative assembly.
2. Identify the source authority of Robert's Rules of Order Newly Revised.
3. Explain the principle that only one question may be considered at a time.
4. Explain the principle that the rights of the minority must be protected while the majority rules.
5. Differentiate between a deliberative assembly and an informal meeting.
6. Explain the purpose of an organization's bylaws relative to its adopted parliamentary authority.

B. Meeting Roles and Officer Duties (12 test items)
1. Identify the duties of the president/chair in presiding over a meeting.
2. Identify the duties of the vice president in the absence of the president.
3. Identify the duties of the secretary in recording and maintaining minutes.
4. Identify the duties of the treasurer in reporting on finances.
5. Explain the role of the parliamentarian as an advisor to the chair.
6. Explain the role of the sergeant-at-arms in maintaining order.
7. Differentiate between an ex officio member and an elected officer.

C. Order of Business and Agenda (10 test items)
1. Identify the standard order of business (call to order, reading/approval of minutes, officer reports, committee reports, unfinished business, new business, announcements, adjournment).
2. Differentiate between the order of business and a meeting agenda.
3. Explain the purpose of a consent agenda.
4. Explain how unfinished business differs from new business on an agenda.
5. Explain the purpose and process of calling a meeting to order and establishing a quorum.

D. Main Motions and Basic Procedure (12 test items)
1. Explain the steps to bring a main motion before the assembly (obtain floor, state motion, second, restate, debate, vote, announce result).
2. Explain the purpose of seconding a motion.
3. Differentiate between a main motion and an incidental main motion.
4. Explain when and how a motion may be withdrawn by its maker.
5. Explain the chair's role in restating and putting a motion to a vote.
6. Identify the requirements for a motion to be in order (germane, one topic, properly seconded).

E. Subsidiary Motions (14 test items)
1. Identify the rank order of subsidiary motions (lay on the table, previous question, limit/extend debate, postpone definitely, refer to committee, amend, postpone indefinitely).
2. Explain the purpose and effect of the motion to amend.
3. Differentiate between amending by inserting/adding, striking out, and substituting.
4. Explain the purpose of the motion to refer to a committee.
5. Explain the purpose and vote requirement of the motion to move the previous question (close debate).
6. Explain the purpose of the motion to postpone to a certain time versus postpone indefinitely.
7. Explain the purpose and effect of the motion to lay on the table.

F. Privileged Motions (10 test items)
1. Identify the rank order of privileged motions (fix time to adjourn, adjourn, recess, raise a question of privilege, call for the orders of the day).
2. Explain the purpose of a question of privilege.
3. Explain the difference between the motion to recess and the motion to adjourn.
4. Explain why privileged motions are not debatable and take precedence over main and subsidiary motions.

G. Incidental Motions (14 test items)
1. Identify common incidental motions (point of order, appeal, suspend the rules, object to consideration, division of the question, division of the assembly).
2. Explain the purpose of a point of order and when it must be raised.
3. Explain the process and effect of an appeal from the decision of the chair.
4. Explain the purpose of the motion to suspend the rules.
5. Explain the purpose of dividing a question into separate motions.
6. Explain the purpose of demanding a division of the assembly on a voice vote.
7. Explain the difference between a point of order and a point of parliamentary inquiry.

H. Voting Procedures and Elections (10 test items)
1. Differentiate between a voice vote, rising vote, show of hands, and ballot vote.
2. Explain the vote threshold required for a majority vote versus a two-thirds vote.
3. Identify motions that require a two-thirds vote (previous question, limit debate, suspend the rules).
4. Explain the process of nominations from the floor versus nominations by a committee.
5. Explain the procedure for conducting an election by ballot and breaking a tie vote.

I. Minutes, Quorum, and Records (8 test items)
1. Identify the required content of meeting minutes (type of meeting, date, attendance, motions and their disposition, time of adjournment).
2. Explain the process for approving or correcting minutes at the next meeting.
3. Define quorum and explain the consequence of proceeding without one.
4. Explain the purpose of maintaining an order of business record and standing rules for future reference.
