FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — PERSONAL FINANCE (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Essential financial skills: budgeting, saving, credit, investing,
and responsible financial decision-making.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Principles of Money (20 test items)
1. Explain forms of financial exchange (cash, credit, debit, EFT).
2. Identify types of currency (paper money, coins, banknotes, government bonds, treasury notes).
3. Describe functions of money (medium of exchange, unit of measure, store of value).
4. Describe sources of income and compensation.
5. Explain the time value of money.
6. Explain the purposes and importance of credit.
7. Explain legal responsibilities with consumer financial products/services.

B. Financial Needs and Goals (15 test items)
1. Explain the need to save and invest.
2. Set financial goals.
3. Develop a personal budget.
4. Determine personal net worth.
5. Describe the nature of budgets.

C. Financial-Services Providers (5 test items)
1. Describe types of financial-services providers.
2. Discuss considerations in selecting a provider.

D. Investment Strategies (5 test items)
1. Explain types of investments.
2. Explain factors when selecting investments.

E. Financial Literacy (50 test items — the biggest section!)
1. Explain the nature of tax liabilities.
2. Interpret a pay stub.
3. Write checks.
4. Maintain financial records.
5. Balance a bank account.
6. Explain the nature of charitable giving.
7. Manage online accounts.
8. Calculate the cost of credit.
9. Demonstrate the wise use of credit.
10. Validate credit history.
11. Make responsible financial decisions.
12. Protect against identity theft.
13. Pay bills.
14. Apply for a consumer loan.
15. Control debt.
16. Prepare personal income tax forms.
17. Discuss options for financing a college education.
18. Discuss the nature of retirement planning.
19. Explain the nature of estate planning.

F. Risk Management (5 test items)
1. Describe the concept of insurance.
2. Determine insurance needs.

OFFICIAL REFERENCE RESOURCES (from FBLA guidelines)
- MBA Research National Business Administration Standards (not required)
- Investopedia Personal Finance; Practical Money Skills; The Balance (investing risk)
