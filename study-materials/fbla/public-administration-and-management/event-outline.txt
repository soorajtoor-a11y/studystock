FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — PUBLIC ADMINISTRATION & MANAGEMENT (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
How government functions and its role in society: public policy,
organizational structure, budgeting, political and economic systems.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Public Finance (15 test items)
1. Explain the role of finance in the public sector.
2. Describe the nature of budgets.
3. Explain the nature of operating budgets.
4. Develop a company's/department's budget.
5. Discuss the nature of public funding.

B. Public Policy and Governance (30 test items — the biggest section!)
1. Discuss the policy lifecycle.
2. Explain principles of effective policy design.
3. Analyze the effectiveness of public policies.
4. Discuss factors influencing policymaking (civic engagement, advocacy, public discourse).
5. Discuss the role of policymaking in governance.
6. Discuss the nature of law and sources of law in the United States.
7. Describe the nature of legal procedure.
8. Discuss the role of administrative law.
9. Describe the relationship among innovation, learning, and change.

C. Communication (10 test items)
1. Explain the nature of effective written communications.
2. Adapt written correspondence to targeted audiences.
3. Write proposals.
4. Discuss digital communication in the public sector (social media, internet).

D. Public Sector Fundamentals (15 test items)
1. Determine the relationship between government and business.
2. Distinguish public administration from private sector management.
3. Discuss the role of the public sector in society.
4. Describe services provided by the public sector.
5. Distinguish among government entities, non-profits, private businesses.

E. Public Management (20 test items)
1. Discuss the nature of human resources management.
2. Discuss the nature of managerial planning.
3. Explain managerial considerations in organizing.
4. Describe managerial considerations in staffing.
5. Discuss managerial considerations in directing.
6. Describe managerial control (control process, types, what is controlled).

F. Ethics (10 test items)
1. Comply with the spirit and intent of laws and regulations.
2. Explain the importance of ethics in the public sector.
3. Discuss the concept of public accountability.
4. Discuss conflicts of interest in the public sector.

OFFICIAL REFERENCE RESOURCES (from FBLA guidelines)
- MBA Research National Business Administration Standards (not required)
- ICMA local government leadership; SIGMA Principles of Public Administration
- University public administration syllabi (Texas A&M, Ohio State, U. Florida, WVU)
