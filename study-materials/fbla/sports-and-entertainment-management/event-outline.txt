FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — SPORTS & ENTERTAINMENT MANAGEMENT (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Round 1 Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 role play).
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Demonstrates understanding of the business principles that govern the
sports and entertainment industry. Covers industry structure and career
paths, event and venue operations, sponsorship and licensing, sports and
entertainment marketing, media and broadcasting rights, talent
management, ticketing and revenue models, and legal/risk issues.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Industry Structure and Career Paths (10 test items)
1. Identify major segments of the sports and entertainment industry (professional sports, collegiate athletics, live entertainment, media).
2. Differentiate between amateur and professional sports organizations.
3. Identify career paths within sports and entertainment management (agent, general manager, event coordinator, marketing director).
4. Explain the role of governing bodies and leagues in organizing competition (e.g., professional leagues, NCAA).
5. Explain how economic factors (market size, disposable income) affect the sports and entertainment industry.

B. Event and Venue Management (14 test items)
1. Identify the phases of event planning (pre-event, day-of-event, post-event evaluation).
2. Explain the purpose of a run-of-show or event timeline document.
3. Identify venue management considerations (capacity, seating configuration, concessions, security).
4. Explain the purpose of a rider in an entertainment contract.
5. Differentiate between a public, private, and municipally owned venue.
6. Explain crowd management and emergency preparedness responsibilities at a venue.
7. Explain the purpose of a post-event evaluation and attendee feedback.

C. Sponsorship, Endorsements, and Licensing (14 test items)
1. Differentiate between sponsorship and endorsement.
2. Explain the concept of activation in a sponsorship agreement.
3. Explain how sponsorship value is measured (media impressions, brand exposure, ROI).
4. Differentiate between exclusive and non-exclusive sponsorship rights.
5. Explain the purpose of licensing agreements for merchandise and branded products.
6. Explain the concept of ambush marketing and why it is prohibited at sponsored events.
7. Identify factors a company considers before entering a sponsorship agreement (target audience fit, cost, exposure).

D. Sports and Entertainment Marketing (14 test items)
1. Explain how the marketing mix (product, price, place, promotion) applies to sports and entertainment properties.
2. Differentiate between marketing a sports/entertainment product to fans versus to sponsors.
3. Explain the concept of fan engagement and loyalty programs.
4. Identify promotional strategies used in sports and entertainment (giveaways, theme nights, social media campaigns).
5. Explain the role of market research in identifying a target fan base.
6. Explain the concept of a brand ambassador in sports and entertainment marketing.
7. Explain how demographic and psychographic segmentation is applied to fan bases.

E. Media Rights and Broadcasting (10 test items)
1. Explain the concept of media/broadcast rights and how they generate revenue.
2. Differentiate between linear broadcast, cable, and streaming distribution models.
3. Explain how advertising revenue relates to broadcast ratings.
4. Explain the purpose of blackout restrictions in broadcast agreements.
5. Explain the growing role of digital and social media platforms in distributing sports/entertainment content.

F. Athlete and Talent Management (12 test items)
1. Explain the role of an agent in representing an athlete or entertainer.
2. Differentiate between an agent and a business manager.
3. Explain the purpose of a standard player/talent contract (compensation, term, performance clauses).
4. Explain the concept of image rights and their commercial value.
5. Explain the purpose of a code of conduct clause in a talent contract.
6. Explain the role of a talent agency versus an individual agent.

G. Ticketing, Revenue, and Finance (14 test items)
1. Identify revenue streams for a sports/entertainment organization (ticket sales, sponsorship, media rights, concessions, merchandise).
2. Differentiate between dynamic pricing and fixed pricing for tickets.
3. Explain the concept of a season ticket and its benefit to venue revenue stability.
4. Explain the purpose of a secondary ticket market and its risks (scalping, fraud).
5. Calculate gate revenue given attendance, average ticket price, and capacity utilization.
6. Explain the concept of a salary cap and luxury tax in professional sports finance.
7. Explain the purpose of a break-even analysis for an event budget.

H. Legal and Risk Issues (12 test items)
1. Identify sources of liability at a sports/entertainment venue (spectator injury, negligence, premises liability).
2. Explain the purpose of a liability waiver and assumption-of-risk clause.
3. Explain the purpose of event insurance (general liability, cancellation insurance).
4. Explain the concept of intellectual property protection for team names, logos, and broadcasts.
5. Differentiate between a breach of contract and a force majeure event in an entertainment contract.
6. Explain the purpose of anti-doping and eligibility regulations in athletic competition.
