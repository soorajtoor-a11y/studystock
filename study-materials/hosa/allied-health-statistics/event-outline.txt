HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — ALLIED HEALTH STATISTICS (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory health/biostatistics curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Descriptive and inferential statistics as applied to health and medical data,
including data presentation, probability, distributions, estimation,
hypothesis testing, correlation, and epidemiological measures.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Foundations of Statistics & Data Types (4 items)
1. Differentiate levels of measurement (nominal, ordinal, interval, ratio).
2. Distinguish qualitative vs. quantitative data and discrete vs. continuous variables.
3. Differentiate population vs. sample and parameter vs. statistic.
4. Identify common data collection methods and sources of bias in health data.

B. Data Presentation & Graphical Methods (5 items)
1. Interpret frequency distribution tables and histograms.
2. Read and construct bar graphs, pie charts, and line graphs of health data.
3. Interpret stem-and-leaf plots and box-and-whisker plots.
4. Identify appropriate graph types for categorical vs. numerical data.
5. Detect misleading graphs (truncated axes, scale distortion).

C. Measures of Central Tendency (5 items)
1. Calculate the mean, median, and mode from a data set.
2. Determine which measure of central tendency is most appropriate for skewed data.
3. Calculate a weighted mean.
4. Identify the effect of outliers on the mean vs. the median.
5. Calculate the mean from a grouped frequency distribution.

D. Measures of Variability (5 items)
1. Calculate the range and interquartile range (IQR).
2. Calculate variance and standard deviation for a sample and a population.
3. Calculate the coefficient of variation.
4. Interpret standard deviation as a measure of spread.
5. Identify outliers using the IQR rule or z-scores.

E. Probability Basics (6 items)
1. Calculate simple, joint, and conditional probability.
2. Differentiate independent vs. dependent events.
3. Apply the addition rule and multiplication rule of probability.
4. Calculate probability using a contingency table.
5. Differentiate sensitivity, specificity, and predictive value in a diagnostic test.
6. Calculate odds and odds ratio vs. relative risk.

F. The Normal Distribution (6 items)
1. Describe the properties of the normal (bell) curve.
2. Apply the empirical rule (68-95-99.7 rule).
3. Calculate and interpret a z-score.
4. Use a z-table to find the proportion of data above/below a value.
5. Differentiate a normal distribution from a skewed distribution.
6. Explain the standard normal distribution and its use in comparing data sets.

G. Sampling & the Central Limit Theorem (4 items)
1. Differentiate sampling methods (random, stratified, systematic, convenience, cluster).
2. Explain sampling error and the sampling distribution of the mean.
3. Explain the Central Limit Theorem and its implications for sample size.
4. Calculate the standard error of the mean.

H. Estimation & Confidence Intervals (4 items)
1. Interpret a confidence interval for a population mean or proportion.
2. Explain how sample size and confidence level affect interval width.
3. Calculate a confidence interval given a mean, standard deviation, and sample size.
4. Interpret margin of error in a health survey report.

I. Hypothesis Testing Basics (5 items)
1. State a null hypothesis and an alternative hypothesis.
2. Differentiate Type I and Type II error.
3. Interpret a p-value and statistical significance (alpha = 0.05).
4. Differentiate a one-tailed and two-tailed test.
5. Identify when to use a t-test vs. a chi-square test.

J. Correlation & Regression (3 items)
1. Interpret a correlation coefficient (r) and a scatterplot.
2. Differentiate correlation from causation.
3. Interpret a simple linear regression line and a predicted value.

K. Epidemiological & Health Statistics (3 items)
1. Calculate incidence rate and prevalence rate.
2. Differentiate morbidity and mortality rates (crude, age-adjusted).
3. Calculate and interpret a case fatality rate.
