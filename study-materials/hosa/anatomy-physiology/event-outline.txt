HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — ANATOMY & PHYSIOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
anatomy & physiology curriculum by body system.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per body system are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Structure and function of the human body across all major systems, from the
cellular level through organ systems and homeostasis.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Chemistry, Cells & Levels of Organization (3 items)
1. Describe the levels of structural organization of the body.
2. Explain basic chemistry relevant to physiology (atoms, molecules, pH, macromolecules).
3. Describe cell structure, organelles, transport, and the cell cycle.

B. Tissues (2 items)
1. Identify the four primary tissue types and their functions.
2. Describe membranes and tissue repair.

C. Integumentary System (3 items)
1. Describe the structure and layers of the skin.
2. Explain the functions of the integumentary system (protection, thermoregulation).

D. Skeletal System (5 items)
1. Identify major bones and bone structure.
2. Explain bone formation, growth, and remodeling.
3. Describe joint types and movements.

E. Muscular System (5 items)
1. Describe the three muscle types and their characteristics.
2. Explain the sliding-filament model of muscle contraction.
3. Identify major skeletal muscles and their actions.

F. Nervous System & Special Senses (6 items)
1. Describe neuron structure and the nerve impulse (action potential).
2. Explain the organization of the CNS and PNS.
3. Describe brain regions and their functions.
4. Explain the senses of vision, hearing, taste, smell, and balance.

G. Endocrine System (4 items)
1. Identify major endocrine glands and their hormones.
2. Explain hormone action and feedback regulation.

H. Cardiovascular System (6 items)
1. Describe blood components and functions.
2. Explain heart anatomy and the cardiac cycle/conduction system.
3. Describe blood vessels and circulation pathways.
4. Explain blood pressure regulation.

I. Lymphatic & Immune System (3 items)
1. Describe lymphatic structures and functions.
2. Explain innate and adaptive immunity.

J. Respiratory System (4 items)
1. Identify respiratory structures.
2. Explain pulmonary ventilation and gas exchange.
3. Describe the control of respiration.

K. Digestive System (4 items)
1. Identify digestive organs and accessory structures.
2. Explain digestion, absorption, and metabolism of nutrients.

L. Urinary System (3 items)
1. Describe kidney and nephron structure.
2. Explain urine formation and fluid/electrolyte balance.

M. Reproductive System & Development (2 items)
1. Describe male and female reproductive anatomy.
2. Explain the basics of fertilization and development.
