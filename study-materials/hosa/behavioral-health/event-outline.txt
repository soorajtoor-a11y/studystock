HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — BEHAVIORAL HEALTH (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreakers).
Item counts per content area (from the official test-plan percentages) shown in
(parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of mental health and substance use disorders, therapeutic
communication and counseling techniques, treatment modalities and levels of
care, and the legal/ethical framework governing behavioral health services.
Covers recognition of disorder categories using DSM-5-TR classification,
crisis intervention and suicide risk, and the roles of the behavioral health
treatment team.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Foundations & History of Behavioral Health (2 items)
1. Describe the evolution of mental health care from asylums to community-based treatment (deinstitutionalization).
2. Identify major theoretical models of mental illness (biological, psychodynamic, behavioral, cognitive, sociocultural).

B. Growth, Development & Personality Theory (3 items)
1. Apply Erikson's stages of psychosocial development to identify age-appropriate behavior and developmental crises.
2. Differentiate Freud's structural model of personality (id, ego, superego) and defense mechanisms (denial, projection, repression, regression).
3. Explain Maslow's hierarchy of needs as it relates to patient motivation and behavior.

C. DSM-5-TR Classification & Diagnostic Process (4 items)
1. Explain the purpose and structure of the DSM-5-TR as the standard diagnostic classification system.
2. Differentiate the multiaxial concepts of diagnosis, prognosis, and etiology.
3. Identify components of a mental status exam (appearance, mood, affect, thought process, orientation).
4. Distinguish between signs and symptoms as used in a psychiatric assessment.

D. Anxiety, OCD & Trauma-/Stressor-Related Disorders (4 items)
1. Identify symptoms of generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, and specific phobias.
2. Differentiate obsessive-compulsive disorder (obsessions vs. compulsions) from generalized anxiety.
3. Identify symptoms and diagnostic criteria of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), including flashbacks and hyperarousal.
4. Describe acute stress disorder and adjustment disorder and how they differ from PTSD by duration/onset.
5. Explain the physiological basis of the fight-or-flight response in anxiety disorders.

E. Depressive & Bipolar Disorders (5 items)
1. Identify diagnostic criteria for major depressive disorder (e.g., anhedonia, sleep/appetite changes, duration).
2. Differentiate persistent depressive disorder (dysthymia) from major depressive disorder by severity and duration.
3. Identify manic and hypomanic episode criteria (elevated mood, grandiosity, decreased need for sleep, pressured speech).
4. Differentiate Bipolar I from Bipolar II disorder based on presence of full manic vs. hypomanic episodes.
5. Recognize seasonal affective disorder and postpartum depression as specifiers of depressive disorders.

F. Schizophrenia Spectrum & Psychotic Disorders (4 items)
1. Differentiate positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech) from negative symptoms (flat affect, avolition, alogia) of schizophrenia.
2. Identify types of delusions (persecutory, grandiose, referential) and hallucinations (auditory, visual, tactile).
3. Distinguish schizophrenia from schizoaffective disorder and brief psychotic disorder by duration and mood component.
4. Explain the dopamine hypothesis as a biological theory of psychosis.

G. Personality Disorders (2 items)
1. Classify personality disorders into Cluster A (odd/eccentric), Cluster B (dramatic/erratic), and Cluster C (anxious/fearful).
2. Identify hallmark features of borderline, antisocial, and narcissistic personality disorders.

H. Substance-Related & Addictive Disorders (4 items)
1. Differentiate substance use, intoxication, withdrawal, and substance-induced disorders.
2. Identify withdrawal syndromes and risks for alcohol (delirium tremens), opioids, and benzodiazepines.
3. Explain the neurobiology of addiction (dopamine reward pathway, tolerance, dependence).
4. Identify criteria and stages of the CAGE and DSM-5-TR substance use disorder severity scale (mild/moderate/severe).

I. Eating Disorders (3 items)
1. Differentiate anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder by behavior pattern and medical risk.
2. Identify medical complications of eating disorders (electrolyte imbalance, cardiac arrhythmia, esophageal damage).
3. Describe the role of body image distortion in the etiology of eating disorders.

J. Neurodevelopmental & Neurocognitive Disorders (4 items)
1. Identify core features of autism spectrum disorder and ADHD (inattention, hyperactivity-impulsivity).
2. Differentiate delirium from dementia by onset, course, and reversibility.
3. Identify stages and features of Alzheimer's disease and other major neurocognitive disorders.
4. Describe intellectual disability classification based on adaptive functioning and IQ.

K. Therapeutic Communication & Counseling Techniques (5 items)
1. Identify therapeutic communication techniques (open-ended questions, reflection, restating, silence, clarification).
2. Identify nontherapeutic communication blocks (giving advice, false reassurance, changing the subject).
3. Explain the principles of active listening and empathy in a helping relationship.
4. Describe motivational interviewing techniques used to address ambivalence in behavior change.
5. Differentiate directive and non-directive counseling approaches.

L. Treatment Modalities & Psychopharmacology (4 items)
1. Differentiate individual, group, family, and milieu therapy approaches.
2. Identify major drug classes used in behavioral health (SSRIs, SNRIs, antipsychotics, mood stabilizers, anxiolytics) and example medications.
3. Describe cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and dialectical behavior therapy (DBT) principles and target populations.
4. Explain the indications and process of electroconvulsive therapy (ECT).

M. Crisis Intervention & Suicide Prevention (4 items)
1. Identify risk factors and warning signs of suicide (prior attempts, hopelessness, giving away possessions).
2. Describe the steps of crisis intervention and de-escalation techniques for an agitated patient.
3. Explain protective factors that reduce suicide risk (social support, treatment engagement, safety planning).
4. Differentiate verbal de-escalation from the use of restraints/seclusion as a last resort.

N. Legal, Ethical Issues & Treatment Settings (2 items)
1. Differentiate voluntary and involuntary commitment and the criteria for emergency detention.
2. Identify levels of care in behavioral health (inpatient, partial hospitalization, intensive outpatient, outpatient) and confidentiality requirements under HIPAA and 42 CFR Part 2.
