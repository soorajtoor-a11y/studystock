HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — BIOCHEMISTRY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory biochemistry curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Chemical structure and function of biological molecules, enzyme kinetics,
metabolic pathways and bioenergetics, and the molecular basis of gene
expression, at an introductory college level.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Water, pH & Buffers (4 items)
1. Explain the properties of water relevant to biological systems (polarity, hydrogen bonding).
2. Calculate pH and pOH from hydrogen ion concentration.
3. Apply the Henderson-Hasselbalch equation to a buffer system.
4. Explain the role of buffers (e.g., the bicarbonate buffer system) in maintaining physiological pH.

B. Amino Acids & Protein Structure (7 items)
1. Identify the general structure of an amino acid and classify side chains (nonpolar, polar, acidic, basic).
2. Describe peptide bond formation and the primary structure of a protein.
3. Differentiate secondary structures (alpha helix, beta sheet).
4. Describe tertiary and quaternary protein structure and the bonds that stabilize them.
5. Explain protein denaturation and the factors that cause it (heat, pH, urea).
6. Relate protein structure to function (e.g., hemoglobin vs. myoglobin).
7. Describe basic methods for determining a protein's primary sequence.

C. Enzymes & Enzyme Kinetics (6 items)
1. Explain enzyme specificity and the induced-fit model.
2. Interpret a Michaelis-Menten curve and define Km and Vmax.
3. Differentiate competitive, noncompetitive, and uncompetitive inhibition.
4. Interpret a Lineweaver-Burk plot.
5. Explain the effect of temperature and pH on enzyme activity.
6. Describe enzyme cofactors and coenzymes (e.g., vitamins as coenzyme precursors).

D. Carbohydrates (4 items)
1. Differentiate monosaccharides, disaccharides, and polysaccharides.
2. Identify glycosidic bond formation and hydrolysis.
3. Differentiate the structure and function of starch, glycogen, and cellulose.
4. Describe the structural difference between glucose, fructose, and galactose.

E. Lipids (4 items)
1. Differentiate saturated, unsaturated, and trans fatty acids.
2. Describe triglyceride structure and formation (ester bonds).
3. Describe phospholipid structure and its role in membrane bilayers.
4. Describe cholesterol structure and its role in membranes and steroid hormone synthesis.

F. Nucleic Acids (5 items)
1. Describe the structure of a nucleotide (sugar, phosphate, nitrogenous base).
2. Differentiate purines and pyrimidines.
3. Describe the double-helix structure of DNA and complementary base pairing.
4. Differentiate DNA and RNA in structure and function.
5. Describe DNA replication (semiconservative model, key enzymes: helicase, DNA polymerase, ligase).

G. Gene Expression (5 items)
1. Describe transcription (RNA polymerase, mRNA synthesis, promoter).
2. Describe translation (ribosomes, tRNA, codons, start/stop codons).
3. Explain the genetic code and its properties (redundancy, universality).
4. Describe post-translational modification of proteins.
5. Explain basic mutation types (point, insertion, deletion) and their effect on protein.

H. Bioenergetics & ATP (4 items)
1. Explain the role of ATP as the energy currency of the cell.
2. Describe exergonic and endergonic reactions and coupled reactions.
3. Explain oxidation-reduction (redox) reactions in metabolism.
4. Describe the role of NAD+/NADH and FAD/FADH2 as electron carriers.

I. Glycolysis & Fermentation (4 items)
1. Describe the net ATP and NADH yield of glycolysis.
2. Identify the substrate and products of glycolysis (glucose to pyruvate).
3. Explain the regulation of glycolysis (key enzyme: phosphofructokinase).
4. Differentiate lactic acid fermentation and alcoholic fermentation.

J. Citric Acid Cycle & Oxidative Phosphorylation (4 items)
1. Describe the entry of pyruvate into the mitochondrion (pyruvate dehydrogenase, acetyl-CoA).
2. Identify the products of one turn of the citric acid (Krebs) cycle.
3. Describe the electron transport chain and chemiosmosis (ATP synthase).
4. Calculate the approximate total ATP yield from complete glucose oxidation.

K. Lipid & Protein Metabolism (3 items)
1. Describe beta-oxidation of fatty acids.
2. Describe transamination and deamination of amino acids.
3. Describe the urea cycle and nitrogen waste excretion.
