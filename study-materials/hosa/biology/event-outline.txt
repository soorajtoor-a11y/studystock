HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — BIOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
AP/introductory college biology curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
General biology across the major unifying themes of the discipline: cell
structure and energetics, genetics and molecular biology, evolution, ecology,
classification, and an overview of human body systems.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Chemistry of Life (3 items)
1. Describe the properties of water and its role in living systems.
2. Identify the four major macromolecules (carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, nucleic acids) and their monomers.
3. Explain dehydration synthesis and hydrolysis reactions.

B. Cell Structure & Function (5 items)
1. Differentiate prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells.
2. Identify major organelles and their functions (nucleus, mitochondria, ER, Golgi, ribosomes, lysosomes).
3. Describe the fluid mosaic model of the plasma membrane.
4. Differentiate passive transport (diffusion, osmosis, facilitated diffusion) and active transport.
5. Explain endocytosis and exocytosis.

C. Cellular Energy: Respiration & Photosynthesis (5 items)
1. Describe the overall inputs and outputs of cellular respiration.
2. Identify the stages of cellular respiration (glycolysis, Krebs cycle, electron transport chain).
3. Describe the overall inputs and outputs of photosynthesis (light reactions, Calvin cycle).
4. Explain the role of chlorophyll and chloroplast structure.
5. Compare aerobic respiration and anaerobic fermentation.

D. Cell Division (4 items)
1. Describe the phases of the cell cycle (interphase, mitosis, cytokinesis).
2. Identify the stages of mitosis and their key events.
3. Describe the stages of meiosis I and II and how they produce genetic variation.
4. Differentiate mitosis and meiosis in purpose and outcome.

E. Mendelian Genetics (5 items)
1. Apply Mendel's law of segregation and law of independent assortment.
2. Use a Punnett square to predict genotypic and phenotypic ratios for monohybrid and dihybrid crosses.
3. Differentiate dominant, recessive, codominant, and incomplete dominance inheritance.
4. Differentiate genotype and phenotype, homozygous and heterozygous.
5. Explain sex-linked inheritance patterns (e.g., color blindness, hemophilia).

F. Molecular Genetics (5 items)
1. Describe DNA structure and the process of DNA replication.
2. Describe transcription and translation (central dogma).
3. Explain the structure and role of mRNA, tRNA, and rRNA.
4. Describe types of mutations and their potential effects.
5. Explain basic concepts of gene regulation (e.g., operons).

G. Evolution & Natural Selection (5 items)
1. Explain Darwin's theory of natural selection and its supporting evidence.
2. Differentiate natural selection, genetic drift, and gene flow as mechanisms of evolution.
3. Describe evidence for evolution (fossil record, homologous structures, molecular biology).
4. Explain the concepts of adaptation and fitness.
5. Describe speciation and reproductive isolation.

H. Ecology (5 items)
1. Define the levels of ecological organization (organism, population, community, ecosystem, biosphere).
2. Describe energy flow through trophic levels and food webs.
3. Explain biogeochemical cycles (carbon, nitrogen, water).
4. Differentiate types of species interactions (predation, competition, symbiosis).
5. Describe population growth models (exponential vs. logistic, carrying capacity).

I. Classification & Diversity (4 items)
1. Describe the hierarchical taxonomic classification system (domain to species).
2. Differentiate the three domains and six kingdoms of life.
3. Explain binomial nomenclature.
4. Describe major characteristics distinguishing plants, animals, fungi, and protists.

J. Human Body Systems Overview (4 items)
1. Identify the major human organ systems and their primary functions.
2. Describe homeostasis and negative feedback mechanisms.
3. Describe the basic structure and function of the nervous and endocrine systems.
4. Describe the basic structure and function of the circulatory and respiratory systems.

K. Viruses & Microorganisms (3 items)
1. Describe the basic structure of a virus and the lytic vs. lysogenic cycle.
2. Differentiate bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protists in basic structure.
3. Explain the role of microorganisms in disease and in ecosystems.

L. Scientific Method & Biotechnology (2 items)
1. Identify the steps of the scientific method and elements of experimental design (control, variables).
2. Describe basic biotechnology techniques (PCR, gel electrophoresis, cloning).
