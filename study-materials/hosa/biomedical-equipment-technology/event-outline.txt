HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — BIOMEDICAL EQUIPMENT TECHNOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreakers).
Item counts per content area (from the official test-plan percentages) shown in
(parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of the installation, calibration, maintenance, and repair of medical
equipment, including electricity/electronics fundamentals, electrical safety
codes, regulatory requirements, and the diagnostic, therapeutic, and
life-support devices used across clinical settings. Includes preventive
maintenance programs, troubleshooting methodology, and infection control as
applied to biomedical equipment.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Role of the Biomedical Equipment Technician (2 items)
1. Describe the scope of practice of a biomedical equipment technician (BMET) within a healthcare facility's clinical engineering department.
2. Identify required certifications and credentialing bodies (e.g., CBET certification through AAMI/ICC).

B. Electricity & Electronics Fundamentals (6 items)
1. Apply Ohm's Law (V = IR) and Watt's Law (P = IV) to calculate voltage, current, resistance, and power.
2. Differentiate series and parallel circuits and calculate total resistance in each configuration.
3. Differentiate alternating current (AC) and direct current (DC) and identify common waveform characteristics (frequency, amplitude, period).
4. Identify basic electronic components (resistor, capacitor, inductor, diode, transistor) and their functions.
5. Explain the function of transformers in stepping voltage up or down.
6. Interpret a basic block diagram or schematic symbol set for a medical device circuit.

C. Electrical Safety Standards & Codes (5 items)
1. Differentiate macroshock and microshock and explain why cardiac patients are more vulnerable to microshock.
2. Identify NFPA 99 (Health Care Facilities Code) requirements for patient care areas.
3. Explain the purpose of ground fault circuit interrupters (GFCIs) and equipotential grounding in patient care areas.
4. Interpret acceptable leakage current limits for chassis and patient lead leakage per applicable safety standards.
5. Identify the class and type ratings of medical equipment (Class I/II, Type B/BF/CF) based on degree of patient protection.

D. Regulatory & Accreditation Bodies (4 items)
1. Identify the role of the FDA in medical device classification (Class I, II, III) and premarket approval/510(k) clearance.
2. Explain the role of The Joint Commission in accrediting healthcare facilities and its Environment of Care/medical equipment management standards.
3. Describe the role of ECRI Institute in independent medical device evaluation and hazard/recall alerts.
4. Identify the purpose of ISO 13485 and IEC 60601 standards for medical electrical equipment.

E. Patient Monitoring Equipment (5 items)
1. Explain the principle of operation of an electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG) monitor and lead placement.
2. Describe how pulse oximetry measures oxygen saturation using light absorption.
3. Identify components and function of a multiparameter vital signs monitor (NIBP, SpO2, temperature, respiration).
4. Explain the operating principle of an invasive blood pressure monitoring transducer.
5. Identify common alarm conditions and priority levels on patient monitors.

F. Diagnostic Imaging Equipment (5 items)
1. Explain how an X-ray tube generates ionizing radiation for diagnostic imaging.
2. Describe the basic principle of ultrasound imaging using piezoelectric transducers.
3. Identify major components of a CT scanner (gantry, detector array, X-ray tube).
4. Describe the basic principle of MRI using magnetic fields and radiofrequency pulses.
5. Explain radiation safety principles (ALARA — as low as reasonably achievable) for imaging equipment technicians.

G. Therapeutic & Life-Support Equipment (6 items)
1. Explain the operating principles and modes of a mechanical ventilator (volume-controlled, pressure-controlled).
2. Describe the function and safety checks of an infusion pump/IV pump, including free-flow protection.
3. Explain the operating principle of a defibrillator/AED, including energy selection and synchronized cardioversion.
4. Identify the function of an electrosurgical unit (ESU) and associated grounding pad safety checks.
5. Describe the function of a dialysis machine in filtering blood for patients with renal failure.
6. Identify the function and safety features of an infant incubator/warmer.

H. Sterilization & Infection Control (3 items)
1. Differentiate sterilization methods used for reusable medical equipment (steam autoclave, ethylene oxide, plasma sterilization).
2. Explain proper cleaning and disinfection procedures for equipment surfaces between patients.
3. Identify infection control precautions a BMET must follow when servicing equipment from isolation rooms.

I. Preventive Maintenance & Calibration (5 items)
1. Explain the purpose of a scheduled preventive maintenance (PM) program for medical equipment.
2. Describe the process of calibrating a device against a known reference standard.
3. Identify the use of a safety analyzer to test electrical safety parameters (leakage current, ground resistance).
4. Explain how equipment risk classification determines PM frequency (high-risk vs. low-risk devices).
5. Interpret a PM checklist and determine whether a device passes or fails inspection.

J. Troubleshooting & Repair Methodology (4 items)
1. Apply a systematic troubleshooting approach (identify symptom, isolate cause, test, repair, verify).
2. Interpret error codes displayed on medical equipment to identify a likely fault.
3. Differentiate a hardware failure from a software/firmware failure during diagnosis.
4. Explain when a device should be removed from service and tagged out per facility policy.

K. Test Equipment & Tools (3 items)
1. Identify the use of a digital multimeter to measure voltage, current, resistance, and continuity.
2. Explain the use of an oscilloscope to display and analyze waveform signals.
3. Identify specialized test equipment used for specific devices (defibrillator analyzer, ventilator analyzer, ESU analyzer).

L. Equipment Documentation, Inventory & Risk Management (2 items)
1. Explain the purpose of an equipment inventory and unique asset tagging in a medical equipment management program.
2. Identify required documentation for a work order, including incident reporting for equipment-related adverse events.
