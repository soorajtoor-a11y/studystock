HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — BIOTECHNOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of laboratory science techniques used in biotechnology research and
industry, including microscopy, aseptic technique and microbial culturing,
molecular biology (DNA/RNA structure and function, PCR, gel electrophoresis),
recombinant DNA and genetic engineering, laboratory measurement/solution
preparation, and the safety and regulatory framework governing biotech labs.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Laboratory Safety & Biosafety (6 items)
1. Identify biosafety levels (BSL-1 through BSL-4) and the organisms/precautions associated with each.
2. Identify personal protective equipment (PPE) required for biotechnology lab work (gloves, goggles, lab coat).
3. Interpret Safety Data Sheet (SDS) sections and chemical hazard pictograms (GHS symbols).
4. Describe proper handling and disposal of biohazardous waste (sharps containers, autoclave bags, red-bag waste).
5. Explain emergency procedures for chemical spills, exposure incidents, and eyewash/safety-shower use.
6. Describe standard precautions and universal precautions as applied in a research lab setting.

B. Microscopy (5 items)
1. Identify the parts of a compound light microscope (ocular, objective, stage, condenser, diaphragm).
2. Calculate total magnification (ocular power x objective power).
3. Differentiate types of microscopy (brightfield, phase-contrast, fluorescence, electron microscopy).
4. Describe proper care, focusing technique (coarse vs. fine adjustment), and storage of a microscope.
5. Interpret magnified specimen images to identify cell structures or stained microorganisms.

C. Aseptic Technique & Microbial Culturing (6 items)
1. Explain the purpose and steps of aseptic technique (flaming a loop, working near a Bunsen burner or biosafety cabinet).
2. Describe streak-plate and pour-plate methods for isolating bacterial colonies.
3. Identify types of culture media (agar plates, nutrient broth, selective/differential media) and their uses.
4. Describe proper use of an incubator (temperature, time) for microbial growth.
5. Explain Gram staining procedure and interpret Gram-positive vs. Gram-negative results.
6. Describe sterilization methods (autoclaving, dry heat, chemical disinfection) and their appropriate uses.

D. Cell Biology & Biomolecules (6 items)
1. Differentiate prokaryotic and eukaryotic cell structures and organelles.
2. Identify the structure and function of the cell membrane (phospholipid bilayer, selective permeability).
3. Describe the structure and function of macromolecules (carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, nucleic acids).
4. Explain enzyme structure and function, including the effects of temperature and pH on enzyme activity.
5. Describe the stages of the cell cycle and mitosis.
6. Explain the central dogma of molecular biology (DNA to RNA to protein).

E. DNA/RNA Structure & Function (6 items)
1. Describe the structure of DNA (double helix, nucleotides, base pairing rules: A-T, G-C).
2. Differentiate DNA and RNA in structure and function (deoxyribose vs. ribose, thymine vs. uracil).
3. Explain the process of DNA replication (semi-conservative, helicase, DNA polymerase).
4. Explain transcription (mRNA synthesis) and translation (ribosomes, tRNA, codons, amino acids).
5. Interpret a codon chart to determine an amino acid sequence from an mRNA sequence.
6. Describe common mutations (point, insertion, deletion) and their potential effects on protein structure.

F. Molecular Techniques: PCR & Gel Electrophoresis (7 items)
1. Explain the purpose and steps of polymerase chain reaction (PCR): denaturation, annealing, extension.
2. Identify the components of a PCR reaction (template DNA, primers, Taq polymerase, dNTPs, buffer).
3. Describe the purpose and setup of agarose gel electrophoresis for separating DNA fragments by size.
4. Interpret a gel electrophoresis result, including use of a DNA ladder/marker to estimate fragment size.
5. Explain the technique and purpose of micropipetting, including proper pipette tip selection.
6. Describe restriction enzyme digestion and how restriction fragment length is used to analyze DNA.
7. Explain the purpose of DNA extraction/isolation from cells prior to analysis.

G. Genetic Engineering & Recombinant DNA (6 items)
1. Explain the process of creating recombinant DNA using restriction enzymes and DNA ligase.
2. Describe the use of plasmid vectors to introduce genes into bacterial cells (transformation).
3. Explain the purpose and process of bacterial transformation, including selection with antibiotic-resistance markers.
4. Describe applications of genetic engineering (insulin production, genetically modified organisms, gene therapy).
5. Explain the basic process and applications of CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing.
6. Describe cloning techniques and the distinction between gene cloning and reproductive cloning.

H. Measurement, Solutions & Pipetting (5 items)
1. Convert between metric units commonly used in biotech labs (mL, µL, mg, µg, molarity).
2. Calculate dilutions and dilution factors (e.g., serial dilution, C1V1 = C2V2).
3. Describe proper technique for preparing a molar or percent solution from a stock solution/reagent.
4. Identify appropriate laboratory glassware and equipment for measurement (graduated cylinder, volumetric flask, micropipette).
5. Explain the importance of accuracy, precision, and significant figures in laboratory measurement.

I. Bioethics, Regulation & Quality (3 items)
1. Describe ethical issues associated with biotechnology (genetic testing, cloning, GMOs, gene editing).
2. Identify the roles of regulatory agencies overseeing biotechnology (FDA, EPA, USDA, NIH guidelines).
3. Explain the purpose of quality control/quality assurance and proper laboratory documentation (lab notebooks, chain of custody).
