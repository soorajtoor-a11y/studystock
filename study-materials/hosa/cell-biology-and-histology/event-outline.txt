HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — CELL BIOLOGY & HISTOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory cell biology and histology curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Cell structure and function at the organelle level, the cell cycle and cell
division, and the microscopic structure of the four primary tissue types
(epithelial, connective, muscle, nervous) that make up the human body.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Cell Theory & Microscopy (3 items)
1. State the tenets of cell theory.
2. Differentiate light microscopy and electron microscopy (TEM/SEM) and their resolution/magnification capabilities.
3. Describe common histological staining techniques (H&E stain, special stains) and their purposes.

B. Plasma Membrane & Transport (5 items)
1. Describe the fluid mosaic model and membrane components (phospholipids, proteins, cholesterol, glycocalyx).
2. Differentiate simple diffusion, facilitated diffusion, and osmosis.
3. Differentiate primary and secondary active transport (e.g., the Na+/K+ pump).
4. Describe endocytosis (phagocytosis, pinocytosis, receptor-mediated) and exocytosis.
5. Explain tonicity and its effect on cells (hypotonic, hypertonic, isotonic solutions).

C. Cytoplasmic Organelles (7 items)
1. Describe the structure and function of the nucleus and nucleolus.
2. Describe rough and smooth endoplasmic reticulum functions.
3. Describe the Golgi apparatus and its role in protein modification and trafficking.
4. Describe mitochondrial structure and its role in ATP production.
5. Describe lysosomes and peroxisomes and their digestive/detoxifying functions.
6. Describe the cytoskeleton components (microfilaments, intermediate filaments, microtubules) and their functions.
7. Describe centrioles, cilia, and flagella structure and function.

D. Cell Cycle, Mitosis & Meiosis (6 items)
1. Describe the phases of interphase (G1, S, G2) and cell cycle checkpoints.
2. Identify the stages of mitosis (prophase, metaphase, anaphase, telophase) and their key events.
3. Describe cytokinesis in animal vs. plant cells.
4. Describe the stages of meiosis I and II and crossing over.
5. Explain the significance of meiosis in producing genetic variation.
6. Describe apoptosis (programmed cell death) and its regulation.

E. Epithelial Tissue (7 items)
1. Classify epithelial tissue by cell shape (squamous, cuboidal, columnar) and layering (simple, stratified, pseudostratified).
2. Identify locations and functions of simple squamous epithelium (e.g., alveoli, capillaries).
3. Identify locations and functions of simple columnar and stratified squamous epithelium.
4. Describe transitional epithelium and its location (urinary bladder).
5. Describe epithelial specializations (microvilli, cilia, goblet cells).
6. Describe the structure and function of the basement membrane.
7. Differentiate exocrine and endocrine glands as epithelial derivatives.

F. Connective Tissue (7 items)
1. Describe the general composition of connective tissue (cells, fibers, ground substance).
2. Differentiate collagen, elastic, and reticular fibers.
3. Identify types of connective tissue proper (loose/areolar, adipose, dense regular, dense irregular).
4. Differentiate hyaline cartilage, elastic cartilage, and fibrocartilage.
5. Describe bone (osseous) tissue structure (osteon, lamellae, osteocytes, canaliculi).
6. Describe blood as a connective tissue (plasma, formed elements).
7. Identify major connective tissue cell types (fibroblasts, macrophages, mast cells, adipocytes).

G. Muscle Tissue (4 items)
1. Differentiate skeletal, cardiac, and smooth muscle tissue histologically.
2. Describe the striated appearance and multinucleated structure of skeletal muscle fibers.
3. Describe intercalated discs and their role in cardiac muscle.
4. Describe the structure of smooth muscle and its involuntary control.

H. Nervous Tissue (4 items)
1. Describe neuron structure (cell body, dendrites, axon, myelin sheath).
2. Differentiate neurons and neuroglia (glial cells) and identify glial cell types.
3. Describe the histological appearance of gray matter vs. white matter.
4. Describe the myelin sheath and nodes of Ranvier and their role in signal conduction.

I. Cell Signaling & Protein Trafficking (4 items)
1. Describe basic types of cell signaling (autocrine, paracrine, endocrine).
2. Explain receptor-ligand binding and signal transduction basics.
3. Describe the process of protein synthesis, folding, and secretion (ER to Golgi to vesicle).
4. Explain the role of molecular motors (kinesin, dynein) in intracellular transport.

J. Tissue Repair & Aging (3 items)
1. Describe the process of tissue regeneration vs. fibrosis (scar formation).
2. Explain factors affecting wound healing.
3. Describe cellular changes associated with aging (senescence, telomere shortening).
