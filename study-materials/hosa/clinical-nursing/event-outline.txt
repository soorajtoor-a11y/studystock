HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — CLINICAL NURSING (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of foundational nursing practice, including the nursing process,
patient assessment and vital signs, infection control, medication
administration basics, patient safety and mobility, hygiene and activities
of daily living, wound care, and the documentation/legal standards that
govern safe, patient-centered nursing care.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Nursing Process (ADPIE) (6 items)
1. Identify the five steps of the nursing process: Assessment, Diagnosis, Planning, Implementation, Evaluation.
2. Differentiate subjective data (patient-reported symptoms) from objective data (measurable signs).
3. Explain the purpose of a nursing care plan and how goals/outcomes are written.
4. Describe how a nursing diagnosis differs from a medical diagnosis.
5. Explain the purpose of evaluating patient outcomes and revising the care plan as needed.
6. Describe therapeutic communication techniques used during the assessment interview (open-ended questions, active listening).

B. Vital Signs & Physical Assessment (8 items)
1. Identify normal ranges for temperature, pulse, respiration, and blood pressure across age groups.
2. Describe proper technique for measuring an apical versus radial pulse.
3. Explain proper technique and equipment for measuring blood pressure (sphygmomanometer, stethoscope, Korotkoff sounds).
4. Identify factors that can cause inaccurate vital sign readings (cuff size, patient position, recent activity).
5. Describe proper technique for measuring oxygen saturation with a pulse oximeter.
6. Explain the components of a head-to-toe physical assessment.
7. Identify signs of respiratory distress (dyspnea, tachypnea, use of accessory muscles).
8. Describe pain assessment using standardized scales (numeric 0-10 scale, Wong-Baker FACES scale).

C. Infection Control & Standard Precautions (6 items)
1. Explain the chain of infection and methods to break each link.
2. Describe standard precautions and transmission-based precautions (contact, droplet, airborne).
3. Identify proper hand hygiene technique and situations requiring handwashing versus alcohol-based sanitizer.
4. Identify proper donning and doffing sequence for personal protective equipment (gown, gloves, mask, eye protection).
5. Describe proper technique for disposing of sharps and biohazardous waste.
6. Explain isolation precaution signage and visitor/staff requirements for each precaution type.

D. Medication Administration Basics (6 items)
1. Identify the "rights" of medication administration (right patient, drug, dose, route, time, documentation).
2. Identify common routes of medication administration (oral, topical, subcutaneous, intramuscular, intravenous).
3. Describe proper technique for administering an oral medication safely.
4. Explain the purpose of checking a medication administration record (MAR) before giving a medication.
5. Describe common medication measurement units and basic dosage calculation (mg, mL, tablets per dose).
6. Explain the nurse's responsibility when a medication error occurs (reporting, patient monitoring).

E. Patient Safety & Mobility (6 items)
1. Identify fall-risk factors and fall-prevention interventions (bed alarms, non-slip footwear, call light within reach).
2. Describe proper body mechanics for lifting, transferring, and repositioning a patient.
3. Explain the correct use of a gait belt during patient ambulation.
4. Describe proper technique for transferring a patient from bed to wheelchair.
5. Identify restraint alternatives and the legal/ethical considerations for restraint use.
6. Explain the purpose of a two-person or mechanical lift transfer for a dependent patient.

F. Hygiene & Activities of Daily Living Care (5 items)
1. Describe proper technique for providing a complete bed bath to a dependent patient.
2. Explain proper oral care technique for a conscious versus an unconscious patient.
3. Identify proper technique for perineal care and its role in infection prevention.
4. Describe proper positioning and support for a patient using a bedpan or urinal.
5. Explain how to assist a patient with dressing/grooming while promoting independence.

G. Wound Care & Skin Integrity (5 items)
1. Identify the stages of pressure injuries (Stage 1 through Stage 4, unstageable, deep tissue injury).
2. Describe interventions to prevent pressure injuries (repositioning schedule, skin assessment, pressure-relieving surfaces).
3. Explain proper technique for a sterile dressing change.
4. Describe signs of wound infection (redness, warmth, purulent drainage, fever).
5. Identify the phases of wound healing (hemostasis, inflammation, proliferation, maturation).

H. Documentation & Legal/Ethical Practice (4 items)
1. Explain the principle "if it isn't documented, it wasn't done" and proper charting technique.
2. Identify patient rights protected under HIPAA and the nurse's role in maintaining confidentiality.
3. Describe the elements of informed consent and the nurse's role in the process.
4. Explain mandatory reporting responsibilities (suspected abuse or neglect).

I. Nutrition & Fluid Balance (4 items)
1. Identify signs of dehydration and fluid overload in a patient.
2. Describe common therapeutic diets (clear liquid, low-sodium, diabetic, renal).
3. Explain proper technique for accurately measuring and recording intake and output (I&O).
4. Describe aspiration precautions for patients with dysphagia (thickened liquids, upright positioning).
