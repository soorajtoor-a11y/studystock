HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — CLINICAL SPECIALTY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of advanced clinical skills that apply across specialty practice
areas. The official test plan rotates a designated specialty focus
(for example, a specific body system or patient population) each competition
year, so this outline is built as a general advanced clinical-knowledge test
covering patient assessment, specialty-relevant terminology and anatomy,
diagnostic data interpretation, procedural and aseptic safety, professional
scope-of-practice judgment, and emergency response. Competitors should verify
the current year's announced specialty focus and layer that content on top
of this general foundation.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Patient Assessment & History Taking (7 items)
1. Describe the components of a comprehensive patient history (chief complaint, history of present illness, past medical/surgical history).
2. Explain proper technique for conducting a focused physical assessment relevant to a presenting complaint.
3. Identify appropriate open-ended and closed-ended interview questions for gathering patient history.
4. Describe the SAMPLE (Signs/Symptoms, Allergies, Medications, Past history, Last oral intake, Events) history format.
5. Explain how to assess a patient's level of consciousness (AVPU scale, Glasgow Coma Scale).
6. Describe proper technique for auscultating heart, lung, and bowel sounds.
7. Identify red-flag findings during assessment that require escalation to a supervising clinician.

B. Specialty Terminology & Anatomy Review (6 items)
1. Identify advanced medical terminology relevant to specialty body systems (cardiovascular, neurological, musculoskeletal, endocrine).
2. Describe relevant anatomical landmarks and structures used in specialty physical examination.
3. Explain terminology used to describe abnormal findings (e.g., edema, cyanosis, erythema, crepitus).
4. Differentiate acute versus chronic presentation of a specialty-relevant condition.
5. Identify common diagnostic tests and imaging studies associated with a specialty area (MRI, CT, EKG, echocardiogram).
6. Describe the roles of allied health professionals within a specialty care team.

C. Vital Signs & Diagnostic Data Interpretation (6 items)
1. Interpret vital sign trends to identify a patient's clinical status change.
2. Interpret a basic 12-lead EKG rhythm strip to identify normal sinus rhythm versus an abnormality.
3. Interpret basic laboratory value ranges relevant to a specialty condition (e.g., troponin, blood glucose, electrolytes).
4. Explain the clinical significance of oxygen saturation and capnography readings.
5. Describe how pain, level of consciousness, and vital sign data are combined to prioritize patient care (triage).
6. Explain how to document and report abnormal diagnostic findings to the healthcare team.

D. Procedural/Aseptic Safety Protocols (7 items)
1. Describe standard precautions and transmission-based precautions relevant to specialty procedures.
2. Explain proper hand hygiene and personal protective equipment selection for a given procedure.
3. Describe sterile field setup and maintenance during a clinical procedure.
4. Identify steps to prevent healthcare-associated infections (catheter care, central line care, surgical site care).
5. Explain proper technique for safe medical equipment handling and disinfection between patients.
6. Describe safe patient positioning and preparation for a specialty procedure.
7. Explain the purpose of a procedural time-out/checklist prior to an invasive procedure.

E. Scope of Practice & Professional Standards (6 items)
1. Explain the concept of scope of practice and how it varies by credential/license level.
2. Describe the elements of informed consent and when it is required.
3. Identify situations requiring referral or escalation to a supervising provider.
4. Explain confidentiality requirements under HIPAA in a specialty care setting.
5. Describe professional standards of conduct and appearance expected in clinical settings.
6. Explain the importance of interprofessional collaboration and clear handoff communication (SBAR).

F. Emergency Response & Triage Basics (6 items)
1. Describe the primary survey (ABCs: Airway, Breathing, Circulation) in an emergency assessment.
2. Explain basic triage categories used to prioritize patients (emergent, urgent, non-urgent).
3. Identify signs and symptoms of common medical emergencies (shock, anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest, stroke).
4. Describe basic first aid/BLS response until advanced help arrives.
5. Explain the purpose of an emergency action plan and chain of command during a code/emergency.
6. Describe proper technique for activating emergency medical services (EMS) and providing a concise situation report.

G. Equipment & Instrumentation Use (6 items)
1. Identify common specialty diagnostic equipment and its purpose (otoscope, ophthalmoscope, stethoscope, reflex hammer).
2. Describe proper calibration and maintenance checks for clinical monitoring equipment.
3. Explain the correct setup and use of oxygen delivery devices (nasal cannula, non-rebreather mask).
4. Describe proper use of an automated external defibrillator (AED).
5. Identify safety checks performed before using electrical or powered medical equipment.
6. Explain proper technique for cleaning, disinfecting, and storing reusable clinical instruments.

H. Documentation, Confidentiality & Legal Issues (6 items)
1. Explain the legal principle that accurate, timely documentation protects both patient and provider.
2. Identify elements that must be included in an incident report.
3. Describe mandatory reporting requirements (abuse, neglect, communicable disease).
4. Explain the difference between negligence and malpractice in a clinical context.
5. Describe patient rights regarding access to their own medical records.
6. Explain proper procedure for correcting an error in a medical record without falsifying documentation.
