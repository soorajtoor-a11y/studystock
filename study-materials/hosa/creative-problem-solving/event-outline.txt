HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — CREATIVE PROBLEM SOLVING (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the team/quiz-bowl round).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of the structured problem-solving process teams use to analyze a
health-related scenario, generate and evaluate creative solutions, and
communicate a recommendation as a team. Covers problem-solving models,
creative-thinking techniques, decision-making tools, group dynamics, and
applying these skills to case scenarios set in healthcare and health science
contexts.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Problem-Solving Process (10 items)
1. Sequence the stages of the problem-solving process (define the problem,
   gather/analyze information, generate alternatives, evaluate options,
   select and implement a solution, review results).
2. Differentiate a well-defined problem statement from a vague or biased one.
3. Explain why gathering facts before brainstorming solutions prevents
   premature closure on a single option.
4. Identify root-cause analysis techniques (5 Whys, fishbone/cause-and-effect
   diagram) used to define a problem accurately.
5. Distinguish symptoms of a problem from its underlying cause in a scenario.
6. Explain the purpose of setting evaluation criteria before comparing
   solution alternatives.
7. Identify barriers to effective problem solving (groupthink, anchoring,
   confirmation bias, jumping to solutions).
8. Explain the role of feedback and reflection in the final review stage.
9. Apply the problem-solving process to a short health-related case scenario.
10. Explain how time constraints affect which stage of the process a team
    should prioritize.

B. Creative Thinking Techniques (8 items)
1. Define brainstorming and identify its core ground rules (defer judgment,
   go for quantity, build on others' ideas).
2. Apply the SCAMPER technique (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to
   another use, Eliminate, Reverse) to generate solution ideas.
3. Explain how mind mapping visually organizes ideas around a central
   problem.
4. Differentiate brainwriting (silent, written idea generation) from verbal
   brainstorming.
5. Define lateral thinking and distinguish it from linear/logical thinking.
6. Identify the purpose of reverse brainstorming (identifying how to cause
   the problem to find ways to prevent it).
7. Explain how analogical thinking (borrowing solutions from unrelated
   fields) can generate novel healthcare solutions.
8. Identify techniques for overcoming creative blocks in a group setting.

C. Decision-Making Models & Tools (7 items)
1. Construct and interpret a weighted decision matrix to compare
   alternatives against criteria.
2. Apply cost-benefit analysis to choose between competing solutions.
3. Conduct a SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
   on a proposed solution.
4. Differentiate consensus decision-making from majority-vote
   decision-making.
5. Identify the steps of a rational/rational-actor decision-making model.
6. Explain how a pros-and-cons list can oversimplify a complex decision.
7. Explain the concept of an "opportunity cost" when selecting one solution
   over another.

D. Team Roles & Group Dynamics in Problem Solving (6 items)
1. Identify common team roles in a problem-solving group (facilitator,
   recorder/scribe, timekeeper, devil's advocate).
2. Explain how assigning roles improves the efficiency of a team's
   process.
3. Identify the stages of team development (forming, storming, norming,
   performing, adjourning) as they relate to problem solving.
4. Explain strategies for resolving conflict within a problem-solving team.
5. Explain groupthink and identify strategies to prevent it (assigning a
   devil's advocate, seeking outside opinions).
6. Explain how diversity of perspective within a team improves solution
   quality.

E. Communication Skills for Problem Solving (5 items)
1. Identify active-listening behaviors that support effective group problem
   solving.
2. Explain the importance of clarifying questions when a team member
   presents an idea.
3. Differentiate constructive feedback from criticism when evaluating a
   teammate's idea.
4. Explain how a team should structure a concise oral presentation of its
   recommended solution.
5. Identify nonverbal communication cues that affect team collaboration.

F. Case Scenario Analysis in Health Settings (8 items)
1. Analyze a short case scenario to identify the actual problem versus
   surface complaints.
2. Identify stakeholders affected by a health-facility or health-science
   problem scenario (patients, staff, administration, community).
3. Apply ethical considerations (patient confidentiality, informed consent)
   when proposing a solution to a healthcare scenario.
4. Identify resource constraints (budget, staffing, time, equipment)
   relevant to a proposed healthcare solution.
5. Evaluate whether a proposed solution to a health scenario is realistic
   and implementable.
6. Identify potential unintended consequences of a proposed healthcare
   solution.
7. Explain how regulatory or safety standards limit solution options in a
   healthcare setting.
8. Differentiate a short-term fix from a sustainable long-term solution in
   a health scenario.

G. Implementation & Evaluation of Solutions (6 items)
1. Identify the steps needed to create an action plan for implementing a
   chosen solution.
2. Explain the purpose of assigning responsibilities and timelines in an
   implementation plan.
3. Identify metrics or indicators used to measure whether a solution
   succeeded.
4. Explain the purpose of a pilot test or trial run before full
   implementation.
5. Explain why teams should plan for contingencies if a solution
   underperforms.
6. Explain how a team documents and communicates final results to
   stakeholders.
