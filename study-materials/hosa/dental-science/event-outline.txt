HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — DENTAL SCIENCE (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of dental anatomy and tooth numbering systems, oral anatomy,
dental instruments and equipment, infection control in the dental setting,
dental radiography basics, oral pathology, chairside assisting duties, and
preventive dentistry, reflecting the scope of practice of a dental assistant
or dental science professional.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Tooth Anatomy & Numbering Systems (7 items)
1. Identify the parts of a tooth (crown, root, enamel, dentin, pulp, cementum).
2. Identify the four types of teeth and their function (incisors, canines, premolars, molars).
3. Apply the Universal Numbering System (1-32) to identify a specific tooth.
4. Apply tooth surface terminology (mesial, distal, occlusal, buccal, lingual/palatal, incisal).
5. Differentiate primary (deciduous) and permanent dentition, including eruption sequence/timing.
6. Identify the components of the periodontium (gingiva, periodontal ligament, alveolar bone, cementum).
7. Describe the anatomical differences between anterior and posterior teeth.

B. Oral Anatomy & Landmarks (5 items)
1. Identify structures of the oral cavity (hard palate, soft palate, tongue, frenum, vestibule).
2. Identify the major salivary glands and their ducts (parotid, submandibular, sublingual).
3. Describe the muscles of mastication and their role in jaw movement.
4. Identify the temporomandibular joint (TMJ) and its function.
5. Describe the innervation relevant to dental anesthesia (trigeminal nerve branches).

C. Dental Instruments & Equipment (7 items)
1. Identify hand instruments used in a dental exam (mouth mirror, explorer, periodontal probe).
2. Identify instruments used for restorative procedures (amalgam carrier, composite instruments, curing light).
3. Identify instruments used for extractions and oral surgery (forceps, elevators, scalpel).
4. Describe the function of the dental handpiece (high-speed and low-speed) and associated burs.
5. Identify equipment used for moisture control (saliva ejector, high-volume evacuator, rubber dam).
6. Describe the setup and function of dental unit components (dental chair, operatory light, air/water syringe).
7. Identify instruments and materials used for impressions (impression trays, alginate, bite registration).

D. Infection Control in Dental Settings (6 items)
1. Describe standard precautions and PPE required for dental procedures (mask, gloves, eyewear, gown).
2. Explain proper sterilization methods for dental instruments (autoclave, chemical vapor sterilization).
3. Describe the difference between sterilization and disinfection, and appropriate uses of each.
4. Explain proper surface barrier and disinfection technique for operatory surfaces between patients.
5. Describe proper handling and disposal of contaminated sharps and biohazardous waste in a dental office.
6. Explain the purpose of sterilization monitoring (biological indicators/spore testing).

E. Dental Radiography Basics (6 items)
1. Identify types of intraoral radiographs (periapical, bitewing, occlusal) and their diagnostic purpose.
2. Describe principles of radiation safety and ALARA (As Low As Reasonably Achievable).
3. Explain the purpose of lead aprons and thyroid collars during radiographic exposure.
4. Describe proper film/sensor placement technique for exposing a diagnostic radiograph.
5. Identify common radiographic errors (cone cutting, elongation, foreshortening, overlap) and their causes.
6. Explain the purpose of panoramic radiography compared to intraoral radiography.

F. Oral Pathology & Common Conditions (6 items)
1. Describe the progression of dental caries (decay) from enamel to pulp involvement.
2. Identify stages of periodontal disease (gingivitis versus periodontitis).
3. Describe common oral lesions and conditions (aphthous ulcers, herpetic lesions, leukoplakia).
4. Explain the causes and presentation of malocclusion (Class I, II, III).
5. Describe the process of dental plaque and calculus (tartar) formation.
6. Identify signs of oral cancer and the purpose of an oral cancer screening exam.

G. Chairside Assisting & Four-Handed Dentistry (6 items)
1. Describe the principles of four-handed dentistry and instrument transfer technique.
2. Explain the dental assistant's role in preparing and mixing restorative materials.
3. Describe proper positioning of the patient, operator, and assistant during a procedure.
4. Explain the assistant's role in taking and recording vital signs and medical history updates.
5. Describe proper technique for suction and retraction to maintain a clear operative field.
6. Explain the dental assistant's role in patient education and post-operative instructions.

H. Preventive Dentistry & Patient Education (4 items)
1. Describe proper toothbrushing and flossing technique for patient education.
2. Explain the purpose and application of fluoride treatments and dental sealants.
3. Describe the relationship between diet (sugar/carbohydrate intake) and dental caries risk.
4. Explain the recommended schedule for routine dental exams and prophylaxis (cleaning).

I. Dental Materials & Charting (3 items)
1. Identify common restorative materials (amalgam, composite resin, glass ionomer) and their properties.
2. Describe standard dental charting symbols and abbreviations used to record findings.
3. Explain the purpose of a treatment plan and how findings are documented in the patient record.
