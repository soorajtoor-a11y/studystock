HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — DENTAL TERMINOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreakers).

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of dental anatomy, tooth numbering and charting systems, dentition,
dental specialties, preventive and restorative procedures, periodontal and
endodontic terminology, oral pathology, dental materials/instruments, and
radiographic and orthodontic terms used throughout the dental profession.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Tooth Anatomy & Structures (5 items)
1. Identify the parts of a tooth (crown, root, neck/cervix, apex).
2. Differentiate the tissues of a tooth (enamel, dentin, cementum, pulp).
3. Identify structures of the pulp cavity (pulp chamber, root canal, apical foramen).
4. Describe the function of the periodontal ligament and alveolar bone in supporting a tooth.
5. Identify anatomical landmarks on a tooth crown (cusp, fossa, cingulum, mamelon).

B. Tooth Numbering Systems (4 items)
1. Apply the Universal Numbering System (1–32 for permanent teeth) to identify a specific tooth.
2. Apply the Universal Numbering System letters (A–T) for primary (deciduous) teeth.
3. Apply the Palmer Notation method using quadrant brackets and numbers/letters.
4. Apply the FDI (ISO) two-digit notation system to identify quadrant and tooth position.
5. Convert a tooth identification between the Universal and FDI numbering systems.

C. Dentition & Tooth Types (4 items)
1. Differentiate primary (deciduous) and permanent (succedaneous) dentition, including eruption sequence.
2. Identify the four types of permanent teeth (incisors, canines/cuspids, premolars/bicuspids, molars) and their functions.
3. Describe the function of the third molars (wisdom teeth) and common indications for extraction.
4. Identify the typical number of teeth in the primary (20) and permanent (32) dentitions.

D. Oral Cavity & Surrounding Structures (4 items)
1. Identify structures of the oral cavity (hard palate, soft palate, uvula, tongue, frenum).
2. Identify the major and minor salivary glands (parotid, submandibular, sublingual) and their ducts.
3. Describe the anatomy and function of the temporomandibular joint (TMJ).
4. Identify the gingiva, mucogingival junction, and vestibule as soft-tissue landmarks.

E. Dental Charting, Surfaces & Directional Terms (5 items)
1. Identify the five tooth surfaces (mesial, distal, occlusal/incisal, buccal/facial, lingual/palatal).
2. Apply directional terms used in dentistry (mesial, distal, apical, coronal, proximal).
3. Interpret a line angle and point angle as used in cavity/restoration charting.
4. Interpret standard charting abbreviations for restorations and conditions (e.g., MOD, DO, PFM).
5. Identify quadrants and sextants of the mouth as used in periodontal and restorative charting.

F. Dental Specialties (4 items)
1. Differentiate orthodontics, periodontics, endodontics, prosthodontics, and oral & maxillofacial surgery by scope of practice.
2. Describe the focus of pediatric dentistry (pedodontics) and oral pathology as specialty areas.
3. Identify the role of a public health dentist versus a general practice dentist.
4. Differentiate the roles of a dentist, dental hygienist, and dental assistant on the dental team.
5. Identify the specialty responsible for treatment of malocclusion using braces and appliances (orthodontics).

G. Preventive & Restorative Procedures (4 items)
1. Describe the purpose and process of dental prophylaxis (professional cleaning).
2. Explain the purpose of fluoride treatments and dental sealants in caries prevention.
3. Differentiate types of dental restorations (amalgam, composite, inlay, onlay, crown, bridge).
4. Describe the steps of a routine restorative procedure (caries removal, cavity preparation, restoration placement).
5. Differentiate a direct restoration from an indirect restoration.

H. Periodontal Terminology (4 items)
1. Differentiate gingivitis and periodontitis by tissue involvement and reversibility.
2. Identify the purpose of scaling and root planing in periodontal treatment.
3. Interpret periodontal probing depth measurements and their significance for periodontal disease staging.
4. Identify signs of periodontal disease (bleeding on probing, gingival recession, tooth mobility).

I. Endodontic Terminology (3 items)
1. Describe the purpose and general steps of root canal therapy (pulpectomy/endodontic therapy).
2. Differentiate reversible and irreversible pulpitis based on symptoms.
3. Identify indications for pulp capping versus full root canal treatment.

J. Oral Pathology & Common Conditions (4 items)
1. Identify the process of dental caries formation (demineralization by bacterial acid production).
2. Describe the characteristics of a periapical abscess and its potential complications.
3. Differentiate malocclusion classifications (Class I, II, III) by molar relationship.
4. Identify common oral lesions and conditions (aphthous ulcer, leukoplakia, herpetic lesion).
5. Describe the process and terminology of tooth eruption disorders (impaction, ankylosis).

K. Dental Materials & Instruments (3 items)
1. Identify common dental restorative materials (amalgam, composite resin, glass ionomer) and their properties.
2. Identify basic dental hand instruments (explorer, periodontal probe, mouth mirror, scaler) and their uses.
3. Identify impression materials and their use in creating dental models/study casts.

L. Radiographic Terminology (3 items)
1. Differentiate types of dental radiographs (bitewing, periapical, panoramic, cephalometric).
2. Explain the principle of the paralleling technique in intraoral radiography.
3. Interpret radiolucent versus radiopaque appearances on a dental radiograph.

M. Orthodontic Terminology (3 items)
1. Identify components of fixed orthodontic appliances (brackets, archwire, bands, ligatures).
2. Differentiate malocclusion classes (Angle's Classification I, II, III) based on molar and jaw relationship.
3. Identify common removable orthodontic appliances (retainer, space maintainer, aligner).
