HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — EXERCISE SCIENCE (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory exercise physiology and kinesiology curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The body's acute and chronic responses to physical activity, covering
musculoskeletal, cardiovascular, and respiratory physiology, bioenergetics,
principles of training, biomechanics, body composition, and exercise testing
and prescription.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Anatomical & Physiological Foundations (4 items)
1. Describe anatomical position and directional/movement terminology (flexion, extension, abduction, adduction).
2. Identify major muscle groups involved in common movements.
3. Describe the structure of skeletal muscle (sarcomere, actin, myosin) relevant to contraction.
4. Explain the sliding filament theory of muscle contraction.

B. Muscle Fiber Types & Neuromuscular Function (4 items)
1. Differentiate Type I (slow-twitch) and Type II (fast-twitch) muscle fibers.
2. Explain motor unit recruitment and the size principle.
3. Describe the role of the neuromuscular junction in muscle activation.
4. Explain the mechanisms of muscle fatigue.

C. Bioenergetics & Energy Systems (6 items)
1. Describe the ATP-PCr (phosphagen) system and its duration/intensity of use.
2. Describe the glycolytic (anaerobic) energy system.
3. Describe the oxidative (aerobic) energy system.
4. Explain the interplay of the three energy systems during varying exercise intensities and durations.
5. Explain lactate threshold and its significance in endurance performance.
6. Calculate and interpret caloric expenditure using metabolic equivalents (METs).

D. Cardiovascular Response to Exercise (6 items)
1. Explain acute cardiovascular responses to exercise (heart rate, stroke volume, cardiac output).
2. Describe the Fick equation and factors affecting cardiac output.
3. Explain chronic cardiovascular adaptations to endurance training.
4. Interpret VO2 max and its significance as a measure of aerobic fitness.
5. Explain blood pressure response during and after exercise.
6. Describe blood flow redistribution during exercise.

E. Respiratory Response to Exercise (4 items)
1. Explain acute respiratory responses to exercise (tidal volume, respiratory rate, minute ventilation).
2. Describe the ventilatory threshold.
3. Explain gas exchange at the alveoli during exercise.
4. Describe chronic respiratory adaptations to training.

F. Principles of Training (6 items)
1. Explain the overload principle and progressive overload.
2. Explain the principle of specificity (SAID principle).
3. Explain the principle of reversibility (detraining).
4. Describe periodization and its phases (macrocycle, mesocycle, microcycle).
5. Explain the FITT principle (frequency, intensity, time, type).
6. Differentiate resistance training program variables (sets, reps, load, rest periods).

G. Resistance Training & Adaptations (5 items)
1. Describe skeletal muscle hypertrophy and its mechanisms.
2. Differentiate muscular strength, power, and endurance.
3. Explain neural adaptations to resistance training.
4. Describe types of muscle contraction (concentric, eccentric, isometric, isokinetic).
5. Explain delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS).

H. Flexibility & Biomechanics (5 items)
1. Differentiate static, dynamic, and PNF stretching.
2. Describe factors affecting range of motion and flexibility.
3. Apply basic biomechanical principles (levers, torque, center of gravity) to human movement.
4. Describe gait analysis basics (stance phase, swing phase).
5. Explain Newton's laws of motion as applied to human movement.

I. Body Composition & Nutrition for Performance (5 items)
1. Differentiate methods of assessing body composition (skinfold, bioelectrical impedance, DEXA, hydrostatic weighing).
2. Calculate body mass index (BMI) and interpret its limitations.
3. Explain macronutrient roles in exercise performance (carbohydrates, protein, fat).
4. Explain hydration and electrolyte balance during exercise.
5. Describe nutrient timing concepts (pre-, intra-, and post-exercise).

J. Exercise Testing & Prescription (5 items)
1. Describe components of a pre-participation health screening (e.g., PAR-Q).
2. Explain graded exercise testing protocols and indications for terminating a test.
3. Interpret the Borg Rating of Perceived Exertion (RPE) scale.
4. Explain the target heart rate/heart rate reserve (Karvonen) method for prescribing intensity.
5. Describe special considerations in exercise prescription for special populations (pediatric, older adult, chronic disease).
