HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — GENERAL CHEMISTRY (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA NGL/Cengage Academic Testing Center Guidelines 2025-2026. Test plan (content-area %) and reference are OFFICIAL. Item counts = official % applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official area.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (1 tiebreaker). Type: ATC (Academic Testing Center) textbook test.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Foundational chemistry: matter, reactions, bonding, and calculations.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Zumdahl & DeCoste. Chemistry, 11th ed. (ISBN 9780357850794). Cengage/NGL.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Concepts, Measurements & Calculations (moles, molar mass, energy) (10 items — 20%)
1. Perform measurements and mole/molar-mass/energy calculations.

B. Elements, Atoms, Ions & Periodicity (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain atomic structure, ions, and periodic trends.

C. Chemical Reactions, Energy & Solutions (15 items — 30%)
1. Balance equations; classify reactions; work with solutions and energy.

D. Composition, Quantities, Kinetics & Equilibrium (5 items — 10%)
1. Apply stoichiometry, reaction rates, and equilibrium.

E. Bonding Concepts (2 items — 4%)
1. Differentiate ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding.

F. Modern Atomic Theory (2 items — 4%)
1. Describe modern atomic/quantum theory.

G. Acids & Bases (2 items — 4%)
1. Explain acid-base concepts and pH.

H. Chemical Nomenclature (2 items — 4%)
1. Name compounds using IUPAC rules.

I. Properties of Matter (solids, liquids, gases) (5 items — 10%)
1. Describe states of matter and their properties.

J. Electrochemistry & Entropy (2 items — 4%)
1. Explain redox, electrochemistry, and entropy.
