HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — GENERAL CHEMISTRY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory college general chemistry curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Foundational chemistry principles including atomic structure, bonding,
chemical reactions and stoichiometry, states of matter and gas laws,
solutions, acid-base chemistry, thermochemistry, equilibrium and kinetics,
and basic nuclear chemistry.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Atomic Structure & Periodic Table (6 items)
1. Describe the structure of the atom (protons, neutrons, electrons) and isotopes.
2. Calculate atomic mass, mass number, and average atomic mass from isotope abundances.
3. Describe electron configuration and orbital notation (s, p, d, f).
4. Explain periodic trends (atomic radius, ionization energy, electronegativity).
5. Identify element classification by periodic table location (metals, nonmetals, metalloids, groups/periods).
6. Explain valence electrons and their role in bonding.

B. Chemical Bonding & Molecular Structure (6 items)
1. Differentiate ionic, covalent, and metallic bonds.
2. Apply Lewis dot structures to represent molecules and ions.
3. Predict molecular geometry using VSEPR theory.
4. Explain the polarity of bonds and molecules.
5. Differentiate sigma and pi bonds and single, double, and triple bonds.
6. Explain intermolecular forces (hydrogen bonding, dipole-dipole, London dispersion) and their effect on physical properties.

C. Chemical Nomenclature & Reactions (5 items)
1. Name and write formulas for ionic and covalent compounds.
2. Balance chemical equations.
3. Classify reaction types (synthesis, decomposition, single/double displacement, combustion).
4. Predict products of a chemical reaction given reactants.
5. Write and interpret net ionic equations.

D. Stoichiometry (6 items)
1. Calculate molar mass and convert between grams, moles, and particles.
2. Determine empirical and molecular formulas from percent composition.
3. Perform mole-to-mole and mass-to-mass stoichiometric calculations.
4. Identify the limiting reactant and calculate theoretical and percent yield.
5. Calculate the molarity of a solution.
6. Perform dilution calculations (M1V1 = M2V2).

E. States of Matter & Gas Laws (6 items)
1. Describe the kinetic molecular theory of gases.
2. Apply Boyle's law, Charles's law, and Gay-Lussac's law.
3. Apply the combined gas law and the ideal gas law (PV = nRT).
4. Explain Dalton's law of partial pressures.
5. Describe phase changes and heating/cooling curves.
6. Explain properties of liquids and solids (surface tension, viscosity, crystal structure).

F. Solutions & Concentration (5 items)
1. Differentiate solute, solvent, and types of solutions (saturated, unsaturated, supersaturated).
2. Calculate concentration in molarity, molality, and percent composition.
3. Explain factors affecting solubility (temperature, pressure, polarity).
4. Explain colligative properties (boiling point elevation, freezing point depression).
5. Describe the process of dissolution at the molecular level.

G. Acids, Bases & pH (6 items)
1. Differentiate Arrhenius, Bronsted-Lowry, and Lewis acid-base definitions.
2. Calculate pH, pOH, [H+], and [OH-].
3. Differentiate strong and weak acids/bases.
4. Explain titration and calculate concentration from titration data.
5. Explain buffer solutions and how they resist pH change.
6. Interpret an acid-base titration curve.

H. Thermochemistry (4 items)
1. Differentiate exothermic and endothermic reactions.
2. Calculate heat transfer using q = mcΔT.
3. Explain enthalpy and Hess's law.
4. Interpret a potential energy (reaction coordinate) diagram, including activation energy.

I. Chemical Equilibrium & Kinetics (4 items)
1. Write and interpret an equilibrium constant expression (Keq).
2. Apply Le Chatelier's principle to predict shifts in equilibrium.
3. Explain factors affecting reaction rate (concentration, temperature, catalyst, surface area).
4. Interpret a reaction rate law and order of reaction.

J. Nuclear Chemistry (2 items)
1. Differentiate alpha, beta, and gamma radiation.
2. Write balanced nuclear equations for radioactive decay and calculate half-life.
