HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — HEALTH INFORMATICS (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreakers).
Item counts per content area (from the official test-plan percentages) shown in
(parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of the systems, standards, and technologies used to capture, store,
exchange, and analyze health information, including electronic health record
(EHR) functions, health data standards and coding systems, interoperability
and health information exchange, data privacy/security, clinical decision
support, health data analytics, telehealth, and emerging health IT
technologies.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Foundations of Health Informatics (3 items)
1. Define health informatics and differentiate it from health information management (HIM).
2. Identify the roles of a health informatics specialist within a healthcare organization.
3. Describe the evolution from paper records to electronic health records driven by the HITECH Act and meaningful use.

B. EHR/EMR Systems & Functions (5 items)
1. Differentiate an electronic medical record (EMR) from an electronic health record (EHR) by scope of data sharing.
2. Identify core EHR functions (clinical documentation, computerized provider order entry/CPOE, e-prescribing, results reporting).
3. Explain the purpose of clinical documentation improvement (CDI) programs.
4. Identify the benefits and risks of copy-forward/copy-paste documentation in an EHR.
5. Describe the role of patient portals in providing patients access to their EHR data.

C. Health Data Standards & Terminologies (5 items)
1. Explain the purpose of HL7 messaging standards in exchanging clinical data between systems.
2. Describe the purpose of the FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) standard for modern health data exchange.
3. Identify the purpose of SNOMED CT as a clinical terminology for documenting diagnoses and findings.
4. Identify the purpose of LOINC in standardizing laboratory and clinical observation codes.
5. Differentiate structured data from unstructured data (e.g., free-text clinical notes) in a health record.

D. Coding & Classification Systems (5 items)
1. Differentiate ICD-10-CM (diagnosis coding) from ICD-10-PCS (inpatient procedure coding).
2. Explain the purpose of CPT codes in reporting outpatient procedures and services.
3. Identify the purpose of HCPCS Level II codes for supplies, equipment, and non-physician services.
4. Explain how DRGs (Diagnosis-Related Groups) are used for inpatient reimbursement classification.
5. Identify the role of a medical coder/coding compliance in accurate claims submission.

E. Interoperability & Health Information Exchange (4 items)
1. Define interoperability and differentiate its levels (foundational, structural, semantic).
2. Explain the purpose of a health information exchange (HIE) in sharing patient data across organizations.
3. Identify barriers to interoperability (proprietary systems, data silos, inconsistent standards).
4. Describe the purpose of an application programming interface (API) in connecting health IT systems.

F. Data Privacy, Security & HIPAA Compliance (5 items)
1. Explain HIPAA Security Rule safeguards (administrative, physical, technical) for electronic PHI.
2. Differentiate encryption, access controls, and audit trails as data security measures.
3. Identify the purpose of role-based access control in limiting EHR data access.
4. Describe steps required following a data breach involving unsecured PHI.
5. Explain the concept of the "minimum necessary" standard in accessing patient information.

G. Clinical Decision Support Systems (4 items)
1. Define clinical decision support systems (CDSS) and identify examples (drug interaction alerts, best-practice alerts).
2. Explain the risk of alert fatigue in clinical decision support implementation.
3. Describe how a CDSS uses clinical guidelines to generate a diagnostic or treatment recommendation.
4. Identify the role of computerized physician order entry (CPOE) combined with CDSS in reducing medication errors.

H. Health Data Analytics & Population Health (4 items)
1. Define population health management and its use of aggregated health data.
2. Differentiate descriptive, predictive, and prescriptive analytics in a healthcare data context.
3. Explain the purpose of a clinical data warehouse/registry in supporting research and quality reporting.
4. Identify how big data analytics supports value-based care initiatives.

I. Telehealth & Mobile Health Technology (3 items)
1. Differentiate synchronous (live video) and asynchronous (store-and-forward) telehealth encounters.
2. Identify the role of remote patient monitoring devices and wearables in mobile health (mHealth).
3. Describe licensure and reimbursement considerations unique to telehealth delivery.

J. Database Management & Health Data Structures (4 items)
1. Identify the basic structure of a relational database (tables, fields, records, primary keys).
2. Explain the purpose of data normalization in reducing redundancy in a health database.
3. Differentiate structured query language (SQL) queries used to retrieve health data.
4. Describe the purpose of metadata in describing and organizing health data.

K. Health IT Governance & Standards Organizations (3 items)
1. Explain the role of the Office of the National Coordinator for Health IT (ONC) in health IT policy.
2. Describe the role of HIMSS in health information and technology professional standards.
3. Identify the role of CMS incentive/reporting programs (e.g., Promoting Interoperability) in EHR adoption.

L. Emerging Technologies & Legal/Ethical Issues in Health IT (5 items)
1. Identify applications of artificial intelligence and machine learning in diagnostic imaging and predictive analytics.
2. Describe the potential use of blockchain technology for secure health data exchange.
3. Explain ethical issues surrounding algorithmic bias in AI-based clinical tools.
4. Identify legal issues related to data ownership, consent, and secondary use of de-identified health data.
5. Describe cybersecurity threats specific to healthcare systems (ransomware, phishing, medical device hacking).
