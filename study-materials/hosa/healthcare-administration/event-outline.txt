HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — HEALTHCARE ADMINISTRATION (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreakers).
Item counts per content area (from the official test-plan percentages) shown in
(parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of the structure and financing of the U.S. healthcare delivery
system, health information management, HIPAA and patient privacy, medical
coding/billing, regulatory and accreditation requirements, facility
organization and staffing, healthcare law and ethics, and the finance,
quality improvement, and policy functions of healthcare administration.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Structure of the U.S. Healthcare Delivery System (5 items)
1. Differentiate levels of care (primary, secondary, tertiary, quaternary) and give an example facility for each.
2. Identify types of healthcare facilities (acute care hospital, ambulatory surgical center, long-term care, hospice, urgent care).
3. Differentiate inpatient and outpatient care delivery models.
4. Describe the role of integrated delivery systems and accountable care organizations (ACOs) in coordinated care.
5. Identify the continuum of care and the role of care transitions/case management.

B. Health Insurance & Reimbursement Models (5 items)
1. Differentiate Medicare, Medicaid, and private/commercial health insurance in terms of eligibility and funding.
2. Differentiate managed care models (HMO, PPO, POS, EPO) by network restrictions and referral requirements.
3. Explain capitation, fee-for-service, and value-based/bundled payment reimbursement methods.
4. Identify the purpose of deductibles, copayments, coinsurance, and premiums in a health plan.
5. Explain the purpose of prior authorization and utilization review in reimbursement.

C. Health Information Management & Medical Records/EHR (5 items)
1. Differentiate a paper medical record from an electronic health record (EHR) and identify EHR components.
2. Identify the purpose of the master patient index (MPI) in health information management.
3. Explain retention requirements and ownership rules for medical records.
4. Describe the process and purpose of a release of information (ROI) request.
5. Identify the role of a health information management (HIM)/medical records department.

D. HIPAA & Patient Privacy (4 items)
1. Explain the purpose of the HIPAA Privacy Rule in protecting protected health information (PHI).
2. Explain the purpose of the HIPAA Security Rule regarding electronic PHI (ePHI) safeguards.
3. Identify permitted disclosures of PHI without patient authorization (treatment, payment, operations).
4. Describe breach notification requirements under HIPAA and consequences of noncompliance.

E. Regulatory & Accreditation Agencies (4 items)
1. Explain the role of the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) in healthcare regulation and reimbursement.
2. Describe the role of The Joint Commission in voluntary accreditation of healthcare organizations.
3. Explain the role of OSHA in workplace safety standards within healthcare facilities.
4. Identify the role of state health departments and licensing boards in facility and provider oversight.

F. Medical Coding, Billing & Claims Processing (5 items)
1. Differentiate ICD-10-CM (diagnosis), CPT (procedure), and HCPCS coding systems by purpose.
2. Describe the steps of the medical billing/revenue cycle (registration, coding, claim submission, payment posting).
3. Identify common reasons for claim denials (coding errors, lack of medical necessity, eligibility issues).
4. Explain the purpose of an Explanation of Benefits (EOB) versus a patient statement.
5. Identify the role of a clearinghouse in electronic claims submission.

G. Healthcare Facility Organization, Staffing & Credentialing (4 items)
1. Interpret a typical hospital organizational chart (governing board, administration, medical staff, departments).
2. Differentiate the roles of a hospital CEO, CMO, CNO, and department director.
3. Explain the credentialing and privileging process for medical staff.
4. Describe common staffing models and scheduling considerations (nurse-to-patient ratios, shift coverage).

H. Healthcare Law, Ethics & Patient Rights (5 items)
1. Explain the elements required for informed consent.
2. Differentiate an advance directive, living will, and durable power of attorney for healthcare.
3. Identify the four elements of medical malpractice (duty, breach, causation, damages).
4. Explain patient rights under the Patient Self-Determination Act and a facility's patient bill of rights.
5. Differentiate negligence, malpractice, and battery in a healthcare liability context.

I. Quality Improvement & Risk Management (4 items)
1. Explain the purpose of a continuous quality improvement (CQI) program in a healthcare facility.
2. Describe the purpose of root cause analysis following a sentinel event.
3. Identify the role of incident reports in a facility's risk management program.
4. Explain the purpose of patient satisfaction surveys (e.g., HCAHPS) in quality measurement.

J. Healthcare Finance & Budgeting (3 items)
1. Differentiate operating and capital budgets in a healthcare organization.
2. Explain the concept of cost centers/revenue centers in healthcare financial management.
3. Interpret basic financial statement terms relevant to a healthcare facility (revenue, expense, net income).

K. Human Resource Management in Healthcare (3 items)
1. Describe the recruitment, hiring, and orientation process for healthcare staff.
2. Explain the purpose of a performance appraisal/evaluation process for employees.
3. Identify components of employee compliance training (e.g., HIPAA, OSHA, infection control) required in healthcare settings.

L. Health Policy & Public Health Administration (3 items)
1. Explain the role of the Affordable Care Act (ACA) in expanding insurance coverage and consumer protections.
2. Describe the role of public health agencies (CDC, state/local health departments) in population health administration.
3. Identify how health policy affects access, cost, and quality of care (the "iron triangle" of healthcare).
