HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — HEALTHCARE ISSUES EXAM (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes.
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of current, significant issues affecting the healthcare industry:
access to and financing of care, quality and delivery of services, ongoing
healthcare reform debates, emerging public health and technology issues,
ethical considerations in health policy, and health disparities. Tests the
ability to evaluate multiple sides of contemporary healthcare policy
questions.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Healthcare Access & Coverage (9 items)
1. Explain the purpose and structure of Medicare (parts A, B, C, D) and
   who it covers.
2. Explain the purpose and structure of Medicaid and how eligibility is
   determined.
3. Identify the major provisions of the Affordable Care Act (ACA),
   including the individual marketplace and pre-existing condition
   protections.
4. Differentiate employer-sponsored insurance, marketplace/individual
   insurance, and government insurance programs.
5. Identify populations most likely to be uninsured or underinsured.
6. Explain the concept of Medicaid expansion and how it varies by state.
7. Identify barriers to healthcare access beyond insurance (transportation,
   provider shortages, language/cultural barriers).
8. Explain the difference between in-network and out-of-network coverage.
9. Explain the concept of essential health benefits required under the
   ACA.

B. Healthcare Cost & Financing (7 items)
1. Identify major drivers of rising healthcare costs (administrative
   costs, prescription drug prices, chronic disease prevalence,
   technology).
2. Differentiate fee-for-service from value-based/capitated payment
   models.
3. Explain the purpose of health savings accounts (HSAs) and
   high-deductible health plans.
4. Explain how deductibles, copayments, and coinsurance affect a patient's
   out-of-pocket costs.
5. Identify the impact of prescription drug pricing on overall healthcare
   spending.
6. Explain the concept of medical debt and its effect on patients.
7. Explain how healthcare spending in the U.S. compares to other developed
   nations relative to health outcomes.

C. Healthcare Quality & Delivery Systems (7 items)
1. Differentiate primary, secondary, and tertiary levels of care.
2. Explain the role of accountable care organizations (ACOs) in
   coordinating patient care.
3. Identify quality measures used to evaluate healthcare delivery (patient
   outcomes, readmission rates, patient satisfaction scores).
4. Explain the patient-centered medical home model of care delivery.
5. Explain the role of accreditation bodies (e.g., The Joint Commission) in
   healthcare quality oversight.
6. Identify causes and consequences of medical errors and strategies to
   reduce them.
7. Explain the concept of care coordination for patients with chronic or
   complex conditions.

D. Healthcare Reform & Policy Debate (7 items)
1. Identify arguments for and against a single-payer healthcare system.
2. Explain the ongoing policy debate over public option health insurance.
3. Identify major legislative milestones in U.S. healthcare reform.
4. Explain the role of state versus federal government in regulating
   healthcare.
5. Identify current proposals to address prescription drug price reform.
6. Explain the political and economic tradeoffs debated in healthcare
   reform proposals.
7. Explain how healthcare workforce shortages influence policy debates
   (physician shortages, nursing shortages).

E. Emerging Health Issues & Public Health Crises (7 items)
1. Identify lessons learned from recent public health emergencies (e.g.,
   pandemic preparedness and response).
2. Explain the growing role of mental health and substance use disorder
   treatment in healthcare policy.
3. Identify the public health impact of the opioid epidemic and policy
   responses to it.
4. Explain the rise of chronic disease management as a public health
   priority (diabetes, obesity, cardiovascular disease).
5. Identify emerging infectious disease threats and vaccination policy
   debates.
6. Explain the impact of an aging population on healthcare demand and
   policy.
7. Identify environmental and climate-related health issues affecting
   public health policy.

F. Health Technology & Innovation (6 items)
1. Explain the growth and regulation of telehealth services.
2. Identify benefits and risks of electronic health records (EHRs) and
   interoperability.
3. Explain how artificial intelligence is being applied in diagnosis and
   healthcare administration.
4. Identify privacy and security concerns related to health data (HIPAA
   compliance, cybersecurity).
5. Explain the impact of wearable health technology on preventive care.
6. Identify policy questions raised by genetic testing and precision
   medicine.

G. Ethics in Healthcare Policy (4 items)
1. Explain ethical tensions between individual liberty and public health
   mandates (e.g., vaccination requirements).
2. Identify ethical considerations in the allocation of scarce healthcare
   resources.
3. Explain informed consent as it relates to healthcare policy and patient
   rights.
4. Identify ethical debates surrounding end-of-life care policy.

H. Health Disparities & Social Determinants (3 items)
1. Identify social determinants of health (income, education, housing,
   environment) and their effect on outcomes.
2. Explain documented health disparities among racial, ethnic, and
   geographic populations.
3. Identify policy approaches aimed at reducing health disparities
   (community health centers, culturally competent care).
