HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — HOME HEALTH AIDE (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of the home health aide's role in providing personal care and
support to clients in the home setting, including activities of daily
living, infection control, vital signs measurement, client safety and fall
prevention, communication with clients and families, body mechanics, basic
nutrition, and home-care documentation.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Role & Scope of the Home Health Aide (5 items)
1. Describe the scope of practice and responsibilities of a home health aide versus a licensed nurse.
2. Explain the importance of following the client's individualized care plan.
3. Describe professional boundaries and appropriate aide-client relationships.
4. Explain confidentiality requirements (HIPAA) as they apply in a client's home.
5. Describe the members of the home care team and the aide's reporting relationship with a supervising nurse.

B. Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) (8 items)
1. Identify the basic activities of daily living (bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, eating, mobility).
2. Describe proper technique for assisting a client with bathing while preserving dignity and privacy.
3. Explain proper technique for assisting with oral hygiene and denture care.
4. Describe proper technique for assisting a client with dressing, choosing weaker-side-first technique for limited mobility.
5. Explain proper technique for assisting a client with toileting and perineal care.
6. Describe proper technique for feeding a client with limited mobility or swallowing difficulty.
7. Explain how to promote a client's independence while assisting with ADLs.
8. Describe proper technique for assisting with grooming tasks (hair care, shaving, nail care).

C. Infection Control in the Home (6 items)
1. Explain proper handwashing technique and situations requiring hand hygiene in the home.
2. Describe standard precautions and appropriate PPE use in a home care setting.
3. Explain proper handling and disposal of contaminated linens and personal care items.
4. Describe proper cleaning and disinfection of frequently touched surfaces and equipment in the home.
5. Explain signs of infection to monitor and report (fever, redness, drainage, odor).
6. Describe proper technique for safe handling and disposal of sharps in a home setting.

D. Vital Signs Measurement (6 items)
1. Identify normal ranges for temperature, pulse, respiration, and blood pressure.
2. Describe proper technique for taking an oral, tympanic, or axillary temperature.
3. Explain proper technique for measuring a radial pulse and counting respirations.
4. Describe proper technique for measuring blood pressure with a manual or automatic cuff.
5. Explain when a home health aide must report an abnormal vital sign to a supervisor.
6. Describe proper documentation of vital sign measurements in the home care record.

E. Client Safety & Fall Prevention (6 items)
1. Identify common fall-risk factors in the home (loose rugs, poor lighting, clutter, unstable furniture).
2. Describe home modifications that reduce fall risk (grab bars, non-slip mats, raised toilet seats).
3. Explain proper response if a client falls, including when to call emergency services.
4. Describe fire safety and emergency evacuation procedures for a client's home.
5. Explain safe use and storage of medical equipment and cleaning chemicals in the home.
6. Describe signs of elder abuse or neglect and the aide's mandatory reporting responsibility.

F. Communication with Clients & Families (5 items)
1. Describe therapeutic communication techniques for interacting with clients (active listening, empathy).
2. Explain appropriate communication strategies for clients with hearing, vision, or cognitive impairment.
3. Describe how to communicate respectfully and effectively with family members and caregivers.
4. Explain cultural competence considerations in providing care to diverse clients.
5. Describe appropriate boundaries when handling client requests outside the care plan.

G. Body Mechanics & Positioning/Transfers (6 items)
1. Explain principles of proper body mechanics to prevent aide and client injury (wide base of support, bending at knees).
2. Describe proper technique for repositioning a client in bed to prevent pressure injuries.
3. Explain proper technique for transferring a client from bed to wheelchair using a gait belt.
4. Describe proper use of assistive devices (walker, cane, wheelchair) during ambulation.
5. Explain proper positioning techniques (Fowler's, supine, lateral) and their purposes.
6. Describe signs that a client's mobility status has changed and should be reported.

H. Nutrition & Basic Home Care (4 items)
1. Describe basic principles of a balanced diet and common therapeutic diet modifications.
2. Explain proper technique for assisting a client with meal preparation within dietary restrictions.
3. Describe signs of dehydration or malnutrition to monitor and report.
4. Explain basic food safety practices in the home (safe food storage and preparation temperatures).

I. Documentation & Care Plans (4 items)
1. Explain the purpose of the individualized client care plan and the aide's role in following it.
2. Describe proper technique for documenting care provided, observations, and client status changes.
3. Explain the principle that accurate, timely documentation protects the client and the aide.
4. Describe the process for reporting changes in a client's condition to the supervising nurse or agency.
