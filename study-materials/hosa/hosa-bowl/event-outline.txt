HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — HOSA BOWL (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the team/quiz-bowl round).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
A quiz-bowl-style knowledge test covering HOSA organizational knowledge
(history, mission, structure, bylaws) alongside general health science
content: anatomy and physiology, medical terminology, current health topics,
and health science career pathways. Rewards broad, cross-disciplinary recall
across both HOSA-specific and health-science subject matter.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. HOSA History, Mission & Structure (8 items)
1. State the year HOSA was founded and its founding purpose.
2. Explain HOSA's mission of enhancing the delivery of compassionate,
   quality health care.
3. Identify HOSA's relationship to Career and Technical Student
   Organizations (CTSOs) and health science education programs.
4. Identify the levels of the HOSA structure (chapter, region, state,
   national/international).
5. Identify the two membership divisions (Secondary and
   Postsecondary/Collegiate).
6. Explain the role of the national HOSA Board of Directors.
7. Identify the purpose of the International Leadership Conference (ILC).
8. Explain how HOSA chapters are chartered at the local level.

B. HOSA Bylaws, Colors, Motto & Creed (6 items)
1. State HOSA's official colors and identify what each color symbolizes.
2. State HOSA's official motto.
3. Recite the opening line and core theme of the HOSA Creed.
4. Identify the components of the HOSA emblem and what each represents.
5. Explain the purpose of HOSA's bylaws in governing chapter operations.
6. Identify requirements for chapter and individual membership eligibility.

C. Parliamentary Procedure Basics (6 items)
1. Identify the order of precedence of motions (privileged, incidental,
   subsidiary, main).
2. Differentiate a main motion from an amendment.
3. Explain the purpose of a point of order.
4. Identify the vote threshold required to pass a main motion versus a
   motion to suspend the rules.
5. Explain the role of the chair in recognizing speakers and calling for
   votes.
6. Explain the purpose of tabling a motion.

D. Anatomy & Physiology (10 items)
1. Identify the major organ systems and their primary functions (circulatory,
   respiratory, digestive, nervous, skeletal, muscular, endocrine).
2. Identify the four chambers of the heart and the path of blood flow
   through them.
3. Differentiate arteries, veins, and capillaries by function and
   structure.
4. Identify the major divisions of the nervous system (central versus
   peripheral).
5. Identify the phases of the cardiac cycle (systole and diastole).
6. Identify the structures involved in gas exchange in the respiratory
   system (alveoli, bronchioles).
7. Identify the organs of the digestive system in order of food passage.
8. Identify the major endocrine glands and one hormone each produces.
9. Identify the layers of skin and their primary functions.
10. Identify the basic unit of the nervous system (neuron) and its parts
    (dendrite, axon, cell body).

E. Medical Terminology (8 items)
1. Identify common prefixes and their meanings (hyper-, hypo-, brady-,
   tachy-).
2. Identify common suffixes for conditions (-itis, -osis, -emia) and
   procedures (-ectomy, -otomy, -scopy).
3. Identify word roots for major organs and body parts.
4. Build a medical term from combining word parts (root, combining vowel,
   suffix).
5. Interpret common medical abbreviations (e.g., NPO, PRN, STAT, BID).
6. Differentiate terms describing directional anatomy (anterior/posterior,
   proximal/distal, superior/inferior).
7. Identify terms for common diagnostic procedures (radiography,
   endoscopy).
8. Identify terms describing common pathological conditions across body
   systems.

F. Current Health Topics & Public Health (7 items)
1. Identify major current public health issues (chronic disease prevention,
   infectious disease outbreaks, vaccination policy).
2. Explain the role of agencies such as the CDC and WHO in public health.
3. Identify social determinants of health (access to care, socioeconomic
   status, environment).
4. Identify major preventable chronic diseases and associated risk factors
   (diabetes, heart disease, obesity).
5. Explain the concept of health disparities among populations.
6. Identify basic epidemiological terms (incidence, prevalence, outbreak,
   pandemic).
7. Identify current trends in health technology (telehealth, electronic
   health records).

G. Health Science Career Pathways (5 items)
1. Identify the health science career clusters/pathways (therapeutic,
   diagnostic, health informatics, support services, biotechnology
   research and development).
2. Differentiate the scope of practice among common allied health
   professions (EMT, medical assistant, radiologic technologist).
3. Identify the general education/credentialing pathway for a physician
   versus a nurse.
4. Identify the purpose of professional certification and licensure in
   health careers.
5. Identify workplace safety standards relevant to health science
   professionals (OSHA, standard precautions).
