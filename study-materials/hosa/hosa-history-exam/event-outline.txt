HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — HOSA HISTORY EXAM (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes.
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of the founding, growth, mission, and organizational traditions of
HOSA-Future Health Professionals. Covers HOSA's early history and founding
leaders, its mission/vision/motto/creed/colors/emblem, national officer and
regional structure, conferences (state and International Leadership
Conference), its relationship to health science education, and historical
milestones.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Founding & Early History of HOSA (8 items)
1. Identify the year and state where HOSA was founded.
2. Identify the original name of the organization before it became HOSA
   (Future Health Professionals).
3. Explain the purpose behind HOSA's founding: to support students
   enrolled in health science education programs.
4. Identify the organization's early affiliation with vocational/health
   occupations education.
5. Explain how HOSA grew from a state-level organization into a national
   organization.
6. Identify the approximate date HOSA received its congressional charter or
   national recognition.
7. Identify key early leaders/founders involved in establishing HOSA.
8. Explain how HOSA's name changed over time to reflect its expanding
   scope (Health Occupations Students of America to HOSA-Future Health
   Professionals).

B. Mission, Vision, Motto, Creed & Colors (7 items)
1. State HOSA's mission statement regarding enhancing the delivery of
   compassionate, quality health care.
2. State HOSA's vision statement.
3. State HOSA's official motto.
4. Recite key lines and themes of the HOSA Creed.
5. Identify HOSA's official colors and what each color represents.
6. Explain the significance of the caduceus or medical symbol used in
   HOSA's branding/emblem.
7. Explain how the mission and creed guide chapter activities and
   competitive events.

C. National Officer Structure & Leadership (6 items)
1. Identify the national officer positions (e.g., President, President-
   Elect/Vice President roles, Secretary, and regional Vice Presidents).
2. Explain the process by which national officers are elected at the
   International Leadership Conference.
3. Identify the general term length and duties of a national officer.
4. Explain the role of the national Board of Directors in governing HOSA.
5. Identify the role of the executive director/national headquarters staff
   in supporting the organization.
6. Explain how state associations elect their own state officers.

D. HOSA Emblem & Symbols (4 items)
1. Identify the components of the official HOSA emblem.
2. Explain the symbolism of the colors used in the HOSA emblem.
3. Identify official HOSA publications or communication channels used to
   share organizational news.
4. Explain proper usage guidelines for displaying the HOSA name and emblem.

E. Regions, Conferences & ILC (7 items)
1. Identify how HOSA divides its membership into geographic regions.
2. Explain the purpose and general structure of a state HOSA leadership
   conference.
3. Identify the purpose of the International Leadership Conference (ILC)
   as HOSA's national conference.
4. Explain the process by which members qualify from chapter to state to
   national competition.
5. Identify major activities that occur at the ILC beyond competitive
   events (general sessions, keynote speakers, national officer elections,
   community service).
6. Explain the significance of HOSA's international membership and
   affiliated chartered associations outside the U.S.
7. Identify the general time of year the ILC is typically held.

F. HOSA's Relationship to Health Science Education (6 items)
1. Explain HOSA's role as the co-curricular Career and Technical Student
   Organization (CTSO) for health science education programs.
2. Explain how HOSA competitive events reinforce classroom health science
   curriculum and career pathways.
3. Identify the role of the chapter advisor, typically a health science
   instructor.
4. Explain how HOSA membership supports career and technical education
   (CTE) goals and the development of the total health science student.
5. Identify how HOSA aligns with national health science career cluster
   standards.
6. Explain the concept of the three-component model shared among CTSOs
   (classroom instruction, CTSO activities, supervised experience).

G. Membership Divisions & Chartered Associations (6 items)
1. Identify the membership divisions within HOSA (Secondary,
   Postsecondary/Collegiate, and alumni/professional divisions).
2. Explain eligibility requirements for each membership division.
3. Identify how local chapters become officially chartered with national
   HOSA.
4. Explain the role of a state association/state advisor in supporting
   local chapters.
5. Identify the process for a chapter to remain in good standing (dues,
   affiliation deadlines).
6. Explain the concept of an international chartered association within
   HOSA.

H. Historical Milestones & Past National Leaders (6 items)
1. Identify significant milestones in HOSA's growth in total membership and
   number of chartered associations over time.
2. Identify major historical changes to competitive event offerings over
   HOSA's history.
3. Explain the significance of anniversaries celebrated in HOSA's history
   (e.g., major founding anniversaries).
4. Identify how HOSA has expanded its scope to reflect emerging health
   science career fields over time.
5. Explain the historical development of HOSA's partnership with industry
   and postsecondary institutions.
6. Identify notable changes in HOSA's national headquarters location or
   organizational leadership over its history.
