HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — HUMAN GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026; content follows the event's
standard lifespan-development curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per stage are proportional estimates — confirm against the current
event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Physical, cognitive, and psychosocial development across the human lifespan,
including major developmental theories and each stage from prenatal to late
adulthood.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Theories of Development (8 items)
1. Explain Erikson's psychosocial stages.
2. Explain Piaget's stages of cognitive development.
3. Describe Freud's psychosexual stages.
4. Explain Kohlberg's stages of moral development.
5. Describe Maslow's hierarchy of needs.
6. Distinguish nature vs. nurture and continuous vs. discontinuous development.

B. Prenatal Development & Birth (5 items)
1. Describe the germinal, embryonic, and fetal stages.
2. Explain the influence of teratogens and prenatal care.
3. Describe the stages of labor and birth.

C. Infancy (birth–1 year) (5 items)
1. Describe physical growth, reflexes, and motor milestones.
2. Explain cognitive development and attachment.

D. Early Childhood (1–6 years) (6 items)
1. Describe physical and motor development.
2. Explain language and cognitive development (preoperational stage).
3. Describe psychosocial development (initiative, play).

E. Middle Childhood (6–12 years) (6 items)
1. Describe physical growth and motor skills.
2. Explain concrete-operational cognition.
3. Describe psychosocial development (industry, peers, self-esteem).

F. Adolescence (12–18 years) (7 items)
1. Describe puberty and physical changes.
2. Explain formal-operational thinking.
3. Describe identity development and psychosocial challenges.

G. Early & Middle Adulthood (6 items)
1. Describe physical changes and health in adulthood.
2. Explain cognitive and psychosocial development (intimacy, generativity).

H. Late Adulthood & Aging (5 items)
1. Describe the physical changes of aging.
2. Explain cognitive changes and dementia vs. normal aging.
3. Describe psychosocial aspects of aging (integrity vs. despair).

I. Death, Dying & Grief (2 items)
1. Explain Kübler-Ross's stages of grief.
2. Describe the grieving process and support.
