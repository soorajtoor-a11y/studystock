HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — HUMAN HEREDITY (GENETICS) (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
human genetics curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
The principles of human heredity, from Mendelian inheritance and pedigree
analysis through the chromosomal and molecular basis of inheritance,
mutations and genetic disorders, population genetics, and modern genetic
technologies.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Foundations of Heredity (3 items)
1. Differentiate genotype and phenotype.
2. Describe Gregor Mendel's experiments with pea plants and his conclusions.
3. Define key terms (allele, locus, homozygous, heterozygous, dominant, recessive).

B. Mendelian Inheritance Patterns (6 items)
1. Apply the law of segregation and the law of independent assortment.
2. Use a Punnett square to solve monohybrid and dihybrid crosses.
3. Calculate probabilities of offspring genotypes and phenotypes from a cross.
4. Differentiate complete dominance, incomplete dominance, and codominance.
5. Explain multiple-allele inheritance (e.g., ABO blood type).
6. Explain polygenic inheritance and continuous variation (e.g., height, skin color).

C. Pedigree Analysis (5 items)
1. Interpret pedigree symbols (affected/unaffected individuals, carriers, generations).
2. Determine the mode of inheritance from a pedigree (autosomal dominant, autosomal recessive).
3. Determine X-linked dominant and X-linked recessive inheritance patterns from a pedigree.
4. Calculate the probability of offspring inheriting a trait based on pedigree data.
5. Differentiate Y-linked and mitochondrial inheritance patterns.

D. Chromosomal Basis of Inheritance (5 items)
1. Describe the structure of chromosomes (chromatin, centromere, karyotype).
2. Explain the behavior of chromosomes during meiosis and how it accounts for Mendel's laws.
3. Explain sex determination in humans (XX/XY, the SRY gene).
4. Describe X-inactivation (Barr bodies, Lyonization).
5. Explain linkage, crossing over, and recombination frequency in gene mapping.

E. Chromosomal Abnormalities (4 items)
1. Describe nondisjunction and its consequences.
2. Identify common autosomal trisomies (Down syndrome/trisomy 21, trisomy 18, trisomy 13).
3. Identify common sex chromosome abnormalities (Turner syndrome, Klinefelter syndrome).
4. Interpret a karyotype to identify chromosomal abnormalities.

F. DNA Structure & Replication (5 items)
1. Describe the double-helix structure of DNA and complementary base pairing.
2. Explain semiconservative DNA replication and key enzymes (helicase, DNA polymerase, ligase, primase).
3. Differentiate leading and lagging strand synthesis, including Okazaki fragments.
4. Describe telomeres and their role in chromosome stability.
5. Explain DNA repair mechanisms.

G. Gene Expression & Regulation (5 items)
1. Describe transcription and translation (the central dogma).
2. Explain the genetic code and codon-anticodon pairing.
3. Describe gene regulation basics (promoters, operons, transcription factors).
4. Explain epigenetics concepts (DNA methylation, histone modification) and their effect on gene expression.
5. Describe alternative splicing and its role in protein diversity.

H. Mutations & Genetic Disorders (6 items)
1. Differentiate point mutations, insertions, deletions, and frameshift mutations.
2. Explain the effect of mutations on protein structure and function.
3. Describe single-gene (Mendelian) disorders (e.g., cystic fibrosis, sickle cell anemia, Huntington's disease).
4. Differentiate trinucleotide repeat disorders (e.g., Huntington's disease, fragile X syndrome).
5. Describe multifactorial/complex genetic disorders (e.g., diabetes, heart disease).
6. Differentiate germline and somatic mutations.

I. Population Genetics (3 items)
1. Apply the Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium equation to calculate allele and genotype frequencies.
2. Explain factors that disrupt Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium (mutation, migration, genetic drift, selection, nonrandom mating).
3. Explain the founder effect and genetic bottleneck.

J. Modern Genetic Technologies (5 items)
1. Describe the process and applications of PCR (polymerase chain reaction).
2. Explain gel electrophoresis and its use in DNA analysis.
3. Describe CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing technology and its applications.
4. Explain genetic testing and screening methods (carrier screening, prenatal testing, amniocentesis, newborn screening).
5. Describe recombinant DNA technology and gene cloning basics.

K. Genetic Counseling & Ethics (3 items)
1. Describe the role of a genetic counselor in interpreting risk and results.
2. Explain ethical considerations in genetic testing (privacy, discrimination, informed consent).
3. Explain the basics of gene therapy and its current applications and limitations.
