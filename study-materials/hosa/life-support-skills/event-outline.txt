HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — LIFE SUPPORT SKILLS (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the team/performance round).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of basic life support and first-aid skills for adult, child, and
infant victims: CPR, AED use, choking relief, bleeding/shock control, and
basic first aid for common injuries and medical emergencies, consistent with
current American Heart Association/American Red Cross guidelines.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Scene Safety & Emergency Recognition (4 items)
1. Explain the steps of scene size-up before approaching a victim (safety, hazards, number of victims).
2. Recognize signs that indicate a life-threatening emergency requiring immediate EMS activation.
3. Explain when and how to call/activate EMS (single rescuer vs. bystanders present).
4. Identify standard precautions (gloves, barrier devices) to use before providing care.

B. Adult CPR (7 items)
1. Assess responsiveness and breathing before beginning CPR on an adult.
2. Identify correct hand placement, compression rate (100-120/min), and depth (at least 2 inches) for adult compressions.
3. Explain the compression-to-ventilation ratio for one-rescuer and two-rescuer adult CPR.
4. Explain proper technique for delivering rescue breaths with a barrier device.
5. Identify signs of effective compressions and when to switch compressors to reduce fatigue.
6. Explain when to stop CPR (return of circulation, AED ready, provider exhaustion, scene becomes unsafe).
7. Differentiate hands-only CPR from conventional CPR and identify when each is appropriate.

C. Child CPR (5 items)
1. Identify the age range that defines a "child" victim for CPR purposes (1 year to puberty).
2. Identify correct hand placement and compression depth for child compressions (about 2 inches, one or two hands).
3. Explain the compression-to-ventilation ratio for one-rescuer and two-rescuer child CPR.
4. Explain differences between adult and child CPR technique and cause of arrest (respiratory vs. cardiac origin).
5. Explain when to use pediatric AED pads/settings versus adult pads on a child.

D. Infant CPR (5 items)
1. Identify correct hand/finger placement and compression depth for infant compressions (about 1.5 inches).
2. Explain the two-finger technique versus two-thumb-encircling-hands technique for infant compressions.
3. Explain the compression-to-ventilation ratio for one-rescuer and two-rescuer infant CPR.
4. Explain how to check for responsiveness and a pulse in an infant (brachial artery).
5. Explain rescue breathing technique differences for infants versus children/adults.

E. AED Use (5 items)
1. Sequence the steps for applying and operating an AED on an adult victim.
2. Identify safety precautions before delivering a shock (clear the victim, wet surfaces, medication patches).
3. Explain correct pad placement and when to use pediatric pads/dose attenuator.
4. Interpret AED voice prompts ("shock advised" vs. "no shock advised") and the required rescuer response to each.
5. Explain integration of AED use with ongoing CPR (minimizing interruptions to compressions).

F. Choking/Airway Obstruction Relief (6 items)
1. Differentiate signs of mild (partial) versus severe (complete) airway obstruction.
2. Perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver) on a responsive adult or child.
3. Explain the modified technique (chest thrusts) used for choking in infants, pregnant, or obese victims.
4. Explain back blows and chest thrusts technique for a choking infant.
5. Explain the sequence of care if a choking victim becomes unresponsive.
6. Explain self-administered abdominal thrust technique for a lone choking rescuer.

G. Bleeding Control & Shock (6 items)
1. Apply direct pressure and a pressure dressing to control external bleeding.
2. Explain when and how to apply a tourniquet for severe, life-threatening bleeding.
3. Recognize signs and symptoms of shock (pale/cool skin, rapid pulse, altered mental status).
4. Explain first-aid positioning and care for a victim in shock.
5. Explain care for a victim with an embedded/impaled object (stabilize, do not remove).
6. Explain nosebleed (epistaxis) first-aid technique.

H. Basic First Aid — Burns, Fractures, Wounds (6 items)
1. Classify burns by depth (superficial, partial-thickness, full-thickness) and describe appropriate first aid for each.
2. Explain first aid for suspected fractures, sprains, and strains (splinting, RICE).
3. Explain first aid for open wounds versus closed wounds.
4. Explain first aid for eye injuries and foreign objects in the eye.
5. Explain first aid for heat-related emergencies (heat cramps, heat exhaustion, heat stroke).
6. Explain first aid for cold-related emergencies (frostbite, hypothermia).

I. Legal/Ethical Considerations (3 items)
1. Explain Good Samaritan law protections and their general limitations for lay rescuers.
2. Explain the concept of implied consent for an unresponsive victim versus expressed consent for a responsive one.
3. Explain a lay rescuer's duty to act versus duty to continue care once started.

J. Special Situations (3 items)
1. Explain basic water-rescue safety principles (reach-throw-don't go) and care for a near-drowning victim.
2. Explain first-aid response to a suspected spinal injury before EMS arrival.
3. Explain first-aid response to an allergic reaction/anaphylaxis, including assisting with an epinephrine auto-injector.
