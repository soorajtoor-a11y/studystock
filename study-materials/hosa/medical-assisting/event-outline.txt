HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — MEDICAL ASSISTING (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of both the clinical and administrative duties of a medical
assistant in an ambulatory care/physician office setting, including office
administration, medical records and insurance/coding basics, patient
preparation and vital signs, infection control and sterilization,
phlebotomy and specimen collection, minor office procedures, pharmacology
basics, and legal/ethical responsibilities.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Medical Office Administrative Procedures (6 items)
1. Describe front-office duties of a medical assistant (scheduling, check-in/check-out, patient flow).
2. Explain proper telephone etiquette and triage for patient calls in a medical office.
3. Describe the purpose and components of an appointment scheduling system (matrix, wave scheduling).
4. Explain proper procedures for maintaining patient confidentiality at the front desk.
5. Describe basic office correspondence and filing systems for paper and electronic records.
6. Explain the medical assistant's role in managing inventory and ordering medical supplies.

B. Medical Records, Insurance & Coding Basics (6 items)
1. Describe components of an electronic health record (EHR) and proper documentation practices.
2. Explain basic health insurance terminology (premium, deductible, copay, coinsurance).
3. Differentiate types of health insurance plans (HMO, PPO, Medicare, Medicaid).
4. Describe the purpose of ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes and CPT procedure codes in billing.
5. Explain the medical assistant's role in verifying insurance eligibility and obtaining prior authorization.
6. Describe HIPAA requirements for protecting patient health information in records and billing.

C. Patient Prep & Vital Signs (6 items)
1. Identify normal ranges for temperature, pulse, respiration, and blood pressure across age groups.
2. Describe proper technique for measuring height, weight, and BMI.
3. Explain proper technique for positioning a patient for an examination (supine, prone, lithotomy, Sims').
4. Describe proper technique for obtaining and documenting a patient's chief complaint and medical history.
5. Explain proper technique for measuring pulse oximetry and visual acuity (Snellen chart).
6. Describe the medical assistant's role in assisting the provider during a physical examination.

D. Infection Control & Sterilization (6 items)
1. Describe standard precautions and PPE selection appropriate to a clinical task.
2. Explain proper hand hygiene technique and indications for handwashing versus hand sanitizer.
3. Describe the steps of instrument sterilization using an autoclave, including biological indicator testing.
4. Explain the difference between sanitization, disinfection, and sterilization.
5. Describe proper technique for maintaining a sterile field during a minor office procedure.
6. Explain proper handling and disposal of biohazardous waste and sharps in the office.

E. Phlebotomy & Specimen Collection Basics (6 items)
1. Identify venipuncture equipment and color-coded tube additives (EDTA, serum-separator, sodium citrate).
2. Describe proper technique for performing a venipuncture and capillary (fingerstick) blood draw.
3. Explain proper patient identification and specimen labeling procedures.
4. Describe common causes of specimen rejection (hemolysis, clotting, mislabeling, insufficient volume).
5. Explain proper technique for collecting a urine specimen (clean-catch midstream) and common point-of-care urinalysis testing.
6. Describe proper technique for preparing and handling specimens for outside laboratory transport.

F. Clinical Procedures & Minor Office Surgery Assisting (6 items)
1. Describe the medical assistant's role in preparing a patient and room for a minor office procedure.
2. Explain proper technique for assisting with wound care and suture/staple removal.
3. Describe proper technique for obtaining and processing an electrocardiogram (EKG).
4. Explain the medical assistant's role in assisting with minor surgical procedures (setup, instrument passing).
5. Describe proper technique for applying and removing bandages/dressings.
6. Explain proper technique for performing basic vision and hearing screenings.

G. Pharmacology & Medication Administration Basics (6 items)
1. Identify the "rights" of medication administration (right patient, drug, dose, route, time, documentation).
2. Describe common routes of medication administration used in the medical office (oral, topical, subcutaneous, intramuscular).
3. Explain proper technique for preparing and administering an intramuscular or subcutaneous injection.
4. Describe basic dosage calculation for oral and injectable medications.
5. Explain the medical assistant's scope regarding prescription refill requests and medication reconciliation.
6. Describe proper storage and handling requirements for medications and vaccines (refrigeration, expiration checks).

H. Legal/Ethical Issues & Professionalism (4 items)
1. Explain the scope of practice and legal limitations of a medical assistant (cannot diagnose or prescribe).
2. Describe the elements of informed consent and the medical assistant's role in the process.
3. Explain confidentiality requirements under HIPAA and consequences of a privacy breach.
4. Describe professional appearance, communication, and ethical conduct expected of a medical assistant.

I. Emergency Procedures & First Aid (4 items)
1. Describe basic first aid response for common office emergencies (syncope, choking, seizure).
2. Explain the medical assistant's role in responding to an anaphylactic reaction.
3. Describe the components of an office emergency action plan and crash cart contents.
4. Explain proper technique for activating emergency medical services (EMS) from the office.
