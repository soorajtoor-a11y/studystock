HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — MEDICAL LAW AND ETHICS (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026; content follows the event's
standard law-and-ethics curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Legal and ethical principles governing health care: the law, standard of care,
patient rights, confidentiality, ethics, licensure, and end-of-life issues.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Legal Concepts & Sources of Law (5 items)
1. Distinguish between criminal law, civil law, and tort law.
2. Explain sources of law (statutory, common, administrative, constitutional).
3. Describe the difference between laws and ethics.
4. Explain intentional torts (assault, battery, defamation, false imprisonment).

B. Standard of Care, Negligence & Malpractice (7 items)
1. Define standard of care and the reasonable-person standard.
2. Explain the four elements of negligence (duty, breach, causation, damages).
3. Distinguish negligence from malpractice.
4. Explain doctrines such as res ipsa loquitur and respondeat superior.

C. Provider–Patient Relationship & Contracts (5 items)
1. Explain the formation and terms of the provider–patient contract.
2. Describe the rights and responsibilities of providers and patients.
3. Explain proper termination of care and abandonment.

D. Patient Rights & Informed Consent (7 items)
1. Explain the elements and importance of informed consent.
2. Describe who may give consent (including for minors and incapacitated patients).
3. Explain implied vs. expressed consent.
4. Describe the Patient's Bill of Rights.

E. Confidentiality, HIPAA & Health Records (7 items)
1. Explain the purpose and key provisions of HIPAA.
2. Describe protected health information (PHI) and permitted disclosures.
3. Explain patient rights regarding health records.
4. Describe proper documentation and record retention.

F. Ethics & Bioethics (7 items)
1. Explain the core bioethical principles (autonomy, beneficence, nonmaleficence, justice).
2. Describe common codes of ethics for health professionals.
3. Analyze ethical dilemmas (e.g., allocation of resources, genetic testing).

G. Licensure, Scope of Practice & Regulation (5 items)
1. Distinguish licensure, certification, and registration.
2. Explain scope of practice and its legal significance.
3. Describe the role of licensing boards and mandatory reporting of misconduct.

H. End-of-Life Issues & Advance Directives (4 items)
1. Explain advance directives, living wills, and durable power of attorney.
2. Describe DNR orders and the determination of death.
3. Explain organ donation law.

I. Public Health Law & Reporting (3 items)
1. Explain mandatory reporting (communicable disease, abuse, vital events).
2. Describe public health authority (quarantine, immunization law).
