HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — MEDICAL MATH (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreakers).
Item counts per content area (from the official test-plan percentages) shown in
(parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Application of mathematical concepts used in healthcare settings, including
fractions/decimals/percentages, ratio-proportion and dimensional analysis,
metric/household/apothecary unit conversions, temperature and military time
conversions, medication dosage calculations, IV flow rate calculations,
weight-based dosing, and interpretation of vital signs and basic healthcare
statistics.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Numeral Systems & Basic Operations (4 items)
1. Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages.
2. Perform basic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) with fractions and mixed numbers.
3. Round a decimal value to the nearest tenth or hundredth as required for dosage accuracy.
4. Solve for an unknown value in a basic algebraic equation used in a dosage formula.

B. Ratio & Proportion / Dimensional Analysis (5 items)
1. Set up and solve a ratio-proportion problem to determine an unknown drug quantity.
2. Apply the means-extremes property to verify a proportion is correctly set up.
3. Apply dimensional analysis (factor-label method) to convert between units in a single calculation.
4. Solve a proportion problem involving concentration expressed as a ratio (e.g., 1:1000 solution).
5. Calculate the number of tablets needed to administer a prescribed dose using ratio-proportion.

C. Metric System & Conversions (5 items)
1. Convert between metric units of weight (mcg, mg, g, kg).
2. Convert between metric units of volume (mL, L).
3. Convert between metric units of length (mm, cm, m).
4. Apply metric prefixes (kilo-, centi-, milli-, micro-) to move a decimal point correctly.
5. Convert a physician's order written in one metric unit to the unit available on the medication label.

D. Household & Apothecary Systems & Conversions (4 items)
1. Convert between household measurements (teaspoon, tablespoon, cup, ounce).
2. Convert between apothecary units (grain, dram) and metric equivalents.
3. Convert pounds to kilograms and kilograms to pounds for dosing purposes.
4. Convert ounces to milliliters for a liquid medication order.

E. Temperature Conversion (3 items)
1. Convert a temperature reading from Fahrenheit to Celsius using the conversion formula.
2. Convert a temperature reading from Celsius to Fahrenheit using the conversion formula.
3. Identify normal body temperature ranges in both Fahrenheit and Celsius scales.

F. Military Time & Time Calculations (3 items)
1. Convert standard (12-hour) time to military (24-hour) time.
2. Convert military time to standard time, including correct use of AM/PM.
3. Calculate elapsed time between two clock times for medication administration scheduling.

G. Oral & Parenteral Dosage Calculations (6 items)
1. Calculate the number of tablets or capsules to administer given a physician's order and tablet strength.
2. Calculate the volume of an oral liquid medication to administer given a concentration and ordered dose.
3. Calculate an injectable dose in milliliters given a medication's concentration (e.g., mg/mL) and ordered dose.
4. Calculate a dose when the medication is supplied in a different unit than ordered, requiring a conversion step.
5. Calculate the number of milliliters needed to reconstitute a powdered medication to a desired concentration.
6. Determine whether a calculated dose is a safe/reasonable amount compared to a standard adult dose range.

H. IV Flow Rates & Drip Calculations (6 items)
1. Calculate an IV flow rate in milliliters per hour (mL/hr) given a total volume and infusion time.
2. Calculate an IV drip rate in drops per minute (gtt/min) given the drop factor of the tubing.
3. Convert between mL/hr and gtt/min using the tubing's drop factor (e.g., 10, 15, 20, 60 gtt/mL).
4. Calculate the infusion time remaining given a current flow rate and volume remaining in the IV bag.
5. Calculate a medication infusion rate given an ordered dose in mcg/kg/min and patient weight.
6. Recalculate an IV flow rate after identifying an infusion is running behind or ahead of schedule.

I. Body Weight & BSA-Based Dosing (4 items)
1. Calculate a weight-based pediatric dose given a dose ordered in mg/kg.
2. Calculate a patient's body surface area (BSA) using a standard formula or nomogram given height and weight.
3. Calculate a chemotherapy or other BSA-based medication dose given a dose ordered in mg/m².
4. Determine whether a calculated pediatric dose exceeds the maximum safe daily dose.

J. Vital Signs, Intake/Output & Statistical Calculations (5 items)
1. Calculate a patient's total fluid intake and output (I&O) over a shift given recorded values.
2. Calculate a patient's mean arterial pressure (MAP) given systolic and diastolic blood pressure readings.
3. Calculate a patient's body mass index (BMI) given height and weight.
4. Calculate a percentage change in a vital sign or lab value between two readings.
5. Calculate an average (mean) from a set of vital sign readings taken over multiple shifts.

K. Solutions, Dilutions & Concentration Calculations (3 items)
1. Calculate the amount of solute needed to prepare a solution of a specified percentage strength.
2. Apply the dilution formula (C1V1 = C2V2) to determine the volume of stock solution and diluent needed.
3. Calculate the final concentration of a solution after combining two solutions of different strengths.

L. Reading Charts, Graphs & Medical Abbreviations in Math Contexts (2 items)
1. Interpret a dosage chart or nomogram to determine a correct medication amount.
2. Interpret common medical abbreviations used in dosage orders (qd, bid, tid, qid, prn, ac, pc, stat) to determine dosing frequency or timing.
