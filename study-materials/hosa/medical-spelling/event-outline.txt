HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — MEDICAL SPELLING (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 written-spelling items, 60 minutes, plus an oral
spelldown round.
Item counts per content area (from the official test-plan percentages) shown in
(parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Accurate spelling of medical terminology encountered in clinical and health
science settings: word roots, prefixes, and suffixes; anatomy, physiology,
and disease/procedure terms organized by body system; pharmacological and
diagnostic terms; commonly misspelled and easily confused medical words; and
correct plural and eponym forms.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Roots, Prefixes, Suffixes & Combining Forms (6 items)
1. Spell common Greek- and Latin-derived word roots correctly (e.g., cardi/o, nephr/o, hepat/o, encephal/o).
2. Spell common prefixes correctly (e.g., dys-, brady-, tachy-, hyper-, hypo-, peri-).
3. Spell common suffixes for conditions correctly (-itis, -osis, -emia, -algia, -megaly).
4. Spell common suffixes for procedures correctly (-ectomy, -otomy, -ostomy, -oscopy, -plasty).
5. Apply correct combining-vowel rules when joining a root to a suffix beginning with a consonant vs. a vowel.
6. Spell multi-root compound terms correctly (e.g., gastroenterology, osteoarthritis, electrocardiogram).

B. Cardiovascular & Hematologic Terms (4 items)
1. Spell cardiovascular structure terms correctly (e.g., myocardium, pericardium, atrioventricular, aorta).
2. Spell cardiovascular condition terms correctly (e.g., arrhythmia, atherosclerosis, thrombophlebitis, aneurysm).
3. Spell hematologic terms correctly (e.g., erythrocyte, thrombocytopenia, hemophilia, leukemia).
4. Spell cardiovascular procedure/diagnostic terms correctly (e.g., angioplasty, echocardiogram, sphygmomanometer).

C. Respiratory Terms (4 items)
1. Spell respiratory structure terms correctly (e.g., bronchiole, pleura, epiglottis, alveoli).
2. Spell respiratory condition terms correctly (e.g., pneumonia, emphysema, bronchiectasis, pertussis).
3. Spell respiratory procedure terms correctly (e.g., tracheostomy, bronchoscopy, thoracentesis).
4. Spell respiratory diagnostic/measurement terms correctly (e.g., spirometry, pulse oximetry, hypoxemia).

D. Musculoskeletal Terms (4 items)
1. Spell skeletal structure terms correctly (e.g., vertebrae, scapula, calcaneus, metacarpal).
2. Spell muscular structure terms correctly (e.g., quadriceps, gastrocnemius, sternocleidomastoid).
3. Spell musculoskeletal condition terms correctly (e.g., osteoporosis, kyphosis, fibromyalgia, rheumatoid arthritis).
4. Spell musculoskeletal procedure terms correctly (e.g., arthroscopy, osteotomy, arthroplasty).

E. Nervous System & Special Senses Terms (4 items)
1. Spell nervous system structure terms correctly (e.g., cerebellum, meninges, myelin, hypothalamus).
2. Spell neurological condition terms correctly (e.g., aneurysm, seizure, hemiparesis, Alzheimer's).
3. Spell ophthalmic and otic structure/condition terms correctly (e.g., conjunctiva, tympanic membrane, glaucoma, tinnitus).
4. Spell neurological diagnostic terms correctly (e.g., electroencephalogram, cerebrospinal, myelogram).

F. Digestive & Gastrointestinal Terms (4 items)
1. Spell digestive structure terms correctly (e.g., esophagus, duodenum, pancreas, peritoneum).
2. Spell GI condition terms correctly (e.g., diverticulitis, cholecystitis, gastroesophageal, cirrhosis).
3. Spell GI procedure terms correctly (e.g., colonoscopy, cholecystectomy, gastrostomy).
4. Spell digestive diagnostic/lab terms correctly (e.g., bilirubin, occult, endoscopy).

G. Endocrine & Reproductive Terms (4 items)
1. Spell endocrine gland/hormone terms correctly (e.g., pituitary, parathyroid, glucagon, aldosterone).
2. Spell endocrine condition terms correctly (e.g., hyperthyroidism, hypoglycemia, acromegaly).
3. Spell reproductive structure terms correctly (e.g., endometrium, spermatogenesis, fallopian).
4. Spell obstetric/reproductive condition terms correctly (e.g., preeclampsia, endometriosis, dysmenorrhea).

H. Integumentary & Urinary Terms (4 items)
1. Spell integumentary structure/condition terms correctly (e.g., epidermis, sebaceous, psoriasis, cellulitis).
2. Spell integumentary procedure terms correctly (e.g., debridement, dermabrasion).
3. Spell urinary structure terms correctly (e.g., ureter, urethra, glomerulus, nephron).
4. Spell urinary condition/procedure terms correctly (e.g., pyelonephritis, hematuria, cystoscopy, nephrolithiasis).

I. Diagnostic, Surgical & Pharmacological Terms (6 items)
1. Spell diagnostic imaging terms correctly (e.g., fluoroscopy, ultrasonography, magnetic resonance).
2. Spell laboratory/pathology terms correctly (e.g., histology, biopsy, hemoglobin, differential).
3. Spell surgical terms correctly (e.g., anastomosis, laparoscopic, anesthesia, hemostasis).
4. Spell drug classification terms correctly (e.g., anticoagulant, antihypertensive, antipyretic, bronchodilator).
5. Spell dosage-form and route terms correctly (e.g., suppository, subcutaneous, sublingual, intramuscular).
6. Spell common pharmacologic suffix terms correctly (e.g., -mab, -olol, -pril, -statin classes as generic term endings).

J. Commonly Confused & Frequently Misspelled Terms (6 items)
1. Correctly spell sound-alike medical term pairs (e.g., ilium vs. ileum, perineal vs. peroneal, mucous vs. mucus).
2. Correctly spell terms with silent or unexpected letters (e.g., pneumonia, phlegm, diarrhea, rhinorrhea).
3. Correctly spell terms with commonly transposed letters (e.g., pelvic, epiglottis, sphygmomanometer).
4. Correctly spell terms distinguished by a single letter with different meanings (e.g., abduction vs. adduction, hypertrophy vs. atrophy).
5. Correctly spell terms with silent "p," "h," or "g" combinations (e.g., psychiatry, phthisis, gnathic).
6. Correctly spell terms frequently misspelled due to double consonants (e.g., appendicitis, tonsillitis, cellulitis).

K. Plural Forms & Eponyms (4 items)
1. Form the correct plural of a medical term ending in -is (e.g., diagnosis to diagnoses, metastasis to metastases).
2. Form the correct plural of a medical term ending in -um or -on (e.g., bacterium to bacteria, ganglion to ganglia).
3. Form the correct plural of a medical term ending in -a or -ix/-ex (e.g., vertebra to vertebrae, appendix to appendices).
4. Spell eponymous disease and syndrome names correctly (e.g., Parkinson's disease, Down syndrome, Alzheimer's disease).
