HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — MEDICAL TERMINOLOGY (ACADEMIC) (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
academic medical terminology curriculum emphasizing word construction and
usage across all body systems.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Systematic study of medical language as an academic linguistics topic: how
medical terms are built from word roots, prefixes, and suffixes, how they are
applied across body systems, and how abbreviations and pharmacologic terms
are used and interpreted correctly.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Word Building Fundamentals (6 items)
1. Define word root, combining vowel, prefix, and suffix as elements of a medical term.
2. Identify the combining form of a given word root (e.g., cardi/o, hepat/o, gastr/o).
3. Determine when a combining vowel is used versus omitted before a suffix.
4. Break an unfamiliar medical term into its component parts to derive its meaning.
5. Build a medical term from a given definition using correct word elements.
6. Distinguish singular and plural forms of medical terms (e.g., diagnosis/diagnoses, vertebra/vertebrae).

B. Rules of Term Construction & Combining Forms (4 items)
1. Apply the rule for linking two roots with a combining vowel.
2. Apply the rule for attaching a suffix beginning with a vowel to a root.
3. Identify common prefixes of position, direction, and number (hyper-, hypo-, brady-, tachy-, poly-).
4. Differentiate similar-sounding word roots with different meanings (e.g., my/o vs. myel/o).

C. Directional Terms, Body Planes & Regions (4 items)
1. Identify anatomical directional terms (superior, inferior, proximal, distal, medial, lateral).
2. Identify the body planes (sagittal, frontal/coronal, transverse) and what each divides.
3. Identify the abdominopelvic quadrants and regions.
4. Identify combining forms for body cavities and regions (crani/o, thorac/o, abdomin/o).

D. Integumentary & Musculoskeletal Terminology (5 items)
1. Identify word roots and terms for skin structures and lesions (derm/o, cutane/o, -itis, -osis).
2. Identify word roots for bones, joints, and cartilage (oste/o, arthr/o, chondr/o).
3. Identify word roots and suffixes for muscle terminology (my/o, -trophy, -asthenia).
4. Build and define terms for common musculoskeletal conditions (osteoarthritis, myalgia, kyphosis).
5. Identify surgical and diagnostic suffixes applied to musculoskeletal terms (-desis, -centesis, -scopy).

E. Cardiovascular & Respiratory Terminology (5 items)
1. Identify word roots for heart and vessel structures (cardi/o, angi/o, phleb/o, ather/o).
2. Build and define terms for cardiovascular conditions (tachycardia, arrhythmia, atherosclerosis).
3. Identify word roots for respiratory structures (pulmon/o, bronch/o, pneum/o, laryng/o).
4. Build and define terms for respiratory conditions (dyspnea, bronchitis, pneumothorax).
5. Differentiate terms with similar roots across the two systems (e.g., -pnea vs. -phonia).

F. Digestive & Urinary Terminology (5 items)
1. Identify word roots for digestive organs (gastr/o, hepat/o, enter/o, chol/e, col/o).
2. Build and define terms for digestive conditions (gastritis, hepatomegaly, cholecystitis).
3. Identify word roots for urinary structures (nephr/o, ren/o, cyst/o, ureter/o).
4. Build and define terms for urinary conditions (nephrolithiasis, cystitis, hematuria).
5. Identify suffixes indicating surgical procedures on digestive/urinary organs (-ectomy, -ostomy, -otomy).

G. Nervous System & Special Senses Terminology (4 items)
1. Identify word roots for nervous system structures (neur/o, cerebr/o, encephal/o, myel/o).
2. Build and define terms for neurologic conditions (encephalitis, neuropathy, hemiplegia).
3. Identify word roots for the eye and ear (ophthalm/o, opt/o, ot/o, audi/o).
4. Build and define terms for sensory conditions (otitis, conjunctivitis, presbyopia).

H. Endocrine & Reproductive Terminology (4 items)
1. Identify word roots for endocrine glands (thyr/o, adren/o, pancreat/o).
2. Build and define terms for endocrine conditions (hyperthyroidism, hypoglycemia).
3. Identify word roots for male and female reproductive structures (orch/o, hyster/o, oophor/o, prostat/o).
4. Build and define common reproductive/obstetric terms (dysmenorrhea, prenatal, nulligravida).

I. Pathology, Diagnostic & Surgical Suffixes/Terms (6 items)
1. Identify general pathology suffixes and their meanings (-itis, -osis, -oma, -pathy, -algia).
2. Identify diagnostic procedure suffixes (-scopy, -graphy, -gram, -metry).
3. Identify surgical suffixes and differentiate their meanings (-ectomy, -otomy, -ostomy, -plasty, -rrhaphy).
4. Differentiate terms describing benign versus malignant growths (-oma vs. carcinoma, sarcoma).
5. Build a diagnostic or surgical term from a described procedure.
6. Identify prefixes indicating abnormal quantity or size (hyper-, hypo-, macro-, micro-).

J. Abbreviations, Symbols & Pharmacologic Terminology (7 items)
1. Interpret common clinical abbreviations (NPO, PRN, STAT, BID, TID, QID).
2. Interpret abbreviations for routes of administration (PO, IV, IM, SubQ).
3. Interpret common symbols used in clinical documentation (>, <, ↑, ↓, ā, p̄).
4. Identify error-prone abbreviations on official "do not use" lists and their preferred alternatives.
5. Identify word roots and suffixes related to pharmacology (-cillin, pharmac/o, -therapy).
6. Interpret abbreviations for units and frequency found on medication orders (mg, mcg, mL, q, hr).
7. Translate a complete abbreviated clinical order into plain-language terminology.
