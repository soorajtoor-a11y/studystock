HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — MICROBIOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory college microbiology curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Structure, classification, growth, and genetics of microorganisms, along with
the mechanisms of infection, host immune response, and the agents used to
control microbial growth in clinical and laboratory settings.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Introduction to Microbiology & Microscopy (3 items)
1. Describe the historical development of microbiology (spontaneous generation, germ theory, Koch's postulates).
2. Compare resolution and magnification principles of light, phase-contrast, and electron microscopy.
3. Identify the major groups of microorganisms studied (bacteria, archaea, fungi, protozoa, viruses, helminths).

B. Chemistry of Life & Biomolecules Relevant to Microbes (2 items)
1. Describe the macromolecules relevant to microbial structure and metabolism (nucleic acids, proteins, lipids, carbohydrates).
2. Explain enzyme structure and function in microbial metabolism.

C. Prokaryotic Cell Structure (5 items)
1. Identify structural components of a bacterial cell (cell wall, plasma membrane, nucleoid, ribosomes, capsule, flagella, pili).
2. Differentiate Gram-positive and Gram-negative cell wall structure.
3. Describe the function of the bacterial capsule, flagella, and pili in pathogenesis and motility.
4. Explain the formation and significance of bacterial endospores.
5. Compare prokaryotic and eukaryotic cell structure.

D. Microbial Classification & Taxonomy (4 items)
1. Apply binomial nomenclature to classify and name microorganisms.
2. Identify criteria used to classify bacteria (morphology, staining, biochemical, genetic).
3. Describe the three-domain system (Bacteria, Archaea, Eukarya).
4. Differentiate major bacterial shapes and arrangements (cocci, bacilli, spirilla; diplo-, staphylo-, strepto-).

E. Bacterial Growth & Nutrition (5 items)
1. Describe requirements for bacterial growth (nutrients, temperature, pH, oxygen).
2. Classify bacteria by oxygen requirement (obligate aerobe, anaerobe, facultative anaerobe, microaerophile).
3. Describe the phases of the bacterial growth curve (lag, log, stationary, death).
4. Calculate bacterial population growth using binary fission and generation time.
5. Describe methods of culturing and enumerating bacteria (streak plate, serial dilution, colony counts).

F. Microbial Genetics (5 items)
1. Describe the structure and replication of bacterial DNA and plasmids.
2. Explain types of mutation and their effects on microbial phenotype.
3. Differentiate mechanisms of horizontal gene transfer (transformation, transduction, conjugation).
4. Explain the role of transposons in genetic variability.
5. Describe how genetic recombination contributes to antibiotic resistance spread.

G. Control of Microbial Growth (Physical & Chemical Methods) (4 items)
1. Differentiate sterilization, disinfection, antisepsis, and sanitization.
2. Describe physical methods of microbial control (autoclaving, pasteurization, filtration, radiation).
3. Describe chemical agents used for disinfection and antisepsis (alcohols, halogens, phenolics).
4. Explain factors affecting the effectiveness of a physical or chemical control method.

H. Antimicrobial Agents & Resistance (5 items)
1. Classify antimicrobial agents by mechanism of action (cell wall synthesis, protein synthesis, nucleic acid synthesis inhibitors).
2. Differentiate bacteriostatic and bactericidal action.
3. Explain the principle and interpretation of antimicrobial susceptibility testing (Kirby-Bauer disk diffusion).
4. Describe mechanisms of antibiotic resistance (enzymatic inactivation, efflux pumps, target modification).
5. Explain the public health significance of multidrug-resistant organisms (MRSA, VRE).

I. Viruses & Viral Replication (5 items)
1. Describe general viral structure (capsid, envelope, genome types).
2. Differentiate the lytic and lysogenic cycles of bacteriophage replication.
3. Describe the general steps of animal virus replication (attachment, penetration, uncoating, synthesis, assembly, release).
4. Differentiate DNA and RNA viruses and explain the role of reverse transcriptase in retroviruses.
5. Describe methods of viral cultivation and detection.

J. Fungi, Protozoa & Helminths (4 items)
1. Describe general characteristics of fungi and differentiate yeasts from molds.
2. Identify major categories of pathogenic fungi and the diseases they cause (mycoses).
3. Describe general characteristics and classification of protozoa and their modes of transmission.
4. Describe general characteristics of parasitic helminths and their life cycles.

K. Host-Pathogen Interactions & Pathogenesis (4 items)
1. Differentiate normal microbiota from pathogens and describe opportunistic infection.
2. Describe mechanisms of bacterial pathogenicity (adhesion, invasion, toxin production).
3. Differentiate exotoxins and endotoxins by source and mechanism of action.
4. Describe the chain of infection and modes of disease transmission.

L. Immunology & Immune Response to Microbes (4 items)
1. Differentiate innate and adaptive immune defenses against microorganisms.
2. Describe the role of phagocytes, complement, and inflammation in innate immunity.
3. Differentiate humoral (antibody-mediated) and cell-mediated adaptive immunity.
4. Explain the principles of active and passive immunization.
