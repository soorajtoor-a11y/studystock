HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — NURSING ASSISTING (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge and entry-level skill of the nursing assistant role in long-term care,
hospital, and home-care settings: resident rights, infection control, safe body
mechanics, measurement of vital signs, assistance with activities of daily
living, and accurate observation/reporting within the nurse aide's legal scope
of practice.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Nurse Aide Role, Scope of Practice & Legal/Ethical Issues (5 items)
1. Define the nursing assistant's role within the care team and chain of command.
2. Differentiate tasks within scope of practice from those requiring a licensed nurse.
3. Explain abuse, neglect, and exploitation categories and mandatory reporting duty.
4. Describe negligence, malpractice, assault, battery, and false imprisonment as they apply to resident care.
5. Explain informed consent and advance directives (living will, DNR, healthcare proxy).

B. Resident Rights & Ethical/Communication Skills (5 items)
1. List rights guaranteed under the Resident's Bill of Rights (privacy, dignity, grievances).
2. Explain confidentiality requirements under HIPAA in a long-term care setting.
3. Describe techniques for communicating with residents who have hearing, vision, or cognitive impairment.
4. Identify communication approaches for residents with dementia or Alzheimer's disease.
5. Explain cultural competence and respecting personal/spiritual preferences in care.

C. Infection Control & Standard/Transmission-Based Precautions (6 items)
1. Explain the chain of infection and how to break it at each link.
2. Demonstrate proper handwashing technique and hand hygiene indications.
3. Identify personal protective equipment (gown, gloves, mask, goggles) and correct donning/doffing order.
4. Differentiate standard precautions from transmission-based precautions (contact, droplet, airborne).
5. Explain principles of medical asepsis versus surgical asepsis.
6. Describe proper handling and disposal of biohazardous/contaminated linens and waste.

D. Safety & Emergency Procedures (5 items)
1. Identify fall-prevention measures and use of gait belts/bed alarms.
2. Explain restraint alternatives and legal restrictions on physical/chemical restraints.
3. Describe the RACE and PASS protocols for fire safety and extinguisher use.
4. Identify signs of choking and steps of the Heimlich maneuver/abdominal thrusts.
5. Explain procedures for reporting incidents (falls, medication errors, injuries).

E. Body Mechanics, Positioning & Transfers (6 items)
1. Apply principles of proper body mechanics to prevent caregiver and resident injury.
2. Perform safe transfer techniques using a gait belt, transfer board, or mechanical lift.
3. Identify standard bed positions (Fowler's, semi-Fowler's, supine, prone, lateral, Sims').
4. Explain proper repositioning technique and turning schedule to prevent pressure injuries.
5. Identify the stages of pressure injuries (Stage I–IV, unstageable, deep tissue injury).
6. Describe use of positioning devices (pillows, bed rails, trochanter rolls).

F. Vital Signs & Measurements (6 items)
1. Identify normal ranges for temperature, pulse, respiration, and blood pressure by age group.
2. Describe correct sites and methods for taking temperature (oral, tympanic, axillary, rectal, temporal).
3. Explain proper technique for counting radial and apical pulse and respirations.
4. Describe correct blood pressure measurement technique and common errors.
5. Explain height and weight measurement procedures and equipment.
6. Identify signs of abnormal vital signs requiring immediate reporting.

G. Personal Care & Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) (6 items)
1. Describe proper technique for a complete bed bath and perineal care.
2. Explain oral hygiene procedures for conscious and unconscious residents.
3. Describe safe dressing/grooming assistance techniques respecting resident independence.
4. Explain proper hair, nail, and skin care procedures.
5. Describe safe shaving procedures and precautions for residents on anticoagulants.
6. Identify principles of promoting resident independence and self-care.

H. Nutrition, Hydration & Elimination (5 items)
1. Identify the basic food groups and components of a therapeutic diet (low-sodium, diabetic, pureed).
2. Explain proper feeding techniques for residents with dysphagia or limited mobility.
3. Describe intake and output (I&O) measurement and recording.
4. Explain signs and symptoms of dehydration and fluid overload.
5. Describe proper use of bedpans, urinals, commodes, and catheter care basics.

I. Restorative Care & Range of Motion (3 items)
1. Identify types of range-of-motion exercises (active, passive, active-assistive).
2. Describe use of adaptive/assistive devices to promote independence (walkers, canes, reachers).
3. Explain the purpose of restorative nursing programs (bowel/bladder training, ambulation).

J. Observation, Documentation & Reporting (3 items)
1. Differentiate objective from subjective observations when reporting resident status.
2. Explain proper charting principles (accuracy, timeliness, legal record requirements).
3. Identify changes in condition that require immediate reporting to the nurse.
