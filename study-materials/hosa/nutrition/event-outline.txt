HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — NUTRITION (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreakers).
Item counts per content area (from the official test-plan percentages) shown in
(parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of human nutrition: macronutrients and micronutrients and their
functions/deficiency diseases, digestion/absorption/metabolism, current
dietary guidelines, nutrition needs across the lifespan, therapeutic and
special diets, nutrition-related disease, and food safety.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Macronutrients — Carbohydrates, Protein & Fat (7 items)
1. Differentiate simple vs. complex carbohydrates and identify food sources of each.
2. Explain the role of dietary fiber (soluble vs. insoluble) and its health effects.
3. Identify essential vs. nonessential amino acids and describe complete vs. incomplete protein sources.
4. Calculate caloric contribution from grams of carbohydrate, protein, and fat (4/4/9 kcal per gram).
5. Differentiate saturated, unsaturated (mono/poly), and trans fats and their cardiovascular effects.
6. Identify essential fatty acids (linoleic, alpha-linolenic/omega-3) and their dietary sources.
7. Explain nitrogen balance (positive vs. negative) and its clinical significance.

B. Vitamins & Deficiency Diseases (7 items)
1. Differentiate fat-soluble (A, D, E, K) from water-soluble (B-complex, C) vitamins by absorption and storage.
2. Identify the deficiency disease associated with each vitamin (e.g., vitamin C/scurvy, vitamin D/rickets, thiamin/beriberi, niacin/pellagra).
3. Identify food sources rich in a specified vitamin.
4. Explain the physiological function of a specified vitamin (e.g., vitamin K in clotting, vitamin A in vision, folate in neural tube development).
5. Identify populations at risk for a specific vitamin deficiency (e.g., folate deficiency in pregnancy, B12 deficiency in vegans/older adults).
6. Identify signs/symptoms of a specified vitamin toxicity (e.g., hypervitaminosis A, vitamin D toxicity).
7. Explain the role of antioxidant vitamins (A, C, E) in reducing oxidative cell damage.

C. Minerals & Deficiency/Toxicity Disorders (6 items)
1. Identify the deficiency disorder associated with a specified mineral (e.g., iron/anemia, iodine/goiter, calcium/osteoporosis).
2. Explain the physiological function of a specified mineral (e.g., sodium/potassium in fluid balance, calcium in bone and muscle contraction).
3. Identify dietary sources rich in a specified mineral.
4. Differentiate major minerals (calcium, phosphorus, magnesium) from trace minerals (iron, zinc, iodine, selenium).
5. Explain factors that enhance or inhibit mineral absorption (e.g., vitamin C enhancing iron absorption, phytates inhibiting zinc absorption).
6. Identify signs/symptoms of a specified mineral toxicity (e.g., iron overload/hemochromatosis, excess sodium and hypertension).

D. Digestion, Absorption & Metabolism (6 items)
1. Sequence the pathway of digestion from ingestion through elimination, identifying each organ's role.
2. Identify the digestive enzyme responsible for breaking down a specified macronutrient (e.g., amylase, lipase, pepsin, trypsin).
3. Explain where each macronutrient is primarily absorbed (e.g., most nutrient absorption in the small intestine/jejunum).
4. Differentiate anabolism from catabolism in metabolism.
5. Explain the role of the liver in nutrient metabolism (glycogen storage, protein synthesis, detoxification).
6. Calculate basal metabolic rate (BMR) factors and explain variables that affect metabolic rate (age, muscle mass, thyroid function).

E. Dietary Guidelines & MyPlate (4 items)
1. Identify the five MyPlate food groups and recommended proportions.
2. Apply Dietary Guidelines for Americans recommendations to a sample meal or diet.
3. Interpret a Nutrition Facts label (serving size, % Daily Value, added sugars).
4. Identify recommended limits for a specified nutrient (e.g., added sugar under 10% of calories, sodium under 2,300 mg/day).

F. Nutrition Across the Lifespan (6 items)
1. Identify increased nutrient needs during pregnancy and lactation (e.g., folate, iron, calcium, additional calories).
2. Identify nutrition considerations for infants (breastfeeding vs. formula, introduction of solids, iron-fortified cereal).
3. Identify nutrition needs and common deficiencies in childhood and adolescence (calcium, iron, growth spurts).
4. Identify nutrition considerations for older adults (decreased caloric need, risk of B12/calcium/vitamin D deficiency, dehydration risk).
5. Identify nutrition considerations for athletes (hydration, carbohydrate loading, protein needs for muscle repair).
6. Explain how pregnancy-related conditions (e.g., gestational diabetes, neural tube defects) relate to specific nutrient intake.

G. Therapeutic & Special Diets (7 items)
1. Identify dietary modifications for a renal diet (restriction of sodium, potassium, phosphorus, protein).
2. Identify dietary modifications for a diabetic/carbohydrate-controlled diet (carbohydrate counting, glycemic index).
3. Identify dietary modifications for a cardiac diet (sodium restriction, saturated fat/cholesterol limits, DASH diet).
4. Identify dietary modifications for gastrointestinal conditions (low-residue, bland, gluten-free for celiac disease).
5. Differentiate diet consistency progressions (clear liquid, full liquid, pureed, mechanical soft, regular).
6. Identify dietary considerations for food allergies vs. food intolerances (e.g., lactose intolerance vs. milk protein allergy).
7. Identify appropriate enteral/parenteral nutrition indications and basic administration considerations.

H. Nutrition-Related Diseases & Disorders (4 items)
1. Identify risk factors and dietary contributors to obesity and metabolic syndrome.
2. Differentiate eating disorders (anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, binge-eating disorder) by clinical signs.
3. Explain the relationship between diet and cardiovascular disease risk (LDL/HDL cholesterol, triglycerides).
4. Explain the relationship between diet and type 2 diabetes risk and management.

I. Food Safety, Labeling & Foodborne Illness (3 items)
1. Identify common foodborne pathogens and their typical food sources (e.g., Salmonella/poultry-eggs, E. coli/undercooked beef, Listeria/unpasteurized dairy).
2. Identify safe food handling practices (temperature danger zone, safe minimum internal cooking temperatures, cross-contamination prevention).
3. Interpret food label claims and terminology (e.g., "reduced sodium," "organic," "whole grain," expiration vs. sell-by dates).
