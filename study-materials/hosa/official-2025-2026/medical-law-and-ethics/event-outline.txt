HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — MEDICAL LAW & ETHICS (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. The test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references below are OFFICIAL (verbatim from the HOSA guideline). Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and to the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (essay tiebreaker).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Legal and ethical principles governing healthcare practice.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Tamparo. Medical Law, Ethics, & Bioethics for the Health Professions. F.A. Davis, latest ed.
- Fremgen. Healthcare Law and Ethics. Pearson, latest ed.
- Ritter & Graham. Medical Law & Professional Ethics. Goodheart-Willcox, latest ed.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. The Basics: Medical Law, Ethics & Bioethics (3 items — 5%)
1. Differentiate law, ethics, and bioethics.
2. Explain sources of law and ethical principles (autonomy, beneficence).

B. The Legal Environment (18 items — 35%)
1. Explain informed consent, standard of care, and professional liability.
2. Describe federal/state regulations, licensure, and the legal system.
3. Explain public duties and mandatory reporting.

C. The Health Care Environment (17 items — 35%)
1. Explain the physician-patient relationship and medical malpractice.
2. Describe medical records, HIPAA, and patient confidentiality.
3. Explain workplace and employment law in healthcare.

D. Bioethical Issues (12 items — 25%)
1. Analyze ethical issues in resource allocation and genetics.
2. Discuss reproductive and end-of-life ethical issues.
