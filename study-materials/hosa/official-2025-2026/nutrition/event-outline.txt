HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — NUTRITION (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. The test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references below are OFFICIAL (verbatim from the HOSA guideline). Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and to the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (tiebreaker per guideline).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Nutrients, digestion and metabolism, nutrition across the lifespan, and medical nutrition therapy.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Roth. Nutrition and Diet Therapy. Cengage, latest ed.
- West. Nutrition and Wellness for Life. Goodheart-Willcox, latest ed.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Carbohydrates, Fats & Proteins (8 items — 15%)
1. Describe the functions, sources, and recommended intake of the macronutrients.
2. Explain how macronutrients are used for energy.

B. Vitamins & Minerals (8 items — 15%)
1. Differentiate fat- vs. water-soluble vitamins and key functions.
2. Describe major minerals, sources, and deficiency/toxicity effects.

C. Water (Fluid & Electrolyte Balance) (5 items — 10%)
1. Explain the role of water and electrolytes in the body.
2. Describe fluid balance and dehydration.

D. Nutrition Through the Life Span (7 items — 15%)
1. Describe nutritional needs from infancy through older adulthood.
2. Explain nutrition needs in pregnancy and lactation.

E. Digestion, Absorption & Metabolism (5 items — 10%)
1. Trace digestion and absorption of nutrients.
2. Explain basal metabolism and energy balance.

F. Cultural Patterns & Food Habits (2 items — 5%)
1. Explain how culture and religion influence food choices.

G. Food-Related Illnesses, Intolerances & Allergies (5 items — 10%)
1. Describe foodborne illness prevention and safe handling.
2. Differentiate food allergy from intolerance.

H. Medical Nutrition Therapy (10 items — 20%)
1. Describe therapeutic diets for common conditions (diabetes, CVD, renal).
2. Explain nutrition assessment and care planning.
