HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — SPORTS MEDICINE (2025–2026)
Source: Official HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026. Test format, content-area TEST PLAN (percentages), and references are OFFICIAL. Item counts = the official percentages applied to the 50-question test. Objectives are representative, aligned to each official content area and the listed references.
Format: 50 multiple-choice questions / 60 minutes (Round 1 written test; highest test score breaks ties). NOTE: Round 2 is a skill component scored by rating sheet — this outline covers the written test.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Prevention, assessment, and care of athletic injuries and illness.

OFFICIAL REFERENCES
- Beam. Orthopedic Taping, Wrapping, Bracing, and Padding. F.A. Davis, latest ed.
- France. Introduction to Sports Medicine and Athletic Training. Cengage, latest ed.
- Prentice. The Role of the Athletic Trainer in Sports Medicine. McGraw Hill, latest ed.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Injury/Illness Prevention & Wellness Promotion (15 items — 30%)
1. Describe injury-prevention strategies (conditioning, protective equipment, taping/bracing).
2. Explain wellness, nutrition, and hydration for athletes.
3. Identify risk factors and pre-participation screening.

B. Examination, Evaluation & Assessment (15 items — 30%)
1. Describe injury evaluation (HOPS/SOAP).
2. Identify anatomical landmarks and assess range of motion.
3. Recognize signs/symptoms of common musculoskeletal injuries.

C. Immediate & Emergency Care (8 items — 16%)
1. Apply emergency action principles and acute injury management (RICE).
2. Recognize and manage medical emergencies (concussion, heat illness).

D. Therapeutic Intervention (8 items — 16%)
1. Describe therapeutic modalities and rehabilitation principles.
2. Explain tissue-healing phases and return-to-play criteria.

E. Healthcare Administration & Professional Responsibilities (4 items — 8%)
1. Explain documentation and legal/ethical responsibilities.
2. Describe the sports-medicine team and scope of practice.
