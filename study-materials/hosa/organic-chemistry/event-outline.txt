HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — ORGANIC CHEMISTRY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory college organic chemistry curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Structure, bonding, nomenclature, and reactivity of carbon-based compounds,
including reaction mechanisms, stereochemistry, and the chemistry of the
functional groups found in biological molecules.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Bonding, Hybridization & Molecular Geometry (5 items)
1. Describe sp3, sp2, and sp hybridization and predict resulting bond angles and geometry.
2. Differentiate sigma and pi bonds and their role in single, double, and triple bonds.
3. Apply VSEPR theory to predict molecular shape of simple organic molecules.
4. Explain resonance structures and their contribution to molecular stability.
5. Describe electronegativity and bond polarity in organic functional groups.

B. Nomenclature of Organic Compounds (IUPAC) (5 items)
1. Apply IUPAC rules to name straight-chain and branched alkanes.
2. Apply IUPAC rules to name alkenes and alkynes, including position and geometry (E/Z, cis/trans).
3. Name organic compounds containing a single functional group (alcohols, halides, amines).
4. Identify common names alongside IUPAC names for frequently encountered compounds.
5. Determine molecular formula and structural formula from a given IUPAC name.

C. Alkanes, Alkenes & Alkynes (4 items)
1. Describe physical properties of alkanes (boiling point trends, solubility) based on structure.
2. Explain conformations of alkanes (staggered, eclipsed) using Newman projections.
3. Describe addition reactions of alkenes (hydrogenation, halogenation, hydration).
4. Apply Markovnikov's rule to predict the major product of an addition reaction.

D. Stereochemistry & Isomerism (5 items)
1. Differentiate constitutional isomers, geometric isomers, and stereoisomers.
2. Identify a chiral (stereogenic) center in an organic molecule.
3. Assign R/S configuration to a stereocenter using CIP priority rules.
4. Differentiate enantiomers, diastereomers, and meso compounds.
5. Explain the biological significance of chirality (e.g., differing drug enantiomer activity).

E. Aromatic Compounds & Benzene Chemistry (4 items)
1. Describe the structure and resonance stabilization of benzene.
2. Apply Hückel's rule to determine aromaticity of a ring system.
3. Explain electrophilic aromatic substitution as a reaction class.
4. Predict directing effects of substituents on further aromatic substitution (ortho/para vs. meta directors).

F. Alkyl Halides & Nucleophilic Substitution/Elimination (5 items)
1. Differentiate SN1 and SN2 mechanisms by rate law, stereochemistry, and substrate structure.
2. Differentiate E1 and E2 elimination mechanisms and predict major product using Zaitsev's rule.
3. Explain the effect of nucleophile strength and solvent on reaction pathway (SN1/SN2/E1/E2).
4. Predict the product of a nucleophilic substitution reaction given a specific alkyl halide and nucleophile.
5. Explain the role of leaving group ability in substitution and elimination reactions.

G. Alcohols, Ethers & Epoxides (4 items)
1. Classify alcohols as primary, secondary, or tertiary.
2. Describe oxidation reactions of alcohols to aldehydes, ketones, and carboxylic acids.
3. Describe the synthesis and reactivity of ethers.
4. Explain ring-opening reactions of epoxides.

H. Aldehydes & Ketones (4 items)
1. Identify the carbonyl functional group and differentiate aldehydes from ketones.
2. Describe nucleophilic addition reactions to the carbonyl group.
3. Explain formation of hemiacetals and acetals from aldehydes/ketones and alcohols.
4. Describe keto-enol tautomerism.

I. Carboxylic Acids & Derivatives (5 items)
1. Describe the structure and acidity of carboxylic acids relative to alcohols.
2. Explain the mechanism of Fischer esterification.
3. Differentiate carboxylic acid derivatives (esters, amides, anhydrides, acid chlorides) by reactivity.
4. Describe hydrolysis reactions of esters and amides.
5. Explain the relative reactivity order of carboxylic acid derivatives toward nucleophilic acyl substitution.

J. Amines & Nitrogen-Containing Compounds (3 items)
1. Classify amines as primary, secondary, or tertiary and describe their basicity.
2. Explain factors affecting amine basicity (resonance, induction, steric effects).
3. Describe amide formation from amines and carboxylic acid derivatives.

K. Spectroscopy & Structure Determination (3 items)
1. Interpret basic infrared (IR) spectra to identify functional groups.
2. Interpret a basic 1H NMR spectrum (chemical shift, splitting pattern, integration) to deduce structure.
3. Use mass spectrometry data (molecular ion, fragmentation) to support structure determination.

L. Biomolecules (Carbohydrates, Lipids, Amino Acids/Proteins) (3 items)
1. Identify functional groups present in monosaccharides and describe glycosidic bond formation.
2. Describe the structure of triglycerides and the reaction forming an ester bond with fatty acids.
3. Describe amino acid structure, the zwitterion form, and peptide bond formation.
