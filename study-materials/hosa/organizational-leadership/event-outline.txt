HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — ORGANIZATIONAL LEADERSHIP (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes.
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of HOSA organizational structure, history, and governing documents;
parliamentary procedure as used in HOSA meetings; and leadership theory and
practice relevant to student officers, including goal setting, communication,
team management, and ethical decision-making in a leadership role.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. HOSA History, Mission & Structure (6 items)
1. Describe the founding history of HOSA and its evolution into HOSA-Future Health Professionals.
2. State the HOSA mission and explain the purpose of the organization.
3. Identify the levels of HOSA structure (chapter, state association, national organization).
4. Identify official HOSA colors, emblem, motto, and creed and their significance.
5. Describe the purpose of the HOSA National Conference and Institutes/competitive events program.
6. Describe the role of the HOSA Alumni and Collegiate divisions within the organization.

B. HOSA Bylaws & Governance (5 items)
1. Explain the purpose and structure of a chapter's bylaws/constitution.
2. Identify the process for amending chapter bylaws.
3. Describe the roles and responsibilities of national and state HOSA officers.
4. Explain the qualifications and election process for chapter officers.
5. Describe the relationship between local chapters, state associations, and the national board of directors.

C. Membership, Divisions & Recognition Programs (4 items)
1. Identify HOSA membership divisions (Secondary, Postsecondary/Collegiate, Alumni, Professional).
2. Describe requirements for active membership and dues structure.
3. Explain the purpose of HOSA recognition/awards programs (e.g., Barbara James Service Award).
4. Explain the purpose of the Healthy Lifestyles and Community Awareness programs.

D. Parliamentary Procedure Basics (7 items)
1. Explain the purpose of parliamentary procedure in conducting orderly meetings.
2. Identify the order of business at a typical chapter meeting.
3. Differentiate a main motion from a subsidiary motion.
4. Explain the process for making, seconding, and voting on a main motion.
5. Identify the quorum requirement and its purpose in conducting business.
6. Differentiate debatable from undebatable motions.
7. Explain the role of the chair/president in recognizing speakers and maintaining order.

E. Motions & Meeting Process (6 items)
1. Identify the correct precedence order among common motions (e.g., table, previous question, postpone, amend).
2. Explain the purpose of the motion to table versus the motion to postpone indefinitely.
3. Explain the purpose and required vote for the motion to amend.
4. Explain the purpose and required vote for the motion for the previous question (close debate).
5. Explain the purpose of a point of order and a point of information.
6. Explain how minutes are recorded and approved at a subsequent meeting.

F. Leadership Theories & Styles (6 items)
1. Differentiate autocratic, democratic, and laissez-faire leadership styles.
2. Explain the concept of situational leadership and adapting style to follower readiness.
3. Differentiate transactional from transformational leadership.
4. Explain servant leadership and its relevance to health-science student organizations.
5. Identify traits and behaviors associated with effective leadership.
6. Explain the difference between leadership and management roles within an organization.

G. Goal Setting & Team Management (5 items)
1. Explain the SMART criteria for setting effective goals.
2. Explain the stages of team development (forming, storming, norming, performing, adjourning).
3. Explain effective delegation principles within a chapter leadership team.
4. Explain the purpose of a program of work/action plan for a chapter year.
5. Explain strategies for motivating chapter members and volunteers.

H. Communication & Conflict Resolution (6 items)
1. Identify components of effective verbal and written communication for a leader.
2. Explain active listening techniques and their role in leadership communication.
3. Differentiate constructive from destructive conflict within a team.
4. Explain steps of a structured conflict-resolution process (e.g., identify issue, seek common ground, agree on solution).
5. Explain the role of nonverbal communication in leadership interactions.
6. Explain strategies for running an effective, inclusive chapter meeting.

I. Ethics & Professionalism in Leadership (5 items)
1. Explain the importance of integrity and accountability in a leadership role.
2. Explain how to handle a conflict of interest as a chapter officer.
3. Explain professional conduct expectations at HOSA conferences and competitive events.
4. Explain the ethical responsibility of a leader in representing the chapter to outside stakeholders.
5. Explain the concept of confidentiality when handling sensitive member or chapter information.
