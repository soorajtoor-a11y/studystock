HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PARLIAMENTARY PROCEDURE (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the team/quiz-bowl round).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of Robert's Rules of Order and standard parliamentary procedure as
applied to conducting an effective, orderly meeting. Covers meeting roles,
the order of business, the classifications and precedence of motions,
voting procedures, and the proper handling of minutes, quorum, and
amendments.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Principles & Purpose of Parliamentary Procedure (5 items)
1. Explain the purpose of parliamentary procedure in protecting the rights
   of the majority and the minority.
2. Identify Robert's Rules of Order as the standard parliamentary authority
   used by HOSA.
3. Explain the principle that only one question may be considered at a
   time.
4. Explain the principle of courtesy and impartiality required of the
   presiding officer.
5. Explain why an organization's bylaws take precedence over Robert's Rules
   of Order when they conflict.

B. Order of Business & Meeting Roles (7 items)
1. Sequence the standard order of business (call to order, reading/approval
   of minutes, officer/committee reports, unfinished business, new
   business, announcements, adjournment).
2. Identify the duties of the president/chair during a meeting.
3. Identify the duties of the secretary in recording proceedings.
4. Identify the duties of the vice president and treasurer.
5. Identify the duties of a parliamentarian in advising the chair.
6. Explain the process for calling a meeting to order and establishing that
   a quorum is present.
7. Explain the proper procedure for a member to obtain the floor before
   speaking.

C. Main Motions (6 items)
1. Define a main motion and identify when it is in order.
2. Sequence the steps for introducing a main motion (obtain the floor,
   make the motion, second, chair states the question, debate, vote,
   chair announces result).
3. Explain the requirement that a main motion must be seconded before
   debate.
4. Explain why a main motion yields to privileged, incidental, and
   subsidiary motions.
5. Identify examples of main motions in a typical chapter meeting.
6. Explain how a main motion may be withdrawn by its maker before a vote.

D. Subsidiary Motions (7 items)
1. Identify the subsidiary motions in their order of precedence (lay on
   the table, previous question, limit/extend debate, postpone
   definitely, refer to committee, amend, postpone indefinitely).
2. Explain the purpose of the motion to amend and identify the types of
   amendments (insert/add, strike out, strike out and insert, substitute).
3. Explain the purpose of the motion to refer to committee.
4. Explain the purpose and effect of the previous question (motion to
   close debate and vote immediately).
5. Explain the purpose of the motion to postpone to a certain time versus
   postpone indefinitely.
6. Explain the purpose of the motion to lay on the table and how it differs
   from postponing.
7. Identify the vote threshold required to adopt each major subsidiary
   motion.

E. Privileged Motions (6 items)
1. Identify the privileged motions in their order of precedence (fix the
   time to which to adjourn, adjourn, recess, raise a question of
   privilege, call for the orders of the day).
2. Explain why privileged motions take precedence over subsidiary and main
   motions.
3. Explain the purpose of a question of privilege.
4. Differentiate a motion to adjourn from a motion to recess.
5. Explain the purpose of the motion to fix the time to which to adjourn.
6. Explain the purpose of calling for the orders of the day.

F. Incidental Motions (6 items)
1. Identify common incidental motions (point of order, appeal, suspend the
   rules, object to consideration, division of the assembly, division of
   the question).
2. Explain the purpose of a point of order and when it may be raised.
3. Explain the process and effect of appealing a decision of the chair.
4. Explain the purpose of the motion to suspend the rules.
5. Explain the purpose of demanding a division of the assembly.
6. Explain the purpose of dividing a question into separate motions.

G. Voting Procedures (6 items)
1. Identify the common methods of voting (voice vote, show of hands, rising
   vote, ballot vote, roll call).
2. Identify the standard vote threshold for adopting a main motion (majority
   vote).
3. Identify motions that require a two-thirds vote and explain why (those
   that limit member rights, such as suspending the rules or closing
   debate).
4. Explain the role of the chair in breaking or not breaking a tie vote.
5. Explain the procedure for a member to change their vote before the
   result is announced.
6. Explain the purpose and procedure of a motion to reconsider a vote.

H. Minutes, Quorum & Amendments (7 items)
1. Identify the required content of meeting minutes (type of meeting, date,
   attendance, motions and their disposition, time of adjournment).
2. Explain what a quorum is and why a meeting cannot conduct binding
   business without one.
3. Explain the procedure for approving minutes at the start of the next
   meeting.
4. Differentiate minutes (a record of actions taken) from a meeting agenda.
5. Explain the process for amending the bylaws of an organization.
6. Identify the notice requirements typically needed to amend bylaws.
7. Explain the difference between amending a motion and amending the
   bylaws.
