HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PATHOPHYSIOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreakers).
Item counts per content area (from the official test-plan percentages) shown in
(parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of disease mechanisms and how the body responds to and compensates
for injury and illness: cellular injury and adaptation, inflammation and
healing, immunity, infection, neoplasia, fluid/electrolyte/acid-base
imbalance, genetic disorders, and common diseases by body system with their
etiology, signs/symptoms, and complications.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Cellular Injury, Adaptation & Death (5 items)
1. Differentiate reversible from irreversible cellular injury.
2. Identify causes of cellular injury (hypoxia, ischemia, toxins, free radicals, physical trauma).
3. Differentiate cellular adaptations (hypertrophy, hyperplasia, atrophy, metaplasia, dysplasia) with an example of each.
4. Differentiate necrosis from apoptosis as mechanisms of cell death.
5. Explain the sequence of events in ischemia-reperfusion injury.

B. Inflammation & Healing (5 items)
1. Identify the cardinal signs of acute inflammation (redness, heat, swelling, pain, loss of function) and their physiologic cause.
2. Differentiate acute from chronic inflammation by cellular mediators and duration.
3. Identify the role of key inflammatory mediators (histamine, prostaglandins, cytokines) in the inflammatory response.
4. Differentiate healing by primary intention vs. secondary intention.
5. Sequence the phases of wound healing (hemostasis, inflammatory, proliferative, remodeling).

C. Immunity, Hypersensitivity & Autoimmune Disorders (5 items)
1. Differentiate innate (nonspecific) from adaptive (specific) immunity.
2. Differentiate humoral (antibody-mediated) from cell-mediated immunity.
3. Classify the four types of hypersensitivity reactions (Type I anaphylactic, Type II cytotoxic, Type III immune complex, Type IV delayed) with an example disease of each.
4. Explain the pathophysiology of a specified autoimmune disorder (e.g., systemic lupus erythematosus, rheumatoid arthritis, type 1 diabetes, multiple sclerosis).
5. Explain the pathophysiology of immunodeficiency (e.g., HIV/AIDS depletion of CD4+ T cells).

D. Infection & Infectious Disease Mechanisms (4 items)
1. Differentiate pathogen types (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites) by structure and mechanism of disease.
2. Explain the chain of infection (reservoir, portal of exit, mode of transmission, portal of entry, susceptible host).
3. Differentiate local from systemic infection, and explain the pathophysiology of sepsis/septic shock.
4. Explain mechanisms of antimicrobial resistance (e.g., MRSA, biofilm formation).

E. Neoplasia & Cancer Biology (4 items)
1. Differentiate benign from malignant neoplasms by growth pattern, differentiation, and metastatic potential.
2. Explain the process of metastasis (local invasion, intravasation, circulation, extravasation).
3. Identify known carcinogenic risk factors (tobacco, UV radiation, HPV, chronic inflammation).
4. Explain the role of oncogenes and tumor suppressor genes (e.g., BRCA1/2, p53) in carcinogenesis.

F. Fluid, Electrolyte & Acid-Base Imbalances (4 items)
1. Differentiate fluid volume deficit (dehydration) from fluid volume excess (edema/overload) by clinical signs and causes.
2. Identify signs/symptoms of a specified electrolyte imbalance (e.g., hyperkalemia, hyponatremia, hypocalcemia).
3. Differentiate respiratory acidosis/alkalosis from metabolic acidosis/alkalosis by underlying cause.
4. Interpret compensatory mechanisms for a given acid-base disturbance (e.g., respiratory compensation for metabolic acidosis).

G. Genetic & Congenital Disorders (3 items)
1. Differentiate inheritance patterns (autosomal dominant, autosomal recessive, X-linked) with an example disorder of each.
2. Explain the pathophysiology of a specified chromosomal disorder (e.g., trisomy 21/Down syndrome).
3. Explain the pathophysiology of a specified congenital structural disorder (e.g., congenital heart defect, neural tube defect).

H. Cardiovascular Pathophysiology (5 items)
1. Explain the pathophysiology of atherosclerosis and its progression to coronary artery disease.
2. Differentiate the pathophysiology of left-sided vs. right-sided heart failure and their resulting signs/symptoms.
3. Explain the pathophysiology of myocardial infarction (ischemia, necrosis, and resulting complications).
4. Explain the pathophysiology of hypertension, including primary vs. secondary causes.
5. Differentiate the pathophysiology of arterial thrombosis/embolism from venous thromboembolism (DVT/PE).

I. Respiratory Pathophysiology (4 items)
1. Differentiate the pathophysiology of obstructive (COPD, asthma) from restrictive lung disease (pulmonary fibrosis).
2. Explain the pathophysiology of pneumonia and its effect on gas exchange.
3. Explain the pathophysiology of pulmonary edema and acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS).
4. Explain the pathophysiology of hypoxia vs. hypoxemia and their common causes.

J. Renal & Urinary Pathophysiology (3 items)
1. Differentiate acute kidney injury from chronic kidney disease by onset, cause, and reversibility.
2. Explain the pathophysiology of glomerulonephritis and its effect on filtration.
3. Explain the pathophysiology of nephrolithiasis (kidney stone formation) and resulting obstruction.

K. Neurological Pathophysiology (3 items)
1. Differentiate the pathophysiology of ischemic stroke from hemorrhagic stroke.
2. Explain the pathophysiology of increased intracranial pressure and its effect on brain perfusion (Cushing's triad).
3. Explain the pathophysiology of a specified neurodegenerative disorder (e.g., Parkinson's disease, Alzheimer's disease).

L. Endocrine & Metabolic Pathophysiology (3 items)
1. Differentiate the pathophysiology of type 1 vs. type 2 diabetes mellitus.
2. Explain the pathophysiology of hyperthyroidism vs. hypothyroidism and their resulting signs/symptoms.
3. Explain the pathophysiology of diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA), including precipitating causes.

M. Musculoskeletal, GI & Integumentary Pathophysiology (2 items)
1. Explain the pathophysiology of osteoarthritis vs. osteoporosis.
2. Explain the pathophysiology of peptic ulcer disease and its major causes (H. pylori infection, NSAID use).
