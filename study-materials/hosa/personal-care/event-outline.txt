HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PERSONAL CARE (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge and entry-level skill of the personal care aide/home health aide role:
assisting clients with personal hygiene, mobility, and daily living needs in the
home or residential setting while maintaining dignity, safety, and clear
communication with clients, families, and the supervising care team.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Role & Scope of the Personal Care Aide (4 items)
1. Define the personal care aide's role and boundaries within the home-care team.
2. Differentiate tasks a personal care aide may perform from those requiring a licensed professional.
3. Explain the chain of communication/reporting to a supervisor, nurse, or agency.
4. Describe professional boundaries and appropriate client relationships.

B. Client Rights, Dignity & Confidentiality (5 items)
1. Explain client rights to privacy, choice, and self-determination.
2. Describe confidentiality requirements under HIPAA in a home-care setting.
3. Explain how to preserve client dignity during personal care tasks.
4. Identify signs of abuse, neglect, or exploitation and mandatory reporting duties.
5. Describe respecting cultural, religious, and personal preferences in daily care.

C. Communication & Interpersonal Skills (5 items)
1. Identify verbal and nonverbal communication techniques with clients.
2. Describe communication strategies for clients with hearing, vision, or cognitive impairment.
3. Explain techniques for communicating with clients who have dementia.
4. Describe appropriate communication with family members and other caregivers.
5. Explain active listening and conflict-resolution techniques in the home setting.

D. Infection Control & Standard Precautions (5 items)
1. Explain proper handwashing technique and when hand hygiene is required.
2. Identify personal protective equipment (gloves, gown, mask) and correct use.
3. Explain standard precautions for handling bodily fluids in the home.
4. Describe proper cleaning/disinfection of surfaces and reusable equipment.
5. Explain proper disposal of soiled linens, dressings, and personal care waste.

E. Home/Environment Safety & Emergency Procedures (5 items)
1. Identify common home safety hazards (throw rugs, poor lighting, clutter, cords).
2. Explain fall-prevention strategies in the home environment.
3. Describe steps to take in a medical emergency, including when to call 911.
4. Identify fire-safety precautions and evacuation procedures in a home setting.
5. Explain proper storage of medications and household chemicals to prevent accidental poisoning.

F. Body Mechanics & Mobility/Transfer Assistance (6 items)
1. Apply principles of proper body mechanics to prevent caregiver and client injury.
2. Perform safe transfer techniques using a gait belt or transfer board.
3. Explain proper technique for assisting with ambulation using a cane, walker, or wheelchair.
4. Describe safe positioning and turning technique to prevent pressure injuries.
5. Identify signs of skin breakdown and when to report them.
6. Explain range-of-motion assistance for bedbound or limited-mobility clients.

G. Personal Hygiene & Grooming Assistance (7 items)
1. Describe proper technique for assisting with bathing (tub, shower, bed bath).
2. Explain oral hygiene assistance procedures, including for denture care.
3. Describe safe hair care, shampooing, and grooming assistance techniques.
4. Explain proper technique for assisting with dressing and undressing.
5. Describe safe nail and skin care procedures, including precautions for diabetic clients.
6. Explain safe shaving assistance and precautions for clients on blood thinners.
7. Identify principles of promoting client independence during hygiene tasks.

H. Nutrition, Hydration & Meal Assistance (5 items)
1. Identify basic food groups and common therapeutic diet modifications.
2. Explain proper technique for assisting a client with eating, including for dysphagia.
3. Describe safe food storage, preparation, and handling in the home.
4. Explain signs and symptoms of dehydration and the importance of fluid intake.
5. Describe documentation of a client's food and fluid intake.

I. Toileting & Elimination Assistance (4 items)
1. Explain proper technique for assisting with toileting, bedpans, and commodes.
2. Describe basic catheter care precautions for a personal care aide.
3. Identify signs of constipation, diarrhea, or urinary tract infection to report.
4. Explain proper perineal care technique and infection-prevention rationale.

J. Documentation & Care Plan Basics (4 items)
1. Explain the purpose of a client care plan and the aide's role in following it.
2. Describe proper documentation of tasks performed and observations made.
3. Differentiate objective observations from subjective statements when reporting.
4. Identify changes in a client's condition that must be reported immediately.
