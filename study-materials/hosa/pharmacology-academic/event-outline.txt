HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PHARMACOLOGY (ACADEMIC) (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory college pharmacology curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Principles governing how drugs move through and act on the body, the major
drug classes organized by body system, and the factors that determine safe
and effective drug therapy, including adverse effects and interactions.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Principles of Pharmacology & Drug Nomenclature (4 items)
1. Differentiate a drug's chemical name, generic name, and brand (trade) name.
2. Define pharmacology, pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, and pharmacotherapeutics.
3. Explain the phases of clinical drug trials and the drug approval process.
4. Describe sources of drugs (synthetic, natural, biotechnology-derived).

B. Pharmacokinetics (Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion) (6 items)
1. Describe factors affecting drug absorption by different routes of administration.
2. Explain drug distribution, including the role of plasma protein binding and the blood-brain barrier.
3. Describe first-pass metabolism and its effect on oral drug bioavailability.
4. Explain the role of cytochrome P450 enzymes in drug metabolism.
5. Describe mechanisms of drug excretion, primarily renal elimination.
6. Calculate and interpret drug half-life and its effect on dosing interval.

C. Pharmacodynamics (Receptors, Dose-Response, Agonists/Antagonists) (5 items)
1. Describe drug-receptor interactions and types of receptors (ion channel, G-protein coupled, enzyme-linked).
2. Differentiate agonists, partial agonists, and antagonists.
3. Interpret a dose-response curve, including potency and efficacy.
4. Explain the concept of therapeutic index and margin of safety.
5. Explain mechanisms of drug tolerance, tachyphylaxis, and dependence.

D. Routes of Administration & Dosage Forms (4 items)
1. Compare enteral routes of administration (oral, sublingual, rectal) and their onset/bioavailability.
2. Compare parenteral routes of administration (IV, IM, subcutaneous, intradermal).
3. Describe topical and transdermal routes and factors affecting absorption.
4. Differentiate common dosage forms (tablet, capsule, suspension, extended-release) and their clinical implications.

E. Autonomic Nervous System Pharmacology (5 items)
1. Differentiate sympathetic and parasympathetic nervous system effects on target organs.
2. Describe the mechanism and effects of cholinergic (parasympathomimetic) drugs.
3. Describe the mechanism and effects of anticholinergic drugs.
4. Describe the mechanism and effects of adrenergic (sympathomimetic) drugs.
5. Describe the mechanism and effects of adrenergic blocking agents (alpha- and beta-blockers).

F. Cardiovascular & Renal Drugs (5 items)
1. Describe the mechanism of action of antihypertensive drug classes (ACE inhibitors, ARBs, calcium channel blockers, diuretics).
2. Describe the mechanism of action of antianginal and antiarrhythmic drugs.
3. Describe the mechanism of action of anticoagulants, antiplatelets, and thrombolytics.
4. Describe the mechanism of action of diuretics and their effect on fluid/electrolyte balance.
5. Describe the mechanism of action of lipid-lowering agents (statins).

G. Central Nervous System Drugs (5 items)
1. Describe the mechanism of action of sedative-hypnotic and anxiolytic drugs (benzodiazepines).
2. Describe the mechanism of action of antidepressant drug classes (SSRIs, SNRIs, tricyclics).
3. Describe the mechanism of action of antipsychotic drugs.
4. Describe the mechanism of action of anticonvulsant drugs.
5. Describe the mechanism of action of opioid analgesics and the concept of opioid receptor activity.

H. Anti-Infective Agents (5 items)
1. Classify antibiotics by mechanism of action (cell wall, protein synthesis, nucleic acid synthesis inhibitors).
2. Differentiate broad-spectrum and narrow-spectrum antibiotics and describe the concept of antibiotic resistance.
3. Describe the mechanism of action of common antiviral drug classes.
4. Describe the mechanism of action of antifungal drugs.
5. Explain principles of appropriate antibiotic use (culture and sensitivity, completing the course of therapy).

I. Endocrine & Reproductive Pharmacology (4 items)
1. Describe the mechanism of action of insulin and oral antidiabetic agents.
2. Describe the mechanism of action of thyroid hormone replacement and antithyroid drugs.
3. Describe the mechanism of action of corticosteroids and their therapeutic uses.
4. Describe the mechanism of action of hormonal contraceptives.

J. Pain Management & Anti-Inflammatory Agents (4 items)
1. Describe the mechanism of action of NSAIDs and the role of COX enzyme inhibition.
2. Compare acetaminophen's mechanism of action and safety profile to NSAIDs.
3. Describe the mechanism of action of opioid versus non-opioid analgesics.
4. Explain the inflammatory pathway targeted by anti-inflammatory drug classes.

K. Adverse Effects, Drug Interactions & Toxicology (3 items)
1. Differentiate types of adverse drug reactions (side effect, allergic reaction, idiosyncratic reaction, toxicity).
2. Describe mechanisms of drug-drug interactions (additive, synergistic, antagonistic).
3. Describe principles of treating drug overdose, including the use of specific antidotes.
