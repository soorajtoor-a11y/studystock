HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PHARMACY SCIENCE (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge and entry-level skill of the pharmacy technician role: prescription
processing, drug classifications and dosage forms, pharmacy calculations,
controlled substance regulations, and safe, legal, and ethical practice in a
retail or institutional pharmacy setting.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Pharmacy Technician Role, Law & Ethics (5 items)
1. Define the pharmacy technician's scope of practice and tasks requiring pharmacist verification.
2. Explain the role of the Board of Pharmacy and state licensing/registration requirements.
3. Describe key provisions of the Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act and Durham-Humphrey Amendment.
4. Explain the difference between OTC, legend (prescription), and controlled medications.
5. Describe ethical responsibilities including honesty in dispensing and reporting errors.

B. HIPAA, Confidentiality & Pharmacy Regulations (4 items)
1. Explain patient confidentiality requirements under HIPAA in a pharmacy setting.
2. Describe requirements for counseling patients under OBRA-90.
3. Explain recordkeeping requirements for prescriptions and controlled substances.
4. Identify requirements for medication error reporting and quality assurance programs.

C. Prescription Components & Terminology (5 items)
1. Identify the required components of a valid prescription (patient info, drug, sig, refills, signature).
2. Differentiate an inscription, subscription, and signatura on a prescription.
3. Explain requirements for verbal, written, and electronic prescriptions.
4. Identify when a prescription requires prior authorization or is non-refillable.
5. Explain the process for transferring a prescription between pharmacies.

D. Medical/Pharmacy Abbreviations & Sig Codes (5 items)
1. Interpret common sig code abbreviations (PO, BID, TID, QID, PRN, HS, AC, PC).
2. Interpret route-of-administration abbreviations (SL, SC/SubQ, IM, IV, PR, OD/OS/OU).
3. Interpret quantity and measurement abbreviations (gtt, mL, mg, mcg, tab, cap).
4. Identify Institute for Safe Medication Practices (ISMP) "do not use" abbreviations.
5. Translate a complete sig (e.g., "1 tab PO BID PRN pain") into patient instructions.

E. Drug Classifications & Common Medications (7 items)
1. Identify major drug classifications by therapeutic use (antibiotic, antihypertensive, analgesic).
2. Identify common brand and generic name pairs for widely prescribed medications.
3. Classify medications by body system affected (cardiovascular, respiratory, endocrine).
4. Describe common indications, general mechanism, and major side effects for major drug classes.
5. Identify common drug interactions and contraindications pharmacists screen for.
6. Differentiate antibiotic classes (penicillins, cephalosporins, macrolides, fluoroquinolones).
7. Identify common vaccines and biologics dispensed or administered through pharmacies.

F. Dosage Forms & Routes of Administration (5 items)
1. Identify solid dosage forms (tablet, capsule, caplet, enteric-coated, extended-release).
2. Identify liquid dosage forms (suspension, solution, elixir, syrup, emulsion).
3. Identify topical and transdermal dosage forms (cream, ointment, patch, gel).
4. Identify parenteral routes and their relative onset of action.
5. Explain special handling for enteric-coated, sublingual, and extended-release forms.

G. Controlled Substances & Scheduling (4 items)
1. Identify DEA controlled substance schedules (I–V) and an example drug from each.
2. Explain refill restrictions for each controlled substance schedule.
3. Describe DEA Form 222 and electronic ordering requirements for Schedule II drugs.
4. Explain proper storage, inventory counts, and diversion-prevention procedures for controlled substances.

H. Pharmacy Calculations (6 items)
1. Calculate days' supply given quantity dispensed and directions for use.
2. Convert between metric, apothecary, and household measurement systems.
3. Calculate dosage based on body weight (mg/kg) or body surface area.
4. Calculate the correct quantity to dispense from a prescription's sig and duration.
5. Calculate dilution and concentration problems (alligation, percent strength).
6. Calculate intravenous flow rates and drip rates.

I. Dispensing, Inventory & Compounding Basics (5 items)
1. Describe the steps in the prescription filling and dispensing workflow.
2. Explain basic non-sterile compounding procedures and equipment (mortar and pestle, graduated cylinder).
3. Identify principles of proper drug storage (temperature, light, expiration dating).
4. Explain inventory management practices (par levels, ordering, recalls, returns).
5. Describe the National Drug Code (NDC) and its use in dispensing and billing.

J. Pharmacy Safety & Error Prevention (4 items)
1. Identify the "rights" of medication administration/dispensing (right patient, drug, dose, route, time).
2. Describe common causes of medication errors (look-alike/sound-alike drugs, transcription errors).
3. Explain proper procedures for handling medication recalls and expired stock.
4. Identify proper use of personal protective equipment when handling hazardous drugs.
