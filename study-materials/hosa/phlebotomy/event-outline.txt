HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PHLEBOTOMY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge and entry-level skill of the phlebotomist role: patient identification
and communication, infection control and safety, venipuncture and capillary
puncture equipment and technique, order of draw and specimen additives, and
proper handling, labeling, and transport of blood specimens for laboratory
testing.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Phlebotomist Role, Scope & Legal/Ethical Issues (4 items)
1. Define the phlebotomist's role within the clinical laboratory and healthcare team.
2. Explain legal issues including consent, negligence, and chain of custody for legal specimens.
3. Describe confidentiality requirements under HIPAA when handling patient specimens.
4. Explain professional conduct and scope-of-practice boundaries for phlebotomists.

B. Infection Control & Safety/OSHA Standards (5 items)
1. Explain the chain of infection and standard precautions in specimen collection.
2. Identify proper hand hygiene and personal protective equipment for venipuncture.
3. Explain OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens Standard requirements for phlebotomists.
4. Describe proper sharps disposal and needlestick injury/exposure protocol.
5. Explain proper disinfection of the collection site and equipment.

C. Patient Identification & Communication (4 items)
1. Explain proper two-identifier patient identification procedure before collection.
2. Describe communication techniques for explaining the venipuncture procedure to a patient.
3. Identify steps to take when a patient identification discrepancy is found.
4. Describe how to handle a patient who refuses or is anxious about blood collection.

D. Venipuncture Equipment & Supplies (6 items)
1. Identify components of the evacuated tube system (holder, needle, tube).
2. Identify needle gauges and lengths appropriate for different patients/procedures.
3. Identify winged infusion (butterfly) sets and their appropriate use.
4. Identify tourniquet use, placement, and time limits.
5. Identify syringe method equipment and when it is preferred over the evacuated tube method.
6. Identify safety-engineered devices required for sharps injury prevention.

E. Venipuncture Site Selection & Technique (6 items)
1. Identify preferred venipuncture sites (median cubital, cephalic, basilic veins).
2. Explain proper technique for vein selection, palpation, and site prep.
3. Identify sites and conditions to avoid (hematoma, mastectomy side, edema, scarring).
4. Explain proper needle insertion angle and technique.
5. Describe correct order of steps for a standard venipuncture procedure.
6. Explain proper technique for bandaging and post-puncture patient instructions.

F. Order of Draw & Tube Additives/Color Codes (6 items)
1. Identify the standard order of draw for evacuated tubes.
2. Identify tube color codes and their corresponding additives (e.g., lavender/EDTA, light blue/citrate).
3. Explain the purpose of anticoagulant versus clot-activator additives.
4. Explain why order of draw prevents additive carryover/cross-contamination.
5. Identify tubes requiring immediate mixing versus those that must not be shaken.
6. Identify special-handling tubes (light-sensitive, chilled, or warmed specimens).

G. Capillary/Dermal Puncture (4 items)
1. Identify indications for capillary puncture versus venipuncture.
2. Identify proper capillary puncture sites for adults, infants, and children.
3. Explain proper lancet technique and depth for capillary puncture.
4. Describe order of draw for capillary specimen collection.

H. Complications & Special Populations (5 items)
1. Identify causes and prevention of hematoma formation during venipuncture.
2. Identify causes and prevention of hemolysis in a blood specimen.
3. Explain precautions for collecting from pediatric, geriatric, and bariatric patients.
4. Describe signs of patient complications during draw (syncope, nerve injury, petechiae).
5. Explain steps to take if a venipuncture attempt fails or a vein collapses.

I. Specimen Handling, Labeling & Transport (5 items)
1. Explain proper specimen labeling requirements at the bedside/point of collection.
2. Describe proper specimen transport conditions (temperature, light protection, timing).
3. Explain criteria for specimen rejection (hemolysis, clotting, mislabeling, insufficient quantity).
4. Describe proper centrifugation procedures and precautions.
5. Explain chain-of-custody documentation for forensic or drug-screening specimens.

J. Quality Assurance & Documentation (5 items)
1. Explain the purpose of quality control and quality assurance in specimen collection.
2. Describe proper documentation of collection time, site, and any complications.
3. Explain procedures for reporting a collection error or incident.
4. Identify requirements for point-of-care testing documentation.
5. Explain proper storage and disposal of expired collection supplies.
