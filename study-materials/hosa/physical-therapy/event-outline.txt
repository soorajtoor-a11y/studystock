HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PHYSICAL THERAPY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge and entry-level skill relevant to the physical therapy aide/assistant
role: musculoskeletal anatomy, range-of-motion and therapeutic exercise,
gait training and assistive devices, therapeutic modalities, and safe patient
handling under the direction of a licensed physical therapist.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. PT Role, Scope of Practice & Healthcare Team (4 items)
1. Differentiate the roles of the physical therapist, physical therapist assistant, and PT aide.
2. Explain the plan-of-care process from referral through evaluation and treatment.
3. Describe supervision requirements and tasks that must be directed by a licensed PT.
4. Explain confidentiality and documentation requirements under HIPAA in a PT setting.

B. Musculoskeletal Anatomy & Terminology (7 items)
1. Identify major bones and bony landmarks relevant to therapy (e.g., greater trochanter, olecranon).
2. Identify major muscle groups and their primary actions (quadriceps, hamstrings, deltoid, rotator cuff).
3. Identify major joints and joint classifications (hinge, ball-and-socket, pivot).
4. Identify ligaments and tendons commonly injured or referenced in therapy (ACL, Achilles tendon).
5. Use anatomical position, directional terms, and body planes correctly.
6. Identify common musculoskeletal terminology (flexion, extension, abduction, adduction, rotation).
7. Differentiate agonist, antagonist, and synergist muscle roles in movement.

C. Range of Motion & Joint Movement Terminology (6 items)
1. Differentiate active, passive, and active-assistive range of motion.
2. Identify normal ranges of motion for major joints.
3. Explain proper use of a goniometer to measure joint angle.
4. Identify factors limiting range of motion (contracture, edema, pain, muscle guarding).
5. Explain manual muscle testing (MMT) grading scale (0–5).
6. Describe end-feel classifications (bony, soft-tissue, capsular).

D. Therapeutic Exercise Types (6 items)
1. Differentiate isometric, isotonic, and isokinetic exercise.
2. Explain the purpose and technique of stretching exercises (static, dynamic, PNF).
3. Describe strengthening exercise progressions using resistance bands and free weights.
4. Explain the purpose of balance and proprioception training.
5. Describe aerobic conditioning exercises used in a rehabilitation plan.
6. Explain core stabilization exercise principles.

E. Gait Training & Assistive Devices (6 items)
1. Identify phases of the normal gait cycle (heel strike, stance, swing, toe-off).
2. Identify common gait abnormalities (antalgic, Trendelenburg, foot drop).
3. Identify assistive devices and their indications (cane, crutches, walker, rolling walker).
4. Explain proper fitting and measurement of crutches and canes.
5. Describe correct gait patterns for assistive devices (three-point, four-point, swing-through).
6. Explain weight-bearing status terminology (NWB, PWB, WBAT, FWB).

F. Therapeutic Modalities (6 items)
1. Explain the physiological effects and indications of cryotherapy (cold packs, ice massage).
2. Explain the physiological effects and indications of thermotherapy (hot packs, paraffin bath).
3. Describe the purpose and use of therapeutic ultrasound.
4. Explain the purpose and use of electrical stimulation (TENS, NMES).
5. Identify contraindications and precautions for common modalities (e.g., no heat over impaired sensation).
6. Describe the purpose of traction and hydrotherapy in treatment plans.

G. Patient Safety, Positioning & Transfers (5 items)
1. Apply proper body mechanics to protect patient and clinician during transfers.
2. Perform safe transfer techniques (bed to chair, chair to standing, using a gait belt).
3. Identify fall-risk factors and fall-prevention strategies during therapy sessions.
4. Explain proper positioning to prevent contractures and pressure injuries.
5. Describe emergency response procedures for a patient who falls or becomes unresponsive during treatment.

H. Common Conditions & Diagnoses (5 items)
1. Identify common orthopedic conditions treated in PT (rotator cuff tear, herniated disc, osteoarthritis).
2. Identify common neurological conditions treated in PT (stroke, spinal cord injury, Parkinson's disease).
3. Explain the general post-surgical rehabilitation process (total knee/hip replacement).
4. Identify signs of overtraining or re-injury during a treatment plan.
5. Explain the difference between acute, subacute, and chronic phases of healing.

I. Documentation & Measurement Tools (3 items)
1. Explain SOAP note format and its use in physical therapy documentation.
2. Describe the purpose of outcome measures and functional assessment tools.
3. Explain proper documentation of patient response to treatment and progress toward goals.

J. Infection Control & Professional Ethics (2 items)
1. Explain standard precautions and equipment cleaning procedures in a PT clinic.
2. Describe ethical responsibilities including informed consent and patient autonomy in therapy.
