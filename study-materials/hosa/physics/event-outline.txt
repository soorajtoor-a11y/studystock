HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PHYSICS (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory college/algebra-based physics curriculum, with health-science
applications noted where relevant.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Fundamental principles of mechanics, fluids, thermodynamics, waves,
electricity and magnetism, and optics, with emphasis on quantitative
problem-solving and applications relevant to the health sciences (circulatory
and respiratory fluid dynamics, diagnostic imaging, sound, and instrumentation).

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Units, Measurement & Vectors (3 items)
1. Apply SI units and metric prefixes to physical quantities.
2. Distinguish scalar and vector quantities and perform basic vector addition.
3. Perform dimensional analysis to check the validity of a physics equation.

B. Kinematics (Motion in One and Two Dimensions) (5 items)
1. Apply the kinematic equations to solve for displacement, velocity, and acceleration under constant acceleration.
2. Interpret position-time and velocity-time graphs.
3. Solve projectile motion problems by separating horizontal and vertical components.
4. Differentiate speed, velocity, and acceleration.
5. Calculate free-fall motion using gravitational acceleration (g = 9.8 m/s^2).

C. Newton's Laws & Dynamics (5 items)
1. Apply Newton's First Law (inertia) to explain the motion of an object with balanced forces.
2. Apply Newton's Second Law (F = ma) to solve for force, mass, or acceleration.
3. Apply Newton's Third Law to identify action-reaction force pairs.
4. Solve problems involving friction (static and kinetic) using the coefficient of friction.
5. Analyze forces on an object on an inclined plane.

D. Work, Energy & Power (5 items)
1. Calculate work done by a constant force using W = Fd cos(theta).
2. Apply the work-energy theorem to relate work and kinetic energy.
3. Calculate gravitational potential energy and elastic potential energy.
4. Apply conservation of mechanical energy to solve motion problems.
5. Calculate power as the rate of work done or energy transferred.

E. Momentum & Collisions (3 items)
1. Calculate linear momentum (p = mv) of an object.
2. Apply conservation of momentum to solve elastic and inelastic collision problems.
3. Calculate impulse and relate it to change in momentum.

F. Rotational Motion & Equilibrium (3 items)
1. Calculate torque produced by a force applied at a distance from a pivot.
2. Apply conditions for static equilibrium (sum of forces and sum of torques equal zero).
3. Describe angular velocity, angular acceleration, and their relationship to linear quantities.

G. Fluids (Pressure, Buoyancy, Flow) (5 items)
1. Calculate pressure as force per unit area and apply it to fluid columns (P = ρgh).
2. Apply Pascal's principle to hydraulic systems.
3. Apply Archimedes' principle to determine buoyant force and predict floating/sinking.
4. Apply the continuity equation to relate fluid velocity and cross-sectional area (e.g., blood flow through vessels of different diameter).
5. Apply Bernoulli's principle to relate fluid pressure and velocity.

H. Thermodynamics & Heat (4 items)
1. Differentiate temperature scales (Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin) and convert between them.
2. Calculate heat transfer using specific heat capacity (Q = mcΔT).
3. Differentiate mechanisms of heat transfer (conduction, convection, radiation).
4. Describe the laws of thermodynamics, including conservation of energy and entropy.

I. Waves & Sound (5 items)
1. Describe wave properties (wavelength, frequency, amplitude, period) and their relationship (v = fλ).
2. Differentiate transverse and longitudinal waves.
3. Describe the Doppler effect and its application to blood flow and diagnostic ultrasound.
4. Calculate sound intensity level in decibels and relate it to hearing safety.
5. Describe resonance and its relevance to acoustic instruments and the human ear.

J. Electricity & Circuits (5 items)
1. Apply Coulomb's law to calculate the force between charged particles.
2. Apply Ohm's law (V = IR) to solve for voltage, current, or resistance in a circuit.
3. Calculate equivalent resistance for resistors in series and in parallel.
4. Calculate electrical power and energy consumption in a circuit.
5. Describe the basic principles of capacitance and the function of a capacitor in a circuit.

K. Magnetism & Electromagnetic Induction (3 items)
1. Describe the relationship between electric current and magnetic fields.
2. Describe the principle of electromagnetic induction (Faraday's law).
3. Explain the basic operating principle of an MRI in terms of magnetic fields and radiofrequency signals.

L. Optics & Light (4 items)
1. Apply the law of reflection and describe image formation by plane and curved mirrors.
2. Apply Snell's law to calculate refraction of light at an interface.
3. Describe image formation by converging and diverging lenses, including the thin-lens equation.
4. Describe the electromagnetic spectrum and the relationship between wavelength, frequency, and energy.
