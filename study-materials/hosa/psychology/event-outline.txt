HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PSYCHOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory college/AP psychology curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Scientific study of behavior and mental processes, covering the major
theoretical perspectives, biological foundations, development across the
lifespan, learning and cognition, personality, psychological disorders, and
the influence of social context on behavior.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. History, Perspectives & Research Methods (5 items)
1. Identify major historical schools of psychology (structuralism, functionalism, psychoanalysis, behaviorism).
2. Differentiate major contemporary perspectives (biological, cognitive, behavioral, humanistic, sociocultural).
3. Differentiate research methods used in psychology (experiment, correlational study, case study, naturalistic observation).
4. Identify independent and dependent variables and explain the purpose of a control group.
5. Explain ethical principles governing psychological research (informed consent, debriefing, IRB review).

B. Biological Bases of Behavior (5 items)
1. Describe the structure and function of the neuron and the process of synaptic transmission.
2. Identify major neurotransmitters and their general behavioral effects (dopamine, serotonin, GABA, acetylcholine).
3. Identify major brain structures and their functions (cerebral cortex lobes, limbic system, brainstem, cerebellum).
4. Differentiate functions of the central and peripheral (somatic/autonomic) nervous systems.
5. Describe the role of the endocrine system in behavior (e.g., cortisol and stress response).

C. Sensation & Perception (4 items)
1. Differentiate sensation and perception.
2. Describe the concept of absolute threshold and difference threshold (just noticeable difference).
3. Explain principles of perceptual organization (Gestalt principles, depth perception).
4. Describe the process of sensory adaptation.

D. States of Consciousness (3 items)
1. Describe the stages of sleep and characteristics of each (REM vs. NREM).
2. Explain theories of the function and purpose of sleep and dreaming.
3. Describe effects of psychoactive drugs on consciousness (depressants, stimulants, hallucinogens).

E. Learning (Classical & Operant Conditioning, Observational Learning) (6 items)
1. Identify components of classical conditioning (unconditioned stimulus/response, conditioned stimulus/response).
2. Differentiate acquisition, extinction, spontaneous recovery, generalization, and discrimination in classical conditioning.
3. Differentiate positive and negative reinforcement and positive and negative punishment in operant conditioning.
4. Differentiate schedules of reinforcement (fixed/variable ratio, fixed/variable interval) and their effect on behavior.
5. Describe observational learning and the role of modeling (Bandura's Bobo doll experiment).
6. Differentiate classical conditioning, operant conditioning, and cognitive/observational learning as distinct processes.

F. Memory & Cognition (5 items)
1. Describe the three stages of memory processing (encoding, storage, retrieval).
2. Differentiate sensory memory, short-term/working memory, and long-term memory.
3. Differentiate explicit (declarative) and implicit (procedural) long-term memory.
4. Describe causes of forgetting (interference, decay, retrieval failure) and memory distortion.
5. Describe problem-solving strategies and cognitive biases (heuristics, availability, confirmation bias).

G. Developmental Psychology (5 items)
1. Describe Piaget's stages of cognitive development.
2. Describe Erikson's stages of psychosocial development.
3. Describe attachment theory and styles of infant attachment (Ainsworth's Strange Situation).
4. Describe Kohlberg's stages of moral development.
5. Describe physical, cognitive, and social changes across adolescence and adulthood.

H. Motivation & Emotion (4 items)
1. Describe major theories of motivation (drive-reduction theory, Maslow's hierarchy of needs).
2. Explain physiological and psychological factors influencing hunger and eating behavior.
3. Describe major theories of emotion (James-Lange, Cannon-Bard, Schachter-Singer two-factor).
4. Explain the physiological components of the stress response (fight-or-flight, general adaptation syndrome).

I. Personality Theories (4 items)
1. Describe Freud's psychoanalytic theory of personality (id, ego, superego; defense mechanisms).
2. Describe the humanistic approach to personality (Rogers, Maslow, self-actualization).
3. Describe the trait approach to personality, including the Big Five personality factors.
4. Differentiate personality assessment methods (projective tests, self-report inventories).

J. Psychological Disorders (DSM Categories) (5 items)
1. Differentiate criteria used to classify abnormal behavior and describe the purpose of the DSM.
2. Identify characteristics of anxiety disorders (generalized anxiety, panic disorder, phobias, OCD).
3. Identify characteristics of mood disorders (major depressive disorder, bipolar disorder).
4. Identify characteristics of schizophrenia spectrum disorders (positive and negative symptoms).
5. Identify characteristics of personality disorders and neurodevelopmental disorders.

K. Therapy & Treatment Approaches (2 items)
1. Differentiate major psychotherapy approaches (psychodynamic, cognitive-behavioral, humanistic).
2. Describe categories of biomedical/pharmacological treatment for psychological disorders.

L. Social Psychology (2 items)
1. Describe principles of conformity, obedience, and group influence (Asch, Milgram studies).
2. Explain attribution theory and common attribution errors (fundamental attribution error).
