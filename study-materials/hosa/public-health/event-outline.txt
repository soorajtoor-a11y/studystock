HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — PUBLIC HEALTH (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the team/performance round).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of core public health principles: essential public health functions,
health promotion and disease prevention, environmental health, vital
statistics, community health assessment and program planning, health policy,
and communication strategies used to improve population health.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Foundations of Public Health (6 items)
1. Define public health and differentiate it from clinical/individual medicine.
2. Identify the three core functions of public health (assessment, policy development, assurance).
3. Identify the Ten Essential Public Health Services.
4. Describe the roles of federal, state, and local public health agencies (CDC, state health departments).
5. Explain the role of the World Health Organization (WHO) in global public health.
6. Describe historical milestones in public health (sanitation reform, vaccination, tobacco control).

B. Health Promotion & Disease Prevention (6 items)
1. Differentiate primary, secondary, and tertiary prevention with concrete public health examples.
2. Explain the purpose and structure of immunization schedules and herd immunity in disease control.
3. Identify major noncommunicable disease risk factors (tobacco use, poor diet, inactivity, alcohol).
4. Explain the health belief model and its use in designing health promotion interventions.
5. Explain the role of workplace and school-based wellness/health promotion programs.
6. Differentiate health promotion from disease prevention activities with examples of each.

C. Environmental Health (5 items)
1. Identify major environmental health hazards (air/water quality, sanitation, vector-borne disease, occupational exposures).
2. Explain the role of environmental health inspections (food safety, water testing) in disease prevention.
3. Explain the public health impact of safe drinking water and wastewater sanitation systems.
4. Identify agencies responsible for environmental health regulation (EPA, OSHA, local health departments).
5. Explain the concept of "One Health" linking human, animal, and environmental health.

D. Epidemiology & Vital Statistics Basics (6 items)
1. Define incidence, prevalence, and mortality/morbidity rates as applied to population health.
2. Explain the purpose of vital records (birth certificates, death certificates) for public health tracking.
3. Interpret life expectancy and infant mortality rate as population health indicators.
4. Explain the purpose of disease surveillance and mandatory disease reporting.
5. Identify leading causes of death and how they inform public health priorities.
6. Explain how demographic data (age, race, socioeconomic status) is used in health disparities analysis.

E. Community Health Assessment & Program Planning (6 items)
1. Explain the steps of a community health needs assessment.
2. Identify components of a logic model for a public health program (inputs, activities, outputs, outcomes).
3. Explain the purpose of setting SMART objectives in public health program planning.
4. Explain the purpose of program evaluation (process, outcome, impact evaluation).
5. Explain how community stakeholders and partnerships are engaged in public health initiatives.
6. Explain the use of Healthy People national objectives in guiding program priorities.

F. Health Policy, Law & Ethics (5 items)
1. Explain the purpose of public health law (quarantine/isolation authority, mandatory reporting).
2. Explain the ethical tension between individual liberty and population health protection.
3. Explain the role of health policy in addressing access to care and insurance coverage.
4. Identify the role of health impact assessments in policy decision-making.
5. Explain informed consent and confidentiality principles as applied in public health practice.

G. Health Education & Communication Strategies (6 items)
1. Explain principles of effective health communication (audience, message, channel, health literacy).
2. Differentiate social marketing campaigns from clinical patient education.
3. Explain the role of media and social media in public health messaging and misinformation control.
4. Explain risk communication principles during a public health emergency.
5. Explain cultural competency considerations in designing health education materials.
6. Explain the purpose of health literacy assessment in tailoring public health messages.

H. Health Equity & Social Determinants of Health (5 items)
1. Define social determinants of health and give examples (income, education, housing, access to care).
2. Differentiate health disparities from health inequities.
3. Explain how socioeconomic status affects access to preventive health services.
4. Explain the concept of health equity as a public health goal.
5. Identify populations at higher risk for health disparities (rural, uninsured, minority populations).

I. Global & Emerging Public Health Issues (5 items)
1. Explain public health preparedness and response planning for pandemics and disasters.
2. Explain the concept of global health security and cross-border disease threats.
3. Explain the public health impact of emerging infectious diseases (novel pathogens, antimicrobial resistance).
4. Explain the role of international travel and trade in disease spread.
5. Explain the public health response framework used during declared public health emergencies.
