HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — SOCIOLOGY (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA / NGL Academic Testing Center event; content follows a standard
introductory college sociology curriculum.
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreaker).
Item counts per topic area are proportional estimates — confirm against the
current event guideline.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Scientific study of human society, social structure, and group behavior,
covering culture and socialization, stratification and inequality, major
social institutions including healthcare, deviance and social control, and
demographic and population concepts relevant to public health.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. The Sociological Perspective & Research Methods (5 items)
1. Define the sociological imagination and differentiate it from individual/psychological explanations of behavior.
2. Differentiate major theoretical perspectives (structural functionalism, conflict theory, symbolic interactionism).
3. Differentiate quantitative and qualitative research methods used in sociology (survey, experiment, ethnography, interview).
4. Differentiate correlation and causation in sociological research findings.
5. Explain ethical considerations in sociological research (informed consent, confidentiality).

B. Culture & Socialization (5 items)
1. Differentiate material and nonmaterial culture and identify components of culture (values, norms, symbols, language).
2. Differentiate folkways, mores, and laws as types of norms.
3. Differentiate cultural relativism and ethnocentrism.
4. Identify agents of socialization (family, peers, school, media, religion) and their role in shaping identity.
5. Differentiate subculture and counterculture.

C. Social Structure, Groups & Organizations (4 items)
1. Differentiate status and role, and explain role conflict and role strain.
2. Differentiate primary and secondary groups and in-groups and out-groups.
3. Describe characteristics of formal organizations and bureaucracy (Weber's ideal type).
4. Describe the concept of social networks and social capital.

D. Social Stratification & Inequality (Class, Race, Gender) (6 items)
1. Differentiate systems of stratification (caste system versus class system).
2. Identify dimensions of social class (wealth, income, education, occupational prestige).
3. Differentiate social mobility types (intergenerational, intragenerational, upward, downward).
4. Explain sociological theories of social inequality (functionalist versus conflict explanations).
5. Differentiate race and ethnicity as sociological (not biological) concepts.
6. Describe the concept of gender as a social construct and explain gender stratification.

E. Social Institutions: Family (4 items)
1. Identify functions of the family as a social institution.
2. Differentiate family structures (nuclear, extended, single-parent, blended).
3. Describe sociological perspectives on marriage, divorce, and changing family patterns.
4. Explain the concept of family as an agent of socialization and social control.

F. Social Institutions: Education (3 items)
1. Identify manifest and latent functions of education.
2. Explain the concept of the hidden curriculum.
3. Describe sociological explanations for educational inequality (tracking, cultural capital).

G. Social Institutions: Religion & Government (3 items)
1. Identify functions of religion as a social institution (Durkheim's perspective).
2. Differentiate types of religious organization (church, sect, cult/new religious movement).
3. Describe functions of government/political institutions in maintaining social order.

H. Social Institutions: Healthcare & Medicine (5 items)
1. Describe the sick role concept (Parsons) and its rights and obligations.
2. Explain the social determinants of health and their effect on health outcomes.
3. Describe medicalization as a sociological concept.
4. Explain sociological perspectives on healthcare access and disparities (race, class, insurance status).
5. Describe the role of the healthcare system as a social institution and the physician-patient relationship.

I. Deviance & Social Control (5 items)
1. Define deviance and explain how it is socially constructed rather than absolute.
2. Describe strain theory as an explanation for deviant behavior.
3. Describe labeling theory and the concept of stigma.
4. Differentiate formal and informal mechanisms of social control.
5. Describe functions of the criminal justice system in responding to deviance.

J. Demography & Population (4 items)
1. Define demography and identify key measures (birth rate, death rate, fertility rate, life expectancy).
2. Explain the stages of the demographic transition model.
3. Describe causes and consequences of population growth and migration.
4. Explain the relevance of demographic data to public health planning.

K. Social Change, Globalization & Collective Behavior (3 items)
1. Identify major sources of social change (technology, social movements, globalization).
2. Differentiate types of collective behavior (crowds, riots, social movements).
3. Describe the concept of globalization and its social and economic effects.

L. Race, Ethnicity & Minority Group Relations (3 items)
1. Differentiate prejudice and discrimination.
2. Describe patterns of intergroup relations (assimilation, pluralism, segregation).
3. Explain the concept of institutional/systemic discrimination.
