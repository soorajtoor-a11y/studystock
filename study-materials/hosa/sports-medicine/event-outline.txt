HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — SPORTS MEDICINE (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge and entry-level skill relevant to the sports medicine/athletic
training field: recognition and management of common athletic injuries,
emergency response and the RICE protocol, taping and protective equipment,
injury prevention and conditioning, and return-to-play decision-making under
the direction of a licensed athletic trainer or physician.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Sports Medicine Team Roles & Scope of Practice (4 items)
1. Differentiate the roles of the athletic trainer, team physician, and student aide.
2. Explain the sports medicine team's chain of command during injury management.
3. Describe legal/ethical issues including consent for minors and confidentiality of injury records.
4. Explain the purpose of a pre-participation physical examination (PPE).

B. Basic Anatomy Relevant to Sports Injuries (7 items)
1. Identify major bones commonly injured in athletics (clavicle, tibia, fibula, metatarsals).
2. Identify major joints and their supporting ligaments (knee: ACL/PCL/MCL/LCL; ankle: ATFL).
3. Identify major muscle groups prone to strain (hamstrings, quadriceps, gastrocnemius, rotator cuff).
4. Identify structures of the knee (menisci, patella, patellar tendon) and shoulder (labrum, rotator cuff).
5. Use anatomical position, directional terms, and planes of movement correctly.
6. Differentiate ligament, tendon, and cartilage tissue and their injury patterns.
7. Identify major nerves at risk in common sports injuries (e.g., peroneal nerve, brachial plexus).

C. Common Athletic Injuries (7 items)
1. Differentiate a sprain from a strain and grade each by severity (Grade I–III).
2. Identify signs and symptoms of a fracture versus a contusion.
3. Identify common overuse injuries (tendinitis, stress fracture, shin splints).
4. Identify signs and symptoms of dislocation and subluxation.
5. Describe common injuries by sport and body region (turf toe, tennis elbow, jumper's knee).
6. Identify signs of a heat-related illness (heat cramps, heat exhaustion, heat stroke).
7. Identify signs and symptoms of exercise-induced anaphylaxis and asthma.

D. Head & Concussion Management (5 items)
1. Identify signs and symptoms of a concussion.
2. Explain the concept of second-impact syndrome and its danger.
3. Describe the general graduated return-to-play protocol following concussion.
4. Explain baseline testing and its role in concussion management.
5. Identify red-flag symptoms requiring emergency transport (loss of consciousness, worsening headache, vomiting).

E. Injury Prevention & Conditioning (5 items)
1. Explain the components of a proper dynamic warm-up and cool-down.
2. Describe principles of flexibility, strength, and conditioning programs for injury prevention.
3. Explain proper hydration and nutrition strategies to prevent exertional illness.
4. Identify environmental risk factors (heat index, WBGT) and modification guidelines for practice.
5. Explain the role of proper technique and equipment fit in preventing injury.

F. Emergency Response & RICE Protocol (6 items)
1. Explain each component of the RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation) protocol.
2. Describe the primary survey/emergency action plan steps for an unresponsive athlete.
3. Explain when and how to activate emergency medical services during an athletic event.
4. Describe spinal immobilization precautions for a suspected spine injury.
5. Explain proper use of an AED in a sudden cardiac arrest emergency.
6. Identify signs requiring immediate physician referral versus sideline management.

G. Taping, Bracing & Protective Equipment (5 items)
1. Explain the purpose and basic technique of ankle taping/wrapping.
2. Identify common braces and their indications (knee brace, ankle brace, wrist splint).
3. Explain proper fitting of protective equipment (helmets, shoulder pads, mouthguards).
4. Describe the purpose of padding and bracing in preventing re-injury.
5. Identify precautions and skin checks needed before and after taping.

H. Rehabilitation & Return-to-Play (5 items)
1. Explain the general phases of injury rehabilitation (acute, recovery, functional/return-to-sport).
2. Describe criteria used to clear an athlete for return to play.
3. Explain the purpose of functional testing before return to competition.
4. Identify psychological factors affecting an athlete's return from injury.
5. Explain the role of the physician and athletic trainer in the return-to-play decision.

I. Nutrition & Hydration for Athletes (3 items)
1. Explain macronutrient needs and timing for athletic performance and recovery.
2. Identify signs and symptoms of dehydration and appropriate rehydration strategies.
3. Explain the risks of disordered eating and the Female/Male Athlete Triad.

J. Legal/Ethical Issues & Documentation (3 items)
1. Explain documentation requirements for injury evaluation and treatment notes.
2. Describe confidentiality requirements for athlete medical information under FERPA/HIPAA.
3. Explain informed consent and parental notification requirements for minor athletes.
