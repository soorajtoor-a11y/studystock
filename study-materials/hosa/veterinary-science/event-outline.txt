HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — VETERINARY SCIENCE (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (Round 1 written-test knowledge domain).
Format: Round 1 Written Knowledge Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (qualifies for the Round 2 skills demonstration).
Item counts per content area are proportional estimates by knowledge-area weight —
confirm against the current guideline, in parentheses.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge and entry-level skill relevant to the veterinary assistant/technician
role: animal anatomy and physiology, species-specific terminology, safe
restraint and handling, preventive care and vaccination schedules, zoonotic
disease awareness, and standard veterinary clinic and hospital procedures.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Veterinary Team Roles & Scope of Practice (4 items)
1. Differentiate the roles of the veterinarian, veterinary technician, and veterinary assistant.
2. Explain tasks within a veterinary assistant's scope of practice versus those requiring a DVM.
3. Describe legal/ethical issues including veterinarian-client-patient relationship (VCPR).
4. Explain confidentiality and recordkeeping requirements for patient/client records.

B. Animal Anatomy & Physiology Basics (7 items)
1. Identify major body systems and their functions across common companion species.
2. Identify anatomical directional terms as applied to quadrupeds (cranial, caudal, dorsal, ventral).
3. Identify major bones and joints relevant to veterinary exams (e.g., stifle, hock, carpus).
4. Describe the basic structure and function of the digestive system in monogastric versus ruminant animals.
5. Identify components of the cardiovascular system and normal heart rate ranges by species.
6. Identify components of the respiratory system and normal respiratory rate ranges by species.
7. Describe the reproductive cycle and terminology for common species (estrus, gestation, parturition).

C. Species-Specific Terminology & Classification (6 items)
1. Identify correct terminology for age/sex classifications (canine, feline, equine, bovine).
2. Classify common companion, livestock, and exotic species by taxonomic group.
3. Identify terminology for gait and movement in equine patients (walk, trot, canter, gallop).
4. Identify common breed-related predispositions to disease (e.g., hip dysplasia in large-breed dogs).
5. Identify dental formulas and aging-by-dentition basics for common species.
6. Identify common veterinary abbreviations (SID, BID, TID, PO, SQ, IM, NPO).

D. Animal Restraint & Handling Techniques (6 items)
1. Explain proper low-stress restraint techniques for dogs and cats.
2. Explain proper restraint techniques for large animals (equine, bovine) including use of a halter/chute.
3. Identify signs of fear, stress, or aggression in an animal and appropriate responses.
4. Describe proper use of restraint equipment (muzzles, cat bags, twitch, squeeze chute).
5. Explain safety precautions to protect both handler and animal during restraint.
6. Describe proper handling technique for exotic and small mammal patients.

E. Vaccination Schedules & Preventive Care (5 items)
1. Identify core versus non-core vaccines for dogs and cats.
2. Explain the standard vaccination schedule for puppies and kittens.
3. Identify common parasite prevention protocols (heartworm, flea/tick, deworming).
4. Explain the purpose and legal requirement of rabies vaccination.
5. Describe routine wellness exam components (weight, temperature, dental check, body condition score).

F. Zoonotic Diseases & Public Health (4 items)
1. Identify common zoonotic diseases (rabies, ringworm, toxoplasmosis, leptospirosis).
2. Explain transmission routes and prevention measures for zoonotic diseases.
3. Describe proper hygiene practices to reduce zoonotic disease risk in a clinic setting.
4. Explain reporting requirements for suspected rabies exposure.

G. Common Diseases & Parasites (6 items)
1. Identify signs and symptoms of common infectious diseases (parvovirus, distemper, feline leukemia).
2. Identify common internal parasites (heartworm, roundworm, hookworm, tapeworm) and diagnostic methods.
3. Identify common external parasites (fleas, ticks, mites) and associated conditions.
4. Describe common metabolic and endocrine conditions (diabetes mellitus, hypothyroidism) in companion animals.
5. Explain basic principles of wound care and bandaging in veterinary patients.
6. Identify signs of common emergencies (bloat/GDV, toxin ingestion, dystocia).

H. Veterinary Office/Clinic Procedures (5 items)
1. Explain proper procedure for taking a patient history and triaging a case.
2. Describe proper technique for collecting and labeling laboratory samples (blood, urine, fecal).
3. Explain standard sterilization and disinfection procedures for surgical instruments.
4. Describe proper inventory and controlled substance recordkeeping in a veterinary clinic.
5. Explain client communication and discharge instruction procedures.

I. Nutrition & Husbandry (4 items)
1. Identify basic nutritional requirements and life-stage feeding for common companion animals.
2. Explain body condition scoring and its use in assessing nutritional status.
3. Describe proper housing, bedding, and environmental enrichment needs by species.
4. Explain safe handling and storage of animal feed and prevention of foodborne illness.

J. Veterinary Law, Ethics & Documentation (3 items)
1. Explain licensing and regulatory requirements governing veterinary practice.
2. Describe ethical considerations in euthanasia and end-of-life care decisions.
3. Explain proper documentation of patient records, treatment plans, and informed consent forms.
