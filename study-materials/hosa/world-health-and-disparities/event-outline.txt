HOSA STUDY OUTLINE — WORLD HEALTH & DISPARITIES (2025–2026)
Source: HOSA Competitive Event Guidelines 2025-2026 (official test plan).
Format: Objective Test — 50 multiple-choice questions, 60 minutes (+ tiebreakers).
Item counts per content area (from the official test-plan percentages) shown in
(parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Knowledge of global health issues and health disparities: social determinants
of health, health equity and access to care, global disease burden and
epidemiologic trends, disparities by race/income/geography, global health
organizations and initiatives, comparative healthcare systems, cultural
competence, and ethical issues in global health.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Social Determinants of Health (6 items)
1. Identify the categories of social determinants of health (economic stability, education access, healthcare access, neighborhood/environment, social/community context).
2. Explain how income and poverty level affect health outcomes.
3. Explain how educational attainment correlates with health literacy and outcomes.
4. Explain how housing conditions and neighborhood environment affect health (e.g., food deserts, lead exposure, air quality).
5. Explain how employment status and occupational conditions affect health outcomes.
6. Explain how social support and community context affect health and chronic disease management.

B. Health Equity, Equality & Access to Care (6 items)
1. Differentiate health equity from health equality with an applied example.
2. Identify barriers to healthcare access (cost, insurance status, transportation, provider shortages, language).
3. Explain the concept of a medically underserved area/population and a health professional shortage area.
4. Identify the effect of health insurance coverage status on preventive care utilization and outcomes.
5. Explain telehealth's role in improving access to care in rural or underserved areas.
6. Identify policy approaches used to expand healthcare access (e.g., community health centers, Medicaid expansion).

C. Global Disease Burden & Epidemiology (7 items)
1. Differentiate communicable (infectious) from noncommunicable (chronic) disease and identify the global burden trend of each.
2. Identify leading global causes of death and disability (e.g., cardiovascular disease, respiratory infections, malaria, HIV/AIDS).
3. Explain the epidemiologic transition (shift from infectious to chronic disease burden as nations develop).
4. Interpret basic epidemiologic measures (incidence vs. prevalence, morbidity vs. mortality rate).
5. Identify factors contributing to the global re-emergence of infectious diseases (vaccine hesitancy, antimicrobial resistance, urbanization).
6. Explain the concept of a pandemic vs. epidemic vs. endemic disease with a historical example of each.
7. Identify the global burden and risk factors of a specified noncommunicable disease (e.g., diabetes, cancer, chronic respiratory disease).

D. Health Disparities by Race, Income & Geography (6 items)
1. Identify a documented health disparity affecting a specific racial or ethnic population (e.g., maternal mortality disparities, hypertension prevalence).
2. Explain how rural vs. urban geography affects healthcare access and outcomes.
3. Explain how income inequality between and within nations correlates with life expectancy and infant mortality.
4. Identify disparities in infant and maternal mortality rates between high-income and low-income countries.
5. Explain the concept of health disparities driven by structural/systemic factors versus individual behavior.
6. Identify disparities in a specified disease's prevalence or outcome linked to socioeconomic status (e.g., diabetes, obesity, asthma).

E. Global Health Organizations & Initiatives (6 items)
1. Identify the role and structure of the World Health Organization (WHO) in global health governance.
2. Identify the role of major global health initiatives/organizations (UNICEF, the Global Fund, Gavi the Vaccine Alliance, PEPFAR).
3. Explain the purpose of the International Health Regulations in coordinating outbreak response.
4. Identify the role of nongovernmental organizations (NGOs) in global health delivery (e.g., Doctors Without Borders/Médecins Sans Frontières).
5. Identify a Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) related to health and its target.
6. Explain the role of the CDC's global health work versus WHO's global mandate.

F. Healthcare Systems Worldwide — Comparative Models (5 items)
1. Differentiate a single-payer national health system from a multi-payer/private insurance-based system.
2. Identify characteristics of a specified country's healthcare system model (e.g., Beveridge, Bismarck, national health insurance, out-of-pocket models).
3. Compare healthcare spending and outcome metrics (life expectancy, infant mortality) across high-income countries.
4. Explain the concept of universal health coverage and countries' progress toward it.
5. Identify strengths and limitations of a specified healthcare financing model.

G. Cultural Competence in Global Healthcare Delivery (5 items)
1. Define cultural competence and cultural humility in a healthcare delivery context.
2. Identify the role of health beliefs and traditional practices in patient care decisions across cultures.
3. Identify strategies for overcoming language barriers in patient care (e.g., professional medical interpreters).
4. Identify potential consequences of a culturally insensitive healthcare interaction.
5. Explain the importance of understanding religious/cultural practices related to diet, modesty, and end-of-life care.

H. Maternal, Child & Environmental Global Health Issues (5 items)
1. Identify leading global causes of maternal mortality (hemorrhage, hypertensive disorders, sepsis, unsafe abortion).
2. Identify leading global causes of under-5 child mortality (pneumonia, diarrheal disease, malaria, neonatal complications).
3. Explain the role of vaccination programs in reducing global childhood mortality.
4. Identify the impact of unsafe water and sanitation on global disease burden (e.g., cholera, waterborne parasitic disease).
5. Explain the health impacts of climate change and environmental degradation on global populations (heat-related illness, vector-borne disease spread, food insecurity).

I. Ethics & Policy in Global Health (4 items)
1. Identify ethical considerations in global health research and aid (informed consent, exploitation of vulnerable populations).
2. Explain the concept of health as a human right and relevant international declarations.
3. Identify ethical tradeoffs in resource allocation during a global health crisis (triage, vaccine distribution equity).
4. Explain the concept of "brain drain" of health workers from low-income to high-income countries and its effect on health systems.
