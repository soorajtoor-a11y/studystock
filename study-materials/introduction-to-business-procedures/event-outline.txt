FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — INTRODUCTION TO BUSINESS PROCEDURES (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Eligibility: 9th & 10th graders only.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Basic office procedures and workplace practices: administrative tasks,
business protocols, and decision-making in a professional environment.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Human Relations
1. Identify appropriate work habits and ethics, including dress.
2. Identify, evaluate, and select training resources.
3. Develop/explain work processes and procedures (organizing, prioritizing).
4. Coordinate staff work schedules and workload distribution.
5. Contribute to job descriptions for staff.
6. Discuss and analyze an employee performance evaluation.
7. Maintain employee records.
8. Update policy and procedures manuals.
9. Conduct new employee orientation and training.
10. Create and maintain productive work relationships.
11. Work in a team to solve problems and share knowledge.
12. Exhibit behaviors that motivate and lead people.

B. Technology Concepts
1. Conduct needs assessments of hardware, software, equipment, supplies.
2. Evaluate and recommend hardware, vendors, warranties, purchasing options.
3. Remove, upgrade, store, install hardware and software.
4. Navigate operating systems and internet applications.
5. Manage files and folders.
6. Use help resources (help desks, online help, manuals).
7. Select and apply productivity software for tasks.
8. Identify and select software for organizational functions/industry.
9. Select and apply multimedia software.

C. Communication Skills
1. Compose, give, and follow oral and written instructions.
2. Identify good listening skills.
3. Interpret verbal and nonverbal cues.
4. Locate/maintain telephone numbers and addresses.
5. Identify techniques for answering, screening, placing calls (incl. conference calls).
6. Identify techniques for holds, transfers, messages.
7. Evaluate emerging communications technologies.
8. Process electronic communications (fax, email, file transmissions).
9. Prepare and deliver oral presentations.
10. Receive/greet visitors and clients; make introductions.
11. Locate, organize, reference information from various sources.
12. Communicate to foster positive relations and provide feedback.
13. Compose business documents (agendas, reports, correspondence).
14. Develop and interpret tables, charts, figures.

D. Decision Making/Management
1. Identify leadership styles and effective leader characteristics.
2. Identify the functions of management.
3. List responsibilities at different management levels.
4. Interpret an organizational chart.
5. Set priorities; develop efficient workflow procedures.
6. Develop efficient office teams; resolve conflicts.
7. Examine potential business problems; offer solutions and contingency plans.
8. Acquire, analyze, organize, synthesize information for decisions.
9. Identify, write, and monitor workplace performance goals.
10. Manage quality-control processes.

E. Career Development
1. Explore business careers through various sources.
2. Develop a career plan.
3. Prepare application letter, resume, employment application, follow-up letter.
4. Identify appropriate/inappropriate job interview behaviors.
5. Identify steps for resigning from a position.
6. Develop and maintain a portfolio and professional documents.
7. Identify employment barriers for nontraditional groups and remedies.
8. Utilize career-advancement activities.

F. Business Operations
1. Identify efficient workstation organization; recommend improvements.
2. Choose best reprographics methods for tasks.
3. Process incoming and outgoing mail, including email.
4. Coordinate special mail and courier services.
5. Arrange travel (reservations, itinerary).
6. Demonstrate time management skills.
7. Coordinate meetings, events, and office activities.
8. Use Personal Information Management applications.
9. Establish procedures to maintain workstation, equipment, supplies.
10. Troubleshoot office equipment problems.
11. Maintain office equipment (toner, paper, cartridges).
12. Manage preventive maintenance and repair.
13. Implement purchasing processes for supplies, equipment, services.
14. Identify types of business ownership.

G. Database/Information Management
1. Establish and maintain document storage and retrieval systems.
2. Prepare and maintain inventory records.
3. Maintain alphabetical, subject, numerical, chronological filing systems.
4. Maintain tickler file systems.
5. Maintain reference libraries, clippings, historical records.
6. Purge records and/or files.
7. Convert and save data using scanning equipment.

H. Ethics/Safety
1. Identify office accident causes; establish safety/security measures.
2. Adhere to privacy, safety, and security policies and legislation.
3. Implement policies for security, privacy, risk management.
4. Demonstrate knowledge of emergency/disaster plans.
5. Discuss responsible technology use and consequences of misuse.
6. Identify office confidentiality concepts and policies.
7. Identify professional conduct characteristics (integrity, loyalty, honesty, courtesy).
8. Analyze ethical issues in the office.
9. Examine ergonomics and its importance.
10. Interpret software license agreements and legal mandates (ADA, Sarbanes-Oxley).

I. Finance
1. Prepare banking transactions (deposit slips, reconciliation).
2. Complete payroll documents manually and electronically.
3. Apply calculator/numeric keypad to business problems.
4. Develop budgets for office/events; manage expenses.
5. Complete purchase requisitions and payment vouchers.
6. Implement expense-control strategies.
7. Manage business records.
8. Record cash fund transactions (e.g., petty cash).
9. Know general finance terms and conditions.

J. Information Processing
1. Utilize software to revise, edit, save, output documents.
2. Transcribe business correspondence.
3. Dictate notes and correspondence.
4. Create and format tables, charts, graphs.
5. Perform word processing functions (merge, sort, search/replace, macros, templates).
6. Convert documents between systems/programs.
7. Create, edit, enhance spreadsheets.
8. Create and edit a database.
9. Extract information using search queries; generate reports.
10. Integrate database, spreadsheet, graphics, word processing files.
11. Complete preprinted and electronic forms.
12. Scan documents, data, graphics.
13. Proofread and edit documents.
14. Develop presentations/visual aids with text and graphics.
15. Use office and online references.
16. Employ collaborative/groupware applications.
17. Reproduce and distribute documents.
