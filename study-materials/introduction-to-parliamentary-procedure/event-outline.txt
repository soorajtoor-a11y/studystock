FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — INTRODUCTION TO PARLIAMENTARY PROCEDURE (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Eligibility: 9th & 10th graders only.
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Basic principles and rules for conducting orderly and effective
meetings: motions, voting procedures, and meeting structure based on
parliamentary law.

TEST COMPOSITION
1. Parliamentary Procedure Principles — 90 questions
2. FBLA Bylaws (Corporate & Division) — 10 questions

KEY STUDY TOPICS (per Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised —
the official handbook for this event)
- Types of motions: main, subsidiary, privileged, incidental
- Order of precedence of motions
- Which motions are debatable, amendable, require a second
- Voting requirements (majority, two-thirds) per motion type
- Meeting structure and order of business
- Quorum, adjournment, recess
- Roles of chair, secretary, members
- Minutes and reports
- Nominations and elections
- Committees and their functions

STUDY SOURCES (official, per FBLA guidelines)
- Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised (official handbook)
- National Association of Parliamentarians website
- FBLA Bylaws at www.fbla.org (for the 10 bylaws questions)
