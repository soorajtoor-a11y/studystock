FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — RETAIL MANAGEMENT (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
Test-item counts per knowledge area shown in (parentheses).
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Core retail operations and strategies: inventory control, sales
techniques, merchandising, and product mix.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Foundational Retail Knowledge (10 test items)
1. Distinguish between retailing and marketing.
2. Explain the nature and scope of distribution.
3. Analyze the impact of technology on retailing.
4. Obtain information from retail databases for product planning/control.

B. Selling (20 test items)
1. Demonstrate a good/service.
2. Convert customer objections into selling points.
3. Close the sale.
4. Demonstrate suggestion selling.
5. Process retail sales documentation.
6. Provide incoming merchandise information to sales staff.
7. Monitor on-floor selling activities.

C. Pricing (10 test items)
1. Select approach for setting base price (cost, demand, competition).
2. Determine cost of product (breakeven, ROI, markup).
3. Calculate break-even point.
4. Set prices.

D. Market Planning (10 test items)
1. Profile target customer.
2. Determine market needs.
3. Determine customer demand for merchandise.

E. Warehousing and Transportation (5 test items)
1. Explain shipping processes.
2. Identify factors in selecting the best shipping method.
3. Plan storage space.

F. Inventory Control (15 test items)
1. Allocate merchandise to stores/regions.
2. Track stock by location at department/class/vendor level.
3. Explain types of unit inventory-control systems.
4. Determine inventory shrinkage.
5. Maintain inventory-control systems.

G. Visual Merchandising (10 test items)
1. Place merchandise for impact.
2. Determine on-floor assortments.
3. Use cross-merchandising techniques.
4. Read/implement planograms.

H. Display Techniques (10 test items)
1. Create promotional signs.
2. Select and use display fixtures/forms.
3. Create displays.

I. Product Mix (10 test items)
1. Determine quality of merchandise to offer.
2. Determine width and depth of assortment strategies.
3. Select mix of brands.
4. Plan merchandise assortment (styling, sizes, quantities, colors).
