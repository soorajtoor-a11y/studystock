FBLA STUDY OUTLINE — SECURITIES & INVESTMENTS (2025–2026)
Source: Official FBLA Competitive Events Guidelines (updated Nov 2025)
Format: Objective Test — 100 multiple-choice questions, 50 minutes
============================================================

EVENT DESCRIPTION
Investment principles and financial markets: stocks, bonds, mutual
funds, portfolio management, and risk analysis.

KNOWLEDGE AREAS AND OBJECTIVES

A. Investment Fundamentals
1. Define terminology common to securities & investments.
2. Demonstrate knowledge of savings.
3. Identify stages of investing and the risk-return relationship.
4. Identify sources of financial information for investment decisions.

B. Investment Wrappers, Taxation, and Trusts
1. Describe individual savings accounts.
2. Demonstrate knowledge of pensions.
3. Demonstrate knowledge of taxation.
4. Describe and identify investment bonds.

C. Investment Products & Funds
1. Identify and explain different types of loans.
2. Explain the purposes of mortgages.
3. Demonstrate knowledge and purpose of life assurance.
4. Describe and explain unit trusts.
5. Describe open-ended investment companies.
6. Identify characteristics of pricing, dealing, and settlement.
7. Identify and explain investment trusts.
8. Demonstrate knowledge of how hedge funds work.

D. Stock Market
1. Explain how the stock market benefits investors and companies.
2. Analyze stock tables for investment information.
3. Describe options for handling accumulated money/earnings.
4. Understand the types of investments.
5. Compare primary vs. secondary markets.
6. Distinguish bull, bear, and pig markets.

E. Stocks Versus Other Investments
1. Demonstrate knowledge of compounding.
2. Explain the purpose of a company.
3. Describe ethical behavior.
4. Understand gathering relevant information.
5. Evaluate financial statements.
6. Define terminology common to stock markets and other investments.

F. Mutual Funds
1. Understand and explain total return.
2. Describe mutual funds and their tax implications.
3. Identify the process of purchasing mutual funds.
4. Explain benefits of investing in mutual funds.
5. Understand fund costs.

G. Basics of Bonds
1. Demonstrate knowledge of bond duration.
2. Describe the process of buying bonds.
3. Describe the process of issuing bonds.
4. Identify the roles of collateral.
5. Compare secured and unsecured bonds.
6. Understand government bonds.
7. Identify U.S. government agency bonds and savings bonds.

H. Derivatives
1. Demonstrate knowledge of futures.
2. Demonstrate knowledge of options.
3. Demonstrate knowledge of swaps.
4. Describe the different uses of derivatives.

I. Financial Services Regulation
1. Explain the Financial Services & Markets Act.
2. Identify financial crime.
3. Explain insider dealing and market abuse.
4. Describe the Data Protection Act 1998.
5. Identify types of breaches, complaints, and compensation.
